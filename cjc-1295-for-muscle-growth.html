What Is CJC-1295?

CJC-1295 (CJC-1295 (Modified GRF 1-29)) is a Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog. Synthetic peptide analog of GHRH (first 29 amino acids). Also known as CJC-1295, Mod GRF 1-29, CJC-1295 no DAC, it has been studied extensively for its potential effects on increased growth hormone secretion, improved body composition, better sleep quality, enhanced recovery, anti-aging effects.

In the research community, CJC-1295 has gained significant attention due to its favorable safety profile and broad range of potential applications. It is one of the most widely studied peptides in its class.

How Does CJC-1295 Work?

CJC-1295 stimulates the pituitary gland to release growth hormone in a pulsatile manner by binding to GHRH receptors. The 'no DAC' version has a shorter half-life, resulting in more natural GH pulses. It amplifies the body's own GH release rather than replacing it.

Understanding these mechanisms helps researchers design more effective protocols and predict potential outcomes. The multi-pathway activity of CJC-1295 is one reason it has attracted so much research interest.

What Does the Research Say About CJC-1295?

Clinical research demonstrates significant increases in GH and IGF-1 levels. Studies show improved body composition, increased lean mass, and reduced body fat when used with GHRP peptides.

It is important to note that while preclinical results are encouraging, the peptide research field is still evolving. Researchers should consult the latest peer-reviewed literature for the most current findings on CJC-1295.

What Is the Recommended CJC-1295 Dosage?

Based on research protocols, common CJC-1295 dosages range from 100-300 mcg, administered 1-3 times daily, typically before bed via subcutaneous injection.

The half-life of CJC-1295 is ~30 minutes (no DAC), ~8 days (with DAC), which influences dosing frequency. Typical research cycles last 8-12 weeks, often paired with Ipamorelin.
