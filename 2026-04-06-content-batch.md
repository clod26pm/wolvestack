RFK Jr. announced on February 27, 2026 that ~14 of 19 Category 2 peptides (including BPC-157) would be moved back to Category 1, restoring legal compounding access. However, as of April 6, the FDA has still not formally published the updated list. Reddit threads on r/peptides and r/biohackers are buzzing with frustration and speculation about the delay. This creates a content window: people are searching for clarity on what's legal, what's coming back, and what the timeline looks like.

**Content angle:** "Here's exactly where the FDA reclassification stands today" — positions us as the go-to source for timely updates.

### Trend #2: First Human IV BPC-157 Safety Study Published

A pilot study (Lee & Burgess, 2025, *Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine*) tested intravenous BPC-157 at 10mg and 20mg doses in two healthy adults. No adverse effects were reported across heart, liver, kidney, thyroid, or blood glucose biomarkers. While the sample size is tiny (n=2), this is the first published human IV safety data for BPC-157 — a major talking point for the community that has relied on animal studies for years.

**Content angle:** "The first human BPC-157 IV study just dropped — here's what it actually says" — nuanced breakdown that builds authority.

### Trend #3: Mastercard BRAM Update Squeezing Peptide Vendors

Mastercard's 2026 BRAM compliance update (GLB 11691.1) has expanded enforcement against merchants selling research peptides. Mainstream processors (Stripe, PayPal, Square) are actively terminating peptide merchant accounts and freezing funds. This is reshaping the supply landscape and driving vendors toward specialized high-risk processors. For consumers, this means potential disruption in sourcing.

**Content angle:** "Why your favorite peptide vendor might disappear overnight" — news angle that drives urgency and positions our affiliate partners (who have stable processing) as reliable options.
