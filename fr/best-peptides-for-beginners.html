Recommendation 1: BPC-157 for Recovery and Gut Health

BPC-157 is the most universally appropriate starting peptide for most people. The breadth of its applications (tendon/ligament healing, gut health, neuroprotection, systemic anti-inflammation) means almost everyone has a relevant target. Its safety profile is exceptional in animal studies with no documented serious adverse events in extensive community use. The administration is straightforward — subcutaneous injection or oral solution — and the dose range is forgiving.

If you have any ongoing soft tissue injury, digestive issues, or simply want the most evidence-supported recovery peptide available, BPC-157 is the best starting point. A 5 mg vial at 250 mcg/day provides 20 days of dosing — a reasonable initial trial period to assess response.

Recommendation 2: Ipamorelin/CJC-1295 for Sleep, Recovery, and Body Composition

The Ipamorelin/CJC-1295 stack is the most appropriate entry point for GH optimisation — cleaner than the older GHRPs, well-understood mechanism, and broad positive community experience. For anyone whose primary goals involve improving sleep quality, accelerating recovery between training sessions, or gradual body recomposition, the pre-sleep injection protocol is a practical and evidence-supported approach.

Start with 100 mcg of each peptide pre-sleep and assess response over 2 weeks before escalating. Monitor sleep quality, morning readiness, and progressive changes in energy and body composition. The effects accumulate over weeks rather than appearing dramatically on day one — patience and consistent dosing are required.

Recommendation 3: Semax or Selank for Cognitive and Stress Goals

For beginners with primarily cognitive or anxiety goals rather than physical recovery objectives, Semax (for cognitive enhancement) or Selank (for anxiety reduction) are the cleanest entry points. Both are intranasal — no needles required. Both have clinical safety data from Russian regulatory approval. Both produce relatively quick subjective feedback (within hours to days) that helps beginners assess response and calibrate dosing.

Start with 100–200 mcg of Semax or 250 mcg of Selank intranasally on a single morning, note the response over 4–6 hours, and adjust from there. The intranasal route is more forgiving in terms of dose precision than injection, making these ideal for peptide beginners who are apprehensive about self-injection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest peptide to start with?

Semax or Selank — intranasal, no reconstitution process, no needles, clear subjective feedback to calibrate dosing. For injection-based peptides, BPC-157 oral (dissolved in water) removes the injection barrier entirely. If comfortable with injections, BPC-157 subcutaneous is the most widely recommended starting injection protocol due to its excellent safety profile and broad applications.

Do I need to inject peptides or can I take them orally?

Semax and Selank are intranasal (nasal spray/drops) — no injections. BPC-157 can be taken orally for gut-specific goals. MK-677 is a genuine oral GH secretagogue. For repair peptides targeting tendons, ligaments, or systemic healing, injection is more effective but not strictly required. Most community beginners start with BPC-157 oral or Semax/Selank to assess how they respond before committing to injection protocols.

How much does a beginner peptide protocol cost?

A basic starter protocol is very accessible. BPC-157 (5 mg vial) costs $30–60 from reputable vendors — sufficient for a 20-day trial at 250 mcg/day. Semax and Selank nasal sprays run $30–60 for a 10 mg supply. A beginner trying BPC-157 for one month can expect to spend $60–120 total including bacteriostatic water and insulin syringes. This is dramatically less expensive than most prescription regenerative medicine options.

How do I know if the peptides are working?

For BPC-157 injury use: reduced pain, improved range of motion, faster return to normal function within 2–4 weeks. For sleep protocols (Ipamorelin/CJC): noticeably deeper sleep, more vivid dreams, better morning readiness within 1–2 weeks. For Semax: sharper mental focus and motivation within hours. For Selank: reduced anxiety and improved social ease within 30–60 minutes. If no response is apparent after 4 weeks at appropriate doses, consider peptide quality (reorder from a different vendor) before assuming the compound does not work for you.

What mistakes do beginners most commonly make?

The three most common beginner mistakes: (1) Starting too many peptides simultaneously, making it impossible to identify what is working. Start with one compound, run it alone for 3–4 weeks, then add others. (2) Poor storage — leaving reconstituted peptides at room temperature or exposing lyophilised powder to heat and humidity. (3) Using underdosed or degraded peptides from low-quality vendors and concluding that the compound does not work. Vendor quality varies enormously; third-party HPLC testing is the only reliable quality indicator.
