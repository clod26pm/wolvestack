Hey —

Quick correction to something I keep seeing in my feed.

The headline making the rounds is that 12 peptides — BPC-157, GHK-Cu, MOTS-c, Epitalon, Melanotan II, and 8 others — are now "FDA approved" or "legal again" as of April 23rd, 2026. That's not what happened.

Here's what did happen, in plain English.

The FDA maintains two lists for substances that compounding pharmacies might want to prepare. Category 1 is a holding bay — substances under review. Category 2 is the "significant safety risk" list, which functionally bans compounding. In 2023, those 12 peptides got dropped onto Category 2 essentially overnight, which is why your local clinic suddenly couldn't fill a BPC-157 prescription.

On April 23rd, the FDA removed them from Category 2. That's a real change. But removal from Category 2 does not automatically put them on the 503A bulk drug substances list — which is the list that actually authorizes licensed compounding pharmacies to use them.

So right now, those 12 peptides exist in a regulatory gray zone. The Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee — PCAC — meets on July 23rd and 24th, 2026, to decide whether to officially place them on the 503A list. That's the meeting that matters.
