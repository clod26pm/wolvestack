How to Verify a CoA and Assess Quality

What to Look For in a CoA:

  • Test method: Should state "HPLC" (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) or "LC-MS/MS" (mass spectrometry). Both are reliable.
  • Purity percentage: Should read ≥98%. 95–97% is acceptable but indicates lower quality. Below 95% is unacceptable.
  • Batch number: CoA should reference the specific batch you're purchasing, not a generic "representative batch."
  • Testing date: Recent (within 3–6 months) is standard. Very old CoAs (1+ year) may not reflect current product.
  • Lab name: Should be a real, accredited analytical lab. Avoid generic lab names or vendor-conducted in-house testing (bias).

Red Flag CoAs:

  • Purity stated as "≥98%" without specific number (vague).
  • No testing method mentioned (unknown methodology).
  • Generic "sample testing" without batch reference.
  • CoA from vendor's own lab (potential for bias).
  • Expired CoA (testing date >1 year ago).
