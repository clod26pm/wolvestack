How to Store DSIP

Temperature, Shelf Life & Stability

📖 5 min read 🔬 Research & Education ⚠️ Not medical advice ✅ Last reviewed: April 2026

Medical Disclaimer

For informational and educational purposes only. Not FDA-approved for human use. Consult a licensed healthcare professional. See full disclaimer.

DSIP powder is stable 12-24 months at room temperature or longer refrigerated (2-8°C). Reconstituted DSIP solution lasts 30 days at room temperature or 90 days refrigerated in bacteriostatic water. Keep away from heat, light, and moisture. Always store in sealed vials with clear labeling.

How Long Is DSIP Powder Stable?

Lyophilized (freeze-dried) DSIP powder is remarkably stable, maintaining potency for 12-24 months at room temperature (68-77°F) when stored in sealed vials. Longer stability is possible with refrigeration: DSIP powder stored at 2-8°C (35-46°F) remains stable for 24-36 months or longer. Stability is reduced in high humidity, direct sunlight, or elevated temperatures. Unopened DSIP vials from reputable suppliers typically carry a 18-24 month shelf-life guarantee.

How Should I Store DSIP Powder?

Store DSIP powder in sealed vials in a cool, dry, dark location. An airtight container in a refrigerator (standard kitchen refrigerator at 2-8°C) is ideal for long-term storage. Avoid freezing, as ice crystal formation can damage peptide structure. Keep away from direct sunlight, as UV light degrades peptides. Store DSIP away from heat sources, moisture, and volatile chemicals. Label vials with the acquisition date so you can track age and plan reconstitution before expiration.

What Happens If DSIP Powder Gets Wet?

Exposure to moisture dramatically reduces DSIP powder shelf life. If a DSIP vial is exposed to water or high humidity, the powder begins to degrade within days. Moisture promotes bacterial growth and chemical decomposition. Always keep DSIP powder vials sealed and in a desiccating environment. If you suspect moisture exposure, discard the vial. Never attempt to dry a wet DSIP vial, as this will not recover the degraded peptide.

How Long Does Reconstituted DSIP Stay Good?

Reconstituted DSIP in bacteriostatic water is stable for 30 days at room temperature (68-77°F) and up to 90 days refrigerated at 2-8°C (35-46°F). The benzyl alcohol preservative in bacteriostatic water inhibits bacterial growth and extends stability. After 30-90 days, DSIP concentration decreases and contamination risk increases, even with proper storage. Always label reconstituted DSIP vials with the reconstitution date and discard after expiration.

Should I Keep Reconstituted DSIP in the Fridge or at Room Temperature?

Both are acceptable. Room temperature storage is convenient for daily injection protocols; refrigerated storage extends shelf life to 90 days (vs. 30 days at room temperature). Choose based on your protocol: if you'll use the entire solution within 30 days, room temperature storage is fine. If reconstitution exceeds 30 days or you want maximum stability, refrigerate. Never freeze reconstituted DSIP, as this damages the peptide structure.

How Do I Know If DSIP Has Gone Bad?

Visual inspection is the best indicator. Fresh DSIP solution should be clear to colorless. If the solution becomes cloudy, discolored (yellowing or browning), or contains visible particles, the peptide has degraded and should be discarded. Smell is not a reliable indicator for peptides, but obvious odor changes suggest contamination. If you have any doubt about solution quality, discard and reconstitute fresh. Using degraded DSIP reduces efficacy and increases contamination risk.

Can Heat or Sunlight Damage DSIP?

Yes. Heat and UV light (sunlight) degrade DSIP peptide structure. Exposure to temperatures above 77°F (25°C) for extended periods reduces potency. Direct sunlight or fluorescent light exposure degrades DSIP rapidly. Always store DSIP in opaque, sealed containers away from light and heat. Never leave DSIP vials in a hot car, near heat sources, or in direct sunlight. If DSIP powder has been exposed to heat or sunlight, potency may be significantly reduced.

What's the Best Long-Term Storage Setup for DSIP?

Optimal long-term storage: DSIP powder in sealed, opaque vials stored in a refrigerator (2-8°C) inside an airtight container with desiccant (silica gel packets). This setup minimizes exposure to light, heat, humidity, and oxidation. Label clearly with acquisition date. For home use, a dedicated peptide storage box in the refrigerator works well. For larger quantities or extended storage (2+ years), consider medical-grade freezer storage (-20°C or below), though this is typically unnecessary for home quantities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I freeze DSIP powder?
A: Not recommended. Freezing (below 0°C) doesn't improve stability and can damage peptide structure via ice crystal formation.

Q: How do I know if DSIP powder has expired?
A: Check visual appearance and smell. Powder should be white/off-white and odorless. Any discoloration or odor suggests degradation.

Q: What if I left DSIP at room temperature for a month?
A: If sealed and at room temperature, powder stability is minimally affected over 1 month. Long-term (6+ months) room temp storage reduces potency.

Q: Can I store DSIP in a regular kitchen fridge?
A: Yes. Standard refrigerator (2-8°C) is perfect for DSIP storage. Keep in sealed vial away from moisture.

Q: How long can reconstituted DSIP be stored if I don't use it daily?
A: 30 days at room temp, 90 days refrigerated. After that, discard.

Q: What if reconstituted DSIP gets freezer-burned?
A: Freezing reconstituted solution damages the peptide. If frozen and thawed, potency is likely compromised. Discard.
