Frequently Asked Questions

What is Kisspeptin?

Kisspeptin (Kisspeptin-10/Kisspeptin-54) is a Neuroendocrine peptide, GPR54 receptor agonist. Endogenous neuropeptide encoded by KISS1 gene; master regulator of the reproductive hormone axis. It is researched for reproductive hormone stimulation, fertility enhancement, egg maturation induction, ovulation triggering, testicular function support.

What is the recommended Kisspeptin dosage?

Common dosages: 100-200 mcg subcutaneous administered daily or pulsatile dosing via subcutaneous injection, intranasal (experimental). Cycle length: single dosing for IVF protocols; multi-week protocols undefined. Half-life: very short for Kisspeptin-10; longer for Kisspeptin-54. What are the side effects of Kisspeptin?

Minimal in early human studies. Potential headaches, flushing, or autonomic symptoms. Theoretical concerns for hormonal imbalance with chronic use.

Is Kisspeptin safe?

Kisspeptin has shown a preliminary safety profile in research. Not FDA-approved. Investigational use only. All research should follow appropriate safety protocols.
