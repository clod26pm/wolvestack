## Trend Report

### Trend #1: FDA Approves Higher-Dose Semaglutide (Wegovy HD 7.2 mg) — Major GLP-1 News

On March 19, 2026, the FDA approved semaglutide 7.2 mg (Wegovy HD) for adults with obesity who need additional weight reduction beyond the standard 2.4 mg dose. The phase 3b STEP UP trial showed approximately 21% mean weight loss at 72 weeks. This is significant for the peptide/biohacker community because it validates the dose-response relationship that researchers have been studying, and it opens the door to discussions about optimizing GLP-1 protocols. Meanwhile, Eli Lilly's retatrutide (a triple-agonist) posted phase 3 results showing up to 16.8% weight loss — the next-gen competition is heating up.

**Content angle:** "The GLP-1 arms race just escalated" — compare the new higher-dose semaglutide data with retatrutide's triple-agonist approach. Educational explainer on how dose escalation works and why researchers are studying whether more is better.

### Trend #2: Chinese Factory-Direct Peptide Sourcing Exposed — Safety Red Flag

A major investigative report (WBUR, March 24, 2026) exposed how unregulated peptides are being purchased directly from Chinese factories through Reddit communities and Discord servers. Factory representatives send "menus" of dozens of peptides to buyers with zero FDA oversight, unknown purity, and contamination risks. Reported side effects include gallstones, nerve pain, and kidney stones. This is generating massive discussion across r/peptides and r/biohackers about sourcing safety and quality verification.

**Content angle:** "How to verify peptide purity" — educational content on third-party testing (COAs, HPLC, mass spectrometry), why sourcing matters, and what red flags to watch for. Positions our affiliate partners (who provide COAs) as the responsible alternative without directly attacking competitors.

### Trend #3: RFK Jr.'s Peptide Reclassification — 14 Peptides Returning to Legal Compounding

The biggest ongoing story: HHS Secretary RFK Jr. announced on February 27 that 14 of 19 peptides on the FDA's Category 2 restricted list are being moved back to Category 1, restoring legal access through licensed compounding pharmacies with physician prescriptions. BPC-157, GHK-Cu, CJC-1295, Sermorelin, and others are affected. The formal FDA updated list is still pending, creating a window of uncertainty. Reddit is buzzing about what this means for access, pricing, and whether grey market sourcing will decline.

**Content angle:** "What the reclassification actually means for you" — break down the difference between Category 1 and Category 2, which peptides are affected, and the practical timeline. Emphasize that a prescription from a licensed provider is still required.
