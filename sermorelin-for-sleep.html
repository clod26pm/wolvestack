Can Sermorelin Help With Sleep?

Sermorelin (Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone analog (GRF 1-29)) is being researched for sleep applications based on its mechanism as a GHRH analog, growth hormone secretagogue.

Binds GHRH receptors on anterior pituitary somatotrophs, activating cAMP-PKA signaling to stimulate GH synthesis and pulsatile secretion. Preserves natural GH feedback mechanisms — unlike exogenous GH injection, Sermorelin lets the body regulate its own GH levels through normal negative feedback.

What Does the Research Show for Sermorelin and Sleep?

FDA-approved November 1997 for pediatric GH deficiency (Geref). Manufacturer discontinued in 2008 for commercial reasons — FDA confirmed NOT withdrawn for safety concerns. Now prescribed off-label through compounding pharmacies.

The relevance to sleep specifically comes from Sermorelin's effects on endogenous GH stimulation, lean mass gain, fat loss, IGF-1 elevation, improved recovery, bone density.

What Protocol Is Used for Sleep?

For sleep applications, the standard Sermorelin protocol is 200-500 mcg daily administered once daily before bedtime via subcutaneous injection for 12-24 weeks; adjust after 4-6 weeks based on IGF-1 levels.

What Side Effects Apply to Sleep Use?
