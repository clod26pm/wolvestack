## Trend Report

### Trend 1: RFK Jr. Peptide Reclassification — Still Pending, Hype Building

On February 27, HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. announced on Joe Rogan's podcast that approximately 14 of 19 peptides on the FDA's Category 2 restricted list would be reclassified back to Category 1, restoring legal compounding pharmacy access. This includes BPC-157, TB-500, CJC-1295, GHK-Cu, and others. As of today, the formal FDA rule change remains in the administrative pipeline and has not been officially published. Meanwhile, Peptide Sciences — the largest gray-market research peptide vendor in the U.S. — voluntarily shut down on March 6, signaling a major industry shift. Reddit communities are buzzing with speculation about timelines, and this creates a massive content window: people want to understand what reclassification means, what changes, and what doesn't.

**Content angle:** "What the peptide reclassification actually means (and what it doesn't)" — clarify that Category 1 is NOT FDA approval, peptides still require physician prescriptions, and quality/sourcing still matters.

### Trend 2: Gray-Market Peptides Go Mainstream on TikTok — Media Backlash Begins

Over 250,000 TikTok videos are now tagged #peptide, and mainstream outlets (NPR, TIME, WBUR) published exposés this month warning about unregulated injectable peptides sourced from Chinese factories flooding social media. Influencers promote affiliate codes for research peptide vendors while framing injections as "optimization tools." Health authorities are raising alarm bells about teenagers injecting unapproved compounds with unknown purity profiles. This backlash creates a trust-building opportunity: position our content as the responsible, research-first voice in a sea of hype.

**Content angle:** "How to evaluate peptide quality and sourcing" — educational content that builds authority and differentiates from reckless influencer content.

### Trend 3: Oral Semaglutide Launch + GLP-1 Enforcement Wave

The FDA approved oral Wegovy (semaglutide 25mg), with Novo Nordisk's full U.S. launch underway. The OASIS 4 trial showed 16.6% mean weight loss at 64 weeks with full adherence. Simultaneously, the FDA issued warning letters to 30 telehealth companies for misleading claims about compounded GLP-1s. Roughly 80% of compounded semaglutide now contains added ingredients (B vitamins, etc.) to circumvent restrictions. This creates a two-sided content opportunity: oral semaglutide as a needle-free option, and the risks of compounded alternatives.

**Content angle:** "Oral vs. injectable semaglutide — what researchers are finding" and "Why the FDA is cracking down on compounded GLP-1s."
