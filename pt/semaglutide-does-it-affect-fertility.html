Does Semaglutide Affect Fertility?

Evidence on Female and Male Fertility, Pregnancy Safety, and Reproductive Outcomes

📖 8 min read 🔬 Research & Education ⚠️ Not medical advice ✅ Last reviewed: April 2026

Medical Disclaimer

For informational and educational purposes only. Semaglutide is a prescription GLP-1 agonist approved for diabetes (Ozempic) and weight loss (Wegovy). Consult a licensed healthcare professional. See full disclaimer.

Semaglutide has not been directly shown to impair fertility in humans, but pregnancy is category C (contraindicated in third trimester). Weight loss itself can improve ovulation and fertility. Limited data exists on male reproductive function. Users planning pregnancy should discuss discontinuation timing and metabolic recovery with providers. No evidence of birth defects, but minimal human pregnancy data exists.

Does Semaglutide Affect Female Fertility?

Direct evidence of semaglutide impairing fertility is absent. However, rapid weight loss can temporarily suppress ovulation through reduced caloric availability and altered hormone levels. Conversely, weight loss in overweight/obese women often improves fertility by restoring ovulation. The net effect depends on baseline BMI and rate of weight loss.

How Does Weight Loss from Semaglutide Affect Female Hormone Levels?

Rapid weight loss decreases estrogen and increases SHBG (sex hormone-binding globulin), potentially disrupting menstrual cycles. This is reversible: weight stabilization restores hormone balance within 1-3 months. In obese women with PCOS, weight loss improves insulin sensitivity and restores ovulation.

What Does FDA Guidance Say About Semaglutide and Pregnancy?

Semaglutide is FDA category C: animal studies show adverse effects, but no human data exists. Manufacturers recommend discontinuing before conception or immediately upon pregnancy detection. No human teratogenicity has been documented, but the lack of safety data necessitates caution.

Can Semaglutide Be Used While Trying to Conceive?

Use during conception attempts requires careful discussion with providers. Some recommend discontinuing before attempting pregnancy (3-6 month washout). Others accept continued use in women stabilized on therapy. Weight loss benefits for overweight women may outweigh theoretical risks. Individual risk assessment is essential.

Is Semaglutide Safe During Pregnancy?

No. Manufacturers and major guidelines recommend discontinuation immediately upon pregnancy detection. Animal studies at high doses show fetal toxicity and birth defects. While human data is minimal (few pregnant users have reported), the precautionary principle applies—discontinue if pregnant.

Does Semaglutide Affect Male Fertility or Sperm Production?

No direct evidence of semaglutide impairing male fertility exists. GLP-1 receptors are absent in testes. Weight loss may improve male fertility by increasing testosterone in overweight men. No data on sperm parameters, motility, or counts in semaglutide users.

What About Semaglutide and Breastfeeding?

Safety during breastfeeding is unknown. The drug likely enters breast milk in small quantities, but metabolism in infant GI tract is unknown. Conservative recommendation: discontinue semaglutide before conception or immediately postpartum. Discuss alternatives (dietary management, other weight loss approaches) with providers.

Should I Stop Semaglutide Before Trying to Conceive?

Recommendations vary by provider. Conservative approach: discontinue 3-6 months before attempting pregnancy, allow weight stabilization and metabolic recovery. Aggressive approach: continue semaglutide through conception attempts if weight loss benefits outweigh theoretical risk. Shared decision-making with providers is essential.

How Long After Stopping Semaglutide Can I Conceive Safely?

Semaglutide's half-life is 7-10 days; clearance is essentially complete within 4-6 weeks. However, metabolic recovery (normalization of appetite, stabilization of weight, restoration of menstrual cycle regularity) takes 2-3 months. Most providers recommend waiting 3 months post-discontinuation before attempting conception.

What If I Become Pregnant While Taking Semaglutide?

Stop immediately and contact your healthcare provider. Notify your OB/GYN of semaglutide exposure. Continue prenatal vitamins and standard pregnancy care. No emergency measures needed—brief exposure during conception or early pregnancy is unlikely to cause harm, but continued exposure through first trimester warrants discussion with providers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can semaglutide cause miscarriage if used early in pregnancy?

No direct evidence exists. Animal studies at high doses show fetal toxicity, but brief early-pregnancy exposure is unlikely to cause miscarriage based on available data. Still, discontinue immediately upon pregnancy detection.

Does weight loss from semaglutide improve fertility chances?

Yes, in overweight/obese women. 5-10% weight loss often restores ovulation and improves fertility. The fertility benefit may outweigh theoretical medication risks, especially for PCOS patients.

Will my menstrual cycle return after stopping semaglutide?

Yes. Menstruation normalizes within 1-3 months as weight stabilizes and metabolic hormones recover. If amenorrhea persists >3 months post-discontinuation, investigate other causes.

Is semaglutide safe for men trying to conceive?

Likely yes, though data is absent. No mechanism of impaired male fertility exists. Weight loss in overweight men may improve testosterone and fertility.

Should I use contraception if using semaglutide?

Semaglutide doesn't reduce contraceptive efficacy. However, weight loss may alter oral contraceptive absorption. Discuss with your healthcare provider if switching to barrier or long-acting contraception during rapid weight loss.

What if I was exposed to semaglutide during conception or early pregnancy?

Notify your OB/GYN. Brief exposure is unlikely to cause harm. Continue prenatal care and standard monitoring. Emergency interventions are not needed, but healthcare providers should be informed.
