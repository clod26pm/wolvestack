## 🔍 Trend Report

### Finding #1: RFK Jr.'s FDA Peptide Reclassification Is Reshaping the Market

On February 27, 2026, HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. announced a major FDA peptide reclassification restoring legal compounding pharmacy access to approximately 14 of the 19 previously restricted peptides — including BPC-157, TB-500, Thymosin Alpha-1, CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, and AOD-9604. These compounds are moving from Category 2 back to Category 1 status, enabling licensed compounding pharmacies to prepare them under physician prescription. Kennedy acknowledged the previous restrictions "created the gray market." This does NOT mean FDA approval — it restores compounding access only. This is the single biggest regulatory shift in the peptide space in years and is generating massive community discussion.

Sources: NPR (March 26, 2026), AgeMD, EliteNP

### Finding #2: FDA Drops 30 Warning Letters on GLP-1 Compounders

On March 3, 2026, the FDA issued 30 warning letters to telehealth companies marketing compounded GLP-1 products with misleading claims suggesting equivalence to FDA-approved drugs. Meanwhile, Novo Nordisk and Hims & Hers announced a major partnership (March 9) where Hims will now offer branded Wegovy/Ozempic while ceasing compounded GLP-1 advertising. The compounded semaglutide market is being squeezed from both regulatory and commercial sides simultaneously.

Sources: FiercePharma, Venable LLP, FDA.gov
