Kisspeptin Cycle Guide

Length, Dosing & PCT Protocol

What Is a Kisspeptin Cycle?

A kisspeptin cycle refers to a structured treatment period using pulsatile kisspeptin administration to restore or optimize reproductive function. Unlike exogenous hormones (testosterone, growth hormone) which suppress pituitary output, kisspeptin reactivates the body's natural reproductive axis through GPR54 receptor agonism. Cycles typically last 4-6 weeks for ongoing reproductive optimization, or can be single-dose IVF trigger protocols. The fundamental advantage: kisspeptin creates no hormonal suppression, so discontinuation allows immediate GnRH axis recovery without needing suppression-reversal periods.

Kisspeptin cycling is physiologically aligned with natural reproductive patterns—it enhances and restores rather than replaces. This makes cycling protocols more natural and sustainable long-term compared to hormone replacement therapies that require careful management of suppression and recovery.

Understanding Pulsatile Dosing Necessity

Kisspeptin's extremely short half-life (3-4 minutes in circulation) mandates pulsatile administration every 90-120 minutes to maintain physiologic hormone patterns. This rapid clearance mirrors natural kisspeptin neuron firing patterns—bursts of activity separated by quiet periods. Continuous kisspeptin exposure (without pulsatile breaks) causes receptor desensitization; GnRH neurons stop responding after 24-48 hours of constant stimulation, collapsing both LH and FSH response.

Pulsatile patterns preserve receptor sensitivity indefinitely through intermittent stimulation. This is why all successful kisspeptin protocols use repeated bolus injections (discrete doses every 90-120 minutes) rather than continuous infusions. The "off-periods" between pulses are essential for maintained efficacy.

Optimal Cycle Duration Frameworks

Reproductive optimization cycles: 4-6 weeks of consistent pulsatile dosing restores menstrual cycles in women, optimizes sperm production in men, and improves hormone balance in both sexes. Most users see meaningful hormone improvements by week 2-3, with maximum benefit by week 4-6. IVF trigger protocols: single kisspeptin dose 34-36 hours before egg retrieval, replacing or complementing hCG as final oocyte maturation trigger. Male fertility restoration: 6-8 weeks minimum to complete spermatogenesis cycle; testosterone improvements appear within 2-3 weeks but sperm parameter optimization requires 8+ weeks. Menstrual restoration: 4-6 weeks to re-establish ovulatory cycles from hypothalamic amenorrhea or other disruption.

Cycle duration depends on baseline reproductive status (how suppressed the axis is) and treatment goals. More severe suppression may require 8-12 weeks; mild dysfunction may recover in 3-4 weeks.

Pulsatile vs Continuous Administration Comparison

Pulsatile (recommended): Bolus doses every 90-120 minutes maintain receptor sensitivity, sustain robust GnRH and LH response for months, produce physiologic hormone patterns, work indefinitely without tolerance. This is the clinically proven approach. Continuous infusion: Causes rapid receptor downregulation; LH response collapses after 24-48 hours; unsuitable for long-term reproductive optimization; only used clinically for acute research purposes. Home-based protocols should never attempt continuous infusion—pulsatile injection protocols are the only practical, effective approach.

The choice is simple for real-world use: pulsatile administration (repeated injections) is the only method that works beyond 48 hours. This dictates that successful kisspeptin users accept frequent injections (typically 6-8 per day across a 12-hour waking window).

Dosing Protocol Structure Within Cycles

Standard pulsatile protocol: 1-3 mcg/kg subcutaneous kisspeptin every 90-120 minutes during waking hours. For a 70 kg person: 70-210 mcg per injection. IVF trigger dose: 6.4 nmol/kg single injection (~3-5 mcg/kg equivalent), timed 34-36 hours before egg retrieval. Beginner protocol: Start 1 mcg/kg every 2 hours, progress to 90-120 minute spacing after 3-5 days. Advanced optimization: Titrate dose upward based on LH response, typically reaching 2-5 mcg/kg per dose at therapeutic levels. Most cycles use 6-8 injections daily across a 12-hour window (e.g., 8am through 8pm).

Consistent timing matters: set regular injection times (every 2 hours during daytime) to maintain steady GnRH neuron stimulation. Missed doses can be compensated with the next scheduled dose, but consistency maximizes results.

Hormone Monitoring Throughout Cycles

Track LH response 30 minutes post-injection: levels should double to decuple baseline within 30 minutes of kisspeptin administration, validating successful GnRH neuron activation. Monitor FSH every 3-5 days; in women, rising FSH indicates follicular recruitment; in men, FSH elevation precedes spermatogenic response. In women: follow ovarian ultrasound for follicle size and ovulation timing, confirming egg maturation progression. In men: repeat semen analysis after 6-8 weeks of consistent dosing to assess sperm count, motility, and morphology improvements.

Initial bloodwork every 2-3 weeks, then monthly during maintenance phases. This monitoring validates protocol efficacy and guides dose adjustments.

Storage, Stability, and Practical Management

Reconstituted kisspeptin requires immediate refrigeration (2-8°C) and remains stable 30-45 days under proper conditions. Protect from light and freeze-thaw cycles. For pulsatile protocols requiring 6-8 daily injections, prepare sterile pre-filled syringes weekly with calculated doses to simplify administration. Label each syringe with date and time. Store in refrigerated container and rotate stock to prevent waste. Some practitioners prepare daily aliquots to ensure maximum stability between injections.

Proper storage directly impacts efficacy: degraded peptide produces diminished GnRH response and suboptimal results. Invest in reliable refrigeration and protect inventory from temperature fluctuations.

Recovery and Break Protocols Between Cycles

A major advantage of kisspeptin over exogenous hormones: kisspeptin creates zero pituitary suppression, so no recovery period is needed. Stopping kisspeptin immediately halts the stimulus; GnRH neurons resume baseline activity within hours. Natural hormone production returns to baseline within 24-48 hours. Users can discontinue at the end of a cycle and the reproductive axis remains functional—unlike testosterone therapy which requires months of recovery from suppression.

This flexibility allows: repeating cycles immediately without danger, taking breaks to assess sustained improvements, or implementing maintenance protocols (lower-dose kisspeptin 1-2x weekly) between major cycles. No suppression means no mandatory recovery windows.

Long-term Cycling Safety Across Multiple Phases

Research in clinical trials spanning years shows no tolerance development, pituitary damage, or serious adverse effects from repeated kisspeptin cycles. Receptor function remains intact across numerous treatment phases. Users report sustained efficacy across 3-6+ cycles without diminishing LH response. Pregnancy and breastfeeding can pause treatment safely; resumption after lactation shows restored responsiveness. This safety profile distinguishes kisspeptin from hormone replacement therapies requiring careful monitoring for long-term suppression risk.

Long-term users consistently report that subsequent cycles produce superior results compared to the first cycle, suggesting progressive axis restoration rather than tolerance development. This validates kisspeptin's sustainable use for extended reproductive optimization.

Post-Cycle Expectations and Sustained Improvements

After completing a kisspeptin cycle: in women, restored ovulatory cycles typically continue indefinitely once reestablished—the reproductive axis "remembers" the normal pattern. In men, elevated testosterone and sperm improvements sustain long-term, though may gradually decrease without maintenance dosing. Many practitioners implement maintenance protocols: lower-dose kisspeptin (1 mcg/kg 1-2x weekly) between major cycles maintains improvements at lower cost. Complete discontinuation also works; many users find improvements persist permanently.

The key distinction from suppressive therapies: improvements from kisspeptin represent axis restoration, not dependency. Once restored, the axis often maintains function without continuous treatment. This makes kisspeptin uniquely suited for temporary optimization rather than requiring lifelong hormone replacement.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a typical kisspeptin cycle last?

Standard reproductive optimization: 4-6 weeks of pulsatile dosing. IVF cycles: single dose trigger protocol. Male fertility: 6-8 weeks minimum to see full spermatogenesis improvement. Cycle length depends on goals and baseline reproductive status. Most practitioners recommend at least 4 weeks for meaningful results.

Do I need breaks between kisspeptin cycles?

No mandatory breaks required since kisspeptin creates no suppression. Users can cycle continuously, take breaks, or alternate cycles with maintenance dosing. Flexibility is a major advantage. Some prefer breaks to assess sustained improvements; others repeat cycles immediately. Both approaches work safely.

Will I develop tolerance to kisspeptin over multiple cycles?

No tolerance documented in research. Users report sustained LH response across 3-6+ cycles. The pulsatile administration pattern (essential for efficacy) prevents receptor desensitization. Clinical trials spanning years show no diminishing response, validating long-term cycling safety.

Can I use kisspeptin continuously without breaks?

Yes, continuous pulsatile kisspeptin is safe. However, most practitioners cycle 4-6 weeks on then take 2-4 week breaks to optimize cost and assess sustained improvements. No physiologic requirement for breaks exists—the choice is practical and personal.

How do I know when my cycle is working?

LH response: should double to 10x baseline within 30 minutes post-injection. Hormone bloodwork: FSH and reproductive hormone elevation within 1-2 weeks. In women: follicle growth visible on ultrasound by week 2-3, ovulation by week 3-4. In men: subjective testosterone effects (energy, libido) within 1-2 weeks; sperm improvement by 6-8 weeks.

What if I miss doses during a cycle?

Occasional missed injections (1-2 per week) have minimal impact on cycle outcomes. Consistency matters, but perfect adherence isn't required. Missing many consecutive doses may delay results by 1-2 weeks. Resume dosing as soon as possible; the cycle continues from that point forward.
