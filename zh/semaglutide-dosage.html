Semaglutide Dosage

Complete Dosing Protocols, Titration Schedules, and Individualization Strategies

📖 8 min read 🔬 Research & Education ⚠️ Not medical advice ✅ Last reviewed: April 2026

Medical Disclaimer

For informational and educational purposes only. Semaglutide is a prescription GLP-1 agonist approved for diabetes (Ozempic) and weight loss (Wegovy). Consult a licensed healthcare professional. See full disclaimer.

Standard semaglutide dosing for weight loss (Wegovy) starts at 0.25mg weekly, escalating by 0.25mg every 4 weeks to a target of 2.4mg weekly. Diabetes dosing (Ozempic) follows similar titration but targets 1mg weekly. Dosing varies by BMI, metabolic status, and side effect tolerance. No dose increases are needed beyond 2.4mg. Individual optimization prevents overdosing and side effect severity.

What Is the Standard Starting Dose of Semaglutide?

For weight loss (Wegovy), semaglutide starts at 0.25mg administered once weekly via subcutaneous injection. This low starting dose allows the GI system to adapt and tolerability evaluation before escalation. Most users tolerate 0.25mg well with minimal side effects.

What Is the Titration Schedule for Semaglutide?

Standard 16-week titration: Week 1-4: 0.25mg, Week 5-8: 0.5mg, Week 9-12: 1.0mg, Week 13-16: 1.7mg, Week 17+: 2.4mg. Each dose is given once weekly on the same day. Slower titration (extend each phase to 6-8 weeks) is an option for users experiencing side effects.

Are There Alternative Dosing Schedules for Slow Dose Escalation?

Yes. Extended titration: remain at 0.25mg for 8 weeks, then escalate by 0.25mg every 6-8 weeks. This slower approach reduces GI side effects, improves tolerability, and may produce more sustainable weight loss. Endpoint dosing can remain at 1.7mg or 1.0mg rather than escalating to 2.4mg.

What Is the Difference Between Ozempic and Wegovy Dosing?

Ozempic (diabetes) targets maintenance at 1.0mg weekly for glucose control. Wegovy (weight loss) targets 2.4mg weekly for maximal GLP-1 receptor saturation. Pharmacology is identical; dosing differs by indication and benefit-to-risk assessment. Ozempic users achieving adequate glucose control at 1.0mg typically don't escalate to 2.4mg.

Can You Adjust Semaglutide Dose Based on Response?

Yes, with provider guidance. Users not achieving adequate weight loss at 1.7mg can escalate to 2.4mg. Users experiencing severe side effects can stabilize at lower doses (0.5-1.0mg) with acceptable weight loss. Dose adjustments should occur at 4-week intervals minimum.

What Happens If You Miss a Semaglutide Dose?

If missed by <2 days: inject immediately, then resume weekly schedule from that day. If missed by >2 days: skip the missed dose, resume the following week on the original day. Never double-dose to compensate. Missing one dose has minimal impact; weight loss trajectory remains largely unchanged.

Is There a Maximum Semaglutide Dose?

FDA-approved maximum is 2.4mg weekly. Doses beyond 2.4mg show no additional weight loss benefit and increase side effect risk. Some off-label use at 3-4mg has been reported anecdotally, but clinical data is absent and risks are undefined. Stay within 2.4mg limits.

How Does Body Weight Affect Semaglutide Dosing?

Semaglutide dosing is weight-independent. A 150-lb person and a 300-lb person receive identical doses (0.25-2.4mg weekly). Weight loss response may differ slightly, but dose escalation follows the same schedule regardless of starting weight. No dose adjustments are made for BMI.

Should Semaglutide Dose Be Reduced for Elderly or Renally Impaired Users?

Age >65: no dose adjustment needed; standard 0.25-2.4mg titration applies. Renal impairment: no dose adjustment required for eGFR >30. Hepatic impairment: no dose adjustment. Use standard titration in most populations unless side effects warrant slower escalation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I can't tolerate escalating to the next dose?

Remain at current dose for 8 additional weeks, then attempt escalation. Many users achieve significant weight loss at 1.0-1.7mg without reaching 2.4mg. Discuss continuation at lower dose with your provider.

Can you take semaglutide more frequently than weekly?

No. Semaglutide is formulated for once-weekly administration. More frequent dosing would lead to toxicity. If you miss a dose, don't compensate with a second dose sooner than 1 week later.

Is there a difference in effectiveness between 1.7mg and 2.4mg?

Yes. 2.4mg produces slightly greater weight loss (5-10% additional beyond 1.7mg) but also increases GI side effects. Individual response varies; some achieve plateau at 1.7mg with acceptable efficacy.

Should I stop my dose if side effects appear?

Not necessarily. Remain at current dose for additional weeks to assess adaptation. Consider slowing future escalations or stabilizing at current dose. Only discontinue if side effects are severe/dangerous.

Does semaglutide dose correlate with weight loss amount?

Partially. Higher doses produce greater weight loss, but response varies widely. Some users lose 25%+ at 1.7mg; others lose only 10% at 2.4mg. Genetics, diet, and lifestyle heavily influence outcomes independent of dose.

Can you stop semaglutide and restart at the same dose?

If discontinuation was <4 weeks, restart at previous dose. If discontinuation was >4 weeks, restart at 0.25mg and re-titrate to avoid side effects. Rapid escalation after a break increases GI disruption risk.
