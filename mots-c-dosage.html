What Is MOTS-C and Why Does Dosing Matter?

MOTS-C (Mitochondrial ORF of the 12S rRNA Type-C) is a Mitochondrial-derived peptide, metabolic regulator. Encoded by the 12S rRNA region of the mitochondrial genome; naturally produced in response to stress, exercise, and aging. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for MOTS-C has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended MOTS-C Dosage?

The most widely used MOTS-C dosage is 5-10 mg per week, administered 2-3 times weekly via subcutaneous injection.

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of MOTS-C is not established in humans, which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For MOTS-C specifically, the 8-12 weeks with 8-12 week breaks cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Mitochondrial-derived peptide, metabolic regulator compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.
