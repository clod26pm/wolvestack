## 🔍 Trend Report

### Trend #1: Peptide Sciences Shutdown Shakes the Gray Market

Peptide Sciences — the largest gray-market research peptide vendor in the U.S., reportedly generating $7.4M/month in online sales — voluntarily shut down on March 6, 2026. No official explanation was given, but industry observers point to escalating FDA enforcement, ITC import actions, and quality concerns (Finnrick testing revealed E-rated retatrutide across 37 samples). This is the clearest signal yet that the gray-market peptide business model is collapsing. Content angle: position this as a reason to go through legitimate compounding pharmacies — and why quality sourcing matters more than ever.

### Trend #2: RFK Jr.'s 14-Peptide Reclassification — Still Pending

On February 27, 2026, HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. announced on Joe Rogan's podcast that ~14 of the 19 peptides on the FDA's Category 2 restricted list would move back to Category 1, restoring legal compounding access with a physician's prescription. BPC-157 is expected to be among them. However, as of March 25, the formal FDA reclassification has NOT been officially published. This creates a window of confusion — and a massive content opportunity to educate audiences on what "Category 1" actually means (legal to compound ≠ FDA-approved drug).

### Trend #3: GHK-Cu Search Volume Explodes +1,016% YoY

GHK-Cu (copper peptide) is now the fastest-growing peptide in search, up over 1,000% year-over-year. Three new randomized controlled trials for wound healing and hair growth were initiated in 2025. The biohacking community is increasingly stacking GHK-Cu with BPC-157 for combined recovery + skin/hair benefits. This is an undertapped content angle — most creators are still focused on BPC-157 alone.
