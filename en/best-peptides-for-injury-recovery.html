Sports injuries and acute trauma trigger inflammatory cascades that delay healing if not optimally managed. GHK-Cu for Collagen Quality

GHK-Cu regulates collagen synthesis and remodeling, ensuring injury repair produces strong, flexible tissue rather than rigid scar. Muscle and ligament injuries require collagen; GHK-Cu ensures quality remodeling. Combined with BPC-157 and TB-500, it prevents fibrosis while supporting tensile strength recovery.

Topical GHK-Cu (10 mcg/mL) applied over injury sites daily; systemic dosing (500 mcg subcutaneous 2-3x weekly) supports deep tissue remodeling. Results within 4-8 weeks: improved tissue quality, accelerated scar maturation, restored function.

ARA-290 for Microcirculation and Ischemic Injury

Acute injury triggers ischemia (reduced blood flow to tissue), creating secondary damage. ARA-290 enhances microvascular function and tissue perfusion, reducing ischemic injury. It works synergistically with BPC-157 and TB-500, ensuring optimal blood supply for growth factors to reach damaged tissue.

Particularly valuable for compartment syndrome risk, crush injuries, or vascular compromise. Dosing: 1-2 mg subcutaneous daily or 3x weekly. Results within 1-2 weeks as tissue perfusion improves and secondary damage resolves.
