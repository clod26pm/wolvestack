Lyophilized sermorelin powder: stable at room temperature (18-25°C) for 12+ months when stored in original container away from light and moisture. Reconstituted sermorelin: must be refrigerated (2-8°C) and is stable 3-7 days (some sources cite 10-14 days with perfect storage). Do not freeze reconstituted sermorelin (ice crystals damage peptide structure). Opened powder vials: use reconstituted solution within 7 days. Proper storage ensures potency and safety; improper storage degrades peptide and risks contamination.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can I keep lyophilized sermorelin?

12+ months at room temperature (18-25°C) in original container, away from light and moisture. Unopened vials maintain stability longer.

How long is reconstituted sermorelin stable?

3-7 days when refrigerated (2-8°C). Some users extend to 10-14 days with perfect storage; don't rely on this. Use within 7 days to be safe.

Should I refrigerate unopened powder?

Not required. Room temperature storage is fine. Refrigeration doesn't improve stability and increases condensation risk when opening vial.

Can I freeze sermorelin after reconstitution?

Not recommended. Freezing damages peptide structure via ice crystal formation. Refrigeration (2-8°C) is standard. Thawed sermorelin should be discarded.

How do I know if sermorelin is degraded?

Visual: cloudiness, discoloration, or particles indicate degradation. Odor: off-odors suggest contamination. Efficacy: reduced effects suggest degradation. Discard if uncertain.

Can I store sermorelin in a regular refrigerator?

Yes. Standard refrigerators (2-8°C) are appropriate. Keep vials in original packaging to protect from light. Avoid temperature fluctuations (opening/closing frequently).
