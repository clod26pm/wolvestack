What Is CJC-1295 and Why Does Dosing Matter?

CJC-1295 (CJC-1295 (Modified GRF 1-29)) is a Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog. Synthetic peptide analog of GHRH (first 29 amino acids) with four amino acid substitutions for enhanced stability. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for CJC-1295 has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended CJC-1295 Dosage?

The most widely used CJC-1295 dosage is 100-300 mcg, administered 1-3 times daily, typically before bed via subcutaneous injection.

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of CJC-1295 is ~30 minutes (no DAC), ~8 days (with DAC), which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For CJC-1295 specifically, the 8-12 weeks, often paired with Ipamorelin cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Growth Hormone Releasing Hormone (GHRH) analog compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

When Should You Dose CJC-1295?

With a half-life of ~30 minutes (no DAC), ~8 days (with DAC), timing your CJC-1295 administration affects peak blood levels. Most protocols call for 1-3 times daily, typically before bed.

How Long Should a CJC-1295 Cycle Last?

Standard CJC-1295 cycles run 8-12 weeks, often paired with Ipamorelin. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

Clinical research demonstrates significant increases in GH and IGF-1 levels in human subjects. Studies show improved body composition with increased lean mass and reduced body fat, particularly when combined with GHRP peptides like Ipamorelin.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Possible water retention, tingling/numbness in hands (carpal tunnel-like), increased appetite, mild headache, and flushing at injection site. Effects are dose-dependent and typically subside with continued use.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most CJC-1295-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does CJC-1295 Dosing Change When Stacking?

The gold standard stack is CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin — GHRH + GHRP together produce GH pulses far greater than either alone, through synergistic pituitary stimulation via two distinct receptor pathways.

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.
