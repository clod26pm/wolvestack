What Is Pinealon?

Pinealon (Glu-Asp-Arg tripeptide (EDR)) is a Tripeptide geroprotector, neuropeptide. Synthetic tripeptide derived from pineal gland secretions; Russian-developed geroprotector.

It is researched for its potential effects on cognitive function improvement, neuroprotection, enhanced cerebellum protection, reduced brain cell apoptosis, emotional balance. Pineal-selective tripeptide with demonstrated neuroprotection against both aging and trauma — one of the few peptides targeting the pineal gland specifically.

For beginners: This guide assumes no prior peptide experience. We'll cover everything from what Pinealon is to how to reconstitute, inject, and structure your first cycle.

How Does Pinealon Work?

Activates gene expression and protein synthesis in neural tissues while reducing apoptosis. Displays neuroprotection through reduced oxidative stress, enhanced cerebellum neuron resistance, and improved mitochondrial function in aging brains.

Understanding the mechanism helps set realistic expectations about what Pinealon can and cannot do.

How Do You Get Started With Pinealon?

Step 1 — Source: Purchase Pinealon from a vendor with third-party Certificate of Analysis (COA) testing. This confirms purity (aim for 98%+) and rules out contamination.

Step 2 — Supplies: You'll need bacteriostatic water, insulin syringes (1mL/100-unit), alcohol swabs, and a clean workspace.

Step 3 — Reconstitute: Add BAC water to the Pinealon vial — use our peptide calculator for exact amounts. Let the water run down the side of the vial; never spray directly on the powder. Swirl gently.

Step 4 — Dose: Draw 10-30 mcg daily using the calculator's syringe unit conversion.

Step 5 — Inject: Clean the injection site with alcohol. Pinch a fold of abdominal fat and insert the needle at 45° for subcutaneous injection. Push the plunger slowly and hold for 5 seconds.

What Should Your First Pinealon Cycle Look Like?

Dosage: Start at the lower end of 10-30 mcg daily. This lets you assess tolerance before committing to a full cycle.

Frequency: once daily via subcutaneous injection.

Duration: 5-10 day cycles, repeated with rest periods. Don't cut cycles short — many Tripeptide geroprotector, neuropeptide effects take weeks to fully manifest.

Off-cycle: Plan a 4-week break before starting another cycle.

What Side Effects Should Beginners Watch For?

Generally well-tolerated. Potential injection site reactions, mild muscle aches, headache, transient lightheadedness.

As a beginner, track everything — dose, time, injection site, and any effects (positive or negative). This data helps optimize future cycles.

What Are Common Beginner Mistakes?

Not using BAC water: Sterile water lacks the preservative that prevents bacterial growth. Always use bacteriostatic water.

Inconsistent dosing: Skipping doses or varying timing significantly reduces outcomes. Set a daily alarm.

Poor storage: Reconstituted Pinealon must stay refrigerated at 2-8°C. Leaving it at room temperature degrades the compound rapidly.

Buying cheap: Low-cost peptides without COA testing may be underdosed, contaminated, or mislabeled. Quality matters more than price.

Bottom Line for Pinealon Beginners

Start at the lower end of 10-30 mcg daily, dose once daily, cycle for 5-10 day cycles, repeated with rest periods, and track everything. Source from COA-tested vendors and follow proper reconstitution protocol.
