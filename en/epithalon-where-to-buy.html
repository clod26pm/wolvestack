Red Flags to Avoid When Buying?

Avoid vendors that make medical claims ("cures aging", "reverses aging"), lack purity documentation, price suspiciously low (quality costs money), or have no customer reviews. Reputable vendors never claim FDA approval or medicinal efficacy—they sell for "research use only." If marketing sounds like medicine, it's a scam. Legitimate vendors are honest about regulatory status and research-only purpose.

Check vendor legitimacy: do they have a professional website (not a bare Shopify store), transparent company information, clear return policies, and third-party purity testing visible? Legitimate vendors publish GC/MS or HPLC purity certificates freely. If a vendor refuses to share purity data or claims it's "proprietary," that's a red flag—reputable labs share testing transparently.

Avoid vendors operating from suspicious jurisdictions, using encrypted payment only (bitcoin, untraceable methods), or shipping from countries known for peptide counterfeiting. Establish vendors operating for 5+ years with consistent customer reviews are safer than brand-new vendors. Read reviews on Reddit, biohacking forums, and peptide communities—user experiences reveal vendor quality quickly.
