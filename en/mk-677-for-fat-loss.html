Fat Mobilization Mechanism

GH directly activates hormone-sensitive lipase, mobilizing triglycerides from adipose tissue for energy. MK-677 increases GH 30-90%, enhancing fat oxidation and increasing metabolic rate 10-15%. During caloric deficit, elevated GH preserves lean mass while fat is preferentially mobilized for energy—ideal body recomposition.

Lean Mass Preservation During Cuts

Caloric deficit catabolic signals threaten muscle breakdown. Elevated GH and IGF-1 from MK-677 counter this, preserving or building muscle despite deficit. Combined with resistance training, users maintain strength and muscle while losing fat. Expected result: leaner, more muscular appearance vs simple weight loss.

Cutting Protocol and Dosing

Deficit: 200-300 calories below maintenance (avoid aggressive 500+ deficits that blunt MK-677's GH response). Dosage: 10-15 mg daily (lower than bulking; maintains satiety better). Training: 4-5x weekly resistance training to preserve muscle. Nutrition: 1.4-1.8 g protein per lb to maximize muscle retention.

Training and Cardio During Cuts

Resistance training 4-5x weekly preserves muscle during deficit. Cardio 3-4x weekly for additional fat loss (30-45 min moderate intensity). MK-677's improved recovery allows more training frequency without overtraining. Progressive overload on lifts remains important to preserve strength and muscle.

Comparing Fat Loss on MK-677 vs Without

Without MK-677 in deficit: 1 lb fat loss weekly, 0.25 lb muscle loss weekly (poor body composition change). With MK-677 in deficit: 1 lb fat loss weekly, 0 lb muscle loss (or slight muscle gain if training hard)—superior final composition. The difference is dramatic over 12-week cut.

FAQ: Fat Loss Questions

Q: Can I use MK-677 if cutting for competition?

A: Yes, but discontinue 4-6 weeks pre-competition (water retention, prolactin can interfere with contest prep).

Q: Does MK-677 increase appetite during cuts?

A: Yes. Manage with high-volume low-calorie foods and meal prep to stick to deficit despite increased hunger.
