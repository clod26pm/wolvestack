AOD-9604 powder (lyophilized) remains stable for 2+ years when stored at room temperature in a cool, dry place away from light. Reconstituted solutions (mixed with bacteriostatic water) last 15–30 days refrigerated at 2–8°C. Proper storage is critical: freezing damages peptides, room temperature degrades reconstituted solutions rapidly, and light exposure reduces potency.

Powder Storage: Unopened Vials

Optimal Conditions:

  • Temperature: Room temperature (20–25°C / 68–77°F) or cool environment.
  • Location: Dark area (away from direct sunlight or UV light).
  • Humidity: Dry environment; avoid moisture exposure.
  • Container: Sealed vial with original desiccant packet intact.

Stability Duration:

Under proper conditions, unopened AOD-9604 powder vials are stable for:

  • 2+ years at room temperature (most common scenario).
  • Indefinitely in freezer (below -18°C / 0°F), though not required.
  • 5+ years refrigerated (2–8°C) in sealed vial.

Best Practice: Store unopened powder at room temperature in a cool, dark place. Avoid the freezer unless you're planning multi-year storage (freezing/thawing cycles damage peptides).

Reconstituted Solution Storage

Critical Parameters:

Temperature (Most Important):

  • Refrigerator (2–8°C): Optimal. Reconstituted AOD-9604 lasts 15–30 days at this temperature.
  • Room temperature (20–25°C): Dangerous. Peptides degrade rapidly; usable for only 2–5 days maximum.
  • Freezer (-18°C or below): NOT recommended. Ice crystal formation damages amino acid chains; once thawed, peptide may be partially denatured.

Light Exposure:

  • Direct sunlight: Avoid entirely. UV light degrades peptides within hours.
  • Ambient indoor light: Acceptable but not ideal. Use opaque storage container if possible.
  • Best practice: Store in original vial (often amber/opaque) inside a dark drawer or box.

Humidity and Contamination:

  • Keep vial sealed. Opening and closing repeatedly introduces air and bacteria.
  • Keep refrigerator humidity normal (not excessively damp). Moisture can promote bacterial growth.
  • Never store open or uncapped vials.

Reconstitution Shelf Life: Expected Timelines

High-Quality Reconstitution (Sterile Technique, Bacteriostatic Water):

  • Days 1–15: Optimal potency. Full efficacy expected.
  • Days 16–30: Acceptable. Slight potency loss (5–10%), but still effective.
  • Days 31–45: Marginal. Potency may be compromised by 20–40%. Not recommended unless unavoidable.
  • Beyond Day 45: Discard. Efficacy cannot be assured.

Low-Quality Reconstitution (Non-Sterile Water, Contamination Risk):

  • Days 1–5: Usable, but bacterial growth begins immediately.
  • Beyond Day 5: High contamination risk. Discard.

Storage Best Practices

Setup Your Storage Environment:

  1. Designate a peptide drawer/box: In your refrigerator, use a dedicated area (lower shelf, away from humidity-prone locations).
  2. Use opaque container: Store vials in a dark box or bag to block ambient light.
  3. Label clearly: Write reconstitution date and expiration date on the vial in permanent marker.
  4. Keep the cap sealed: Between injections, cap the vial tightly. Use a fresh needle each time to withdraw.
  5. Store away from temperature fluctuations: Avoid back of refrigerator door (temperature varies with frequent opening). Use the back of the main shelf instead.

Travel and Portable Storage

Short Trips (1–3 Days):

  • Reconstituted vial: Use a small insulated bag with an ice pack. Maintain 2–8°C.
  • Powder vial: No special care needed; room temperature is fine.

Long Trips (1–2 Weeks):

  • Reconstituted solutions: Use a travel cooler with ice packs or reusable cooling elements. Keep temperature at 2–8°C. Replace ice as needed.
  • Better option: Travel with powder vials only; reconstitute at your destination using locally sourced bacteriostatic water.

International Travel / Customs:

Be aware of legal considerations. AOD-9604 may be regulated or prohibited in some jurisdictions. Research local laws before traveling with peptides across borders. Powder is easier to travel with than reconstituted solutions (less risk of confiscation for "liquid" restrictions).

How to Tell If Your AOD-9604 Is Degraded

Visual Signs of Degradation (Powder):

  • Color change (from white/off-white to yellowed or discolored).
  • Visible moisture or clumping (suggests water exposure).
  • Unusual odor when vial is opened.

Visual Signs of Degradation (Reconstituted):

  • Cloudiness that doesn't clear within 5 minutes (suggests contamination).
  • Discoloration (yellowing or browning).
  • Particles or sediment floating in solution.
  • Foul smell (indicates bacterial growth).

Functional Signs (No Visual Issues):

  • Using reconstituted solution beyond 30 days = assume potency loss even if it looks clear.
  • No measurable results after 4 weeks of use (despite diet/training discipline) = potential degradation or vendor quality issue.

Quality Testing and Reconstitution Best Practices

Assessing Vial Quality Upon Receipt
When you receive AOD-9604 powder, inspect the vial carefully. The powder should appear white to off-white, dry, and free of moisture or discoloration. If the vial arrives with visible moisture, yellowing, or unusual odors, contact your vendor immediately—these are signs of poor storage or potential contamination during manufacturing. Do not use compromised vials.

Reconstitution Temperature Optimization
When reconstituting AOD-9604, use room-temperature bacteriostatic water (20–25°C). Some users warm the bacteriostatic water slightly (to 25–30°C) before adding to the powder vial; this accelerates dissolution without degrading the peptide. Never use hot water (>40°C), which risks peptide denaturation. Allow 2–5 minutes for full dissolution after adding bacteriostatic water; do not shake vigorously (this introduces air bubbles and can denature the peptide).

Verification of Reconstitution Success
A properly reconstituted AOD-9604 solution should be clear, colorless to pale yellow, and free of visible particles or cloudiness. If cloudiness persists after 10 minutes of gentle mixing, the powder may be contaminated or the bacteriostatic water may be substandard. Discard and do not use. Clear solutions are safe to inject; cloudy solutions pose contamination risk and should not be used.

FAQ: AOD-9604 Storage

Can I freeze reconstituted AOD-9604?

Not recommended. Freezing forms ice crystals that damage the peptide structure. Once thawed, potency is compromised. Stick to 2–8°C refrigeration for reconstituted solutions. If you need long-term storage, freeze the powder instead (it tolerates freezing better).

How long can I store AOD-9604 powder?

Unopened powder vials are stable for 2+ years at room temperature in a cool, dark place. In a freezer, stability is essentially indefinite (5+ years confirmed). Check the vial for signs of damage (moisture, discoloration) but expect minimal degradation if properly stored.

What temperature should my refrigerator be for peptide storage?

2–8°C (35–46°F) is the goldstandard. Most home refrigerators maintain this range in the main shelf area (not the door). If your refrigerator runs warmer, it will degrade reconstituted peptides faster. Verify temperature with a thermometer if storing for long periods.

Is it OK to store reconstituted AOD-9604 at room temperature for a few days?

Technically usable for 2–5 days, but degradation is significant. By day 3, potency loss may be 10–20%. By day 5, 30%+. Room temperature accelerates bacterial growth too, increasing contamination risk. Always refrigerate reconstituted solutions; it's the safest practice.

Should I store powder vials in the refrigerator?

Not necessary. Room temperature (cool, dark) is fine for unopened powder. Refrigeration doesn't hurt, but it's not required. The extra benefit of refrigerating powder is minimal; focus refrigeration space on reconstituted solutions, which degrade faster.

What if I expose reconstituted AOD-9604 to light for a few hours?

Brief light exposure (a few hours) causes minimal damage. Extended UV exposure (sunlight, several hours) degrades peptides significantly. If you accidentally leave your vial in sunlight for a few hours, potency is likely reduced by 5–15%. Discard if exposed to direct sunlight for extended periods.
