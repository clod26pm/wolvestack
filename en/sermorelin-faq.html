Frequently Asked Questions

Is sermorelin FDA-approved?

Sermorelin (Geref) is FDA-approved only for growth hormone deficiency diagnosis; off-label use for performance/anti-aging is not FDA-approved. Available for research use only.

What are the main side effects?

Water retention (most common), joint pain/arthralgia (10-20% at higher doses), carpal tunnel syndrome (rare), temporary cortisol elevation, numbness/tingling. Side effects are dose-dependent.

Is sermorelin safer than HGH?

Sermorelin is gentler (stimulates your own GH, not exogenous hormone), carries lower cancer risk, and doesn't suppress HPTA. HGH is stronger but carries higher cardiovascular/cancer concerns.

Can I stack sermorelin with testosterone?

Yes. Testosterone and sermorelin have synergistic effects on muscle gain and fat loss. No direct contraindications. Monitor for excessive water retention and lipid changes.

How do I inject sermorelin correctly?

Inject subcutaneously (under the skin) at 45-degree angle, preferably on abdomen or thigh. Use 30-31 gauge needle. Rotate injection sites daily to prevent lipodystrophy.

Does sermorelin require refrigeration?

Reconstituted sermorelin requires refrigeration (2-8°C) and should be used within 3-7 days. Lyophilized powder is stable at room temperature until reconstituted.
