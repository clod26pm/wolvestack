Store GHRP-6 powder sealed, desiccated, at 2-8°C (refrigerated) or room temperature (protected from light). Lyophilized powder remains stable for 12-24 months if sealed. After reconstitution, store at 2-8°C in refrigerator. Bacteriostatic water reconstitution maintains potency ~90% for 30 days; sterile water maintenance is 7-10 days. Avoid freezing, repeated freeze-thaw cycles, direct sunlight, and heat exposure.

Powder Storage Conditions

GHRP-6 arrives as lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder sealed in vial with desiccant. Proper storage: sealed vial at 2-8°C (refrigerator) or room temperature (20-25°C) protected from light. Desiccant and seal preserve potency by preventing moisture absorption (which denatures protein). Unopened vials maintain 90%+ potency for 12-24 months if kept dry and cool. Once vial is opened for reconstitution, remaining powder degrades more rapidly due to moisture exposure.

Reconstituted Solution Storage

After reconstitution with bacteriostatic water or normal saline, store solution at 2-8°C in refrigerator. Do NOT freeze. Refrigerated storage maintains 90%+ potency for 30 days. Room temperature storage: potency degrades 5-10% daily, losing 50% activity within 7-10 days. Bacteriostatic water (contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative) provides superior storage stability compared to sterile water (7-10 day max). Minimize air exposure and light exposure during storage; opaque container in dark location ideal.

Freeze-Thaw Damage

Freezing reconstituted GHRP-6 denatures protein structure, reducing potency significantly. Each freeze-thaw cycle reduces potency by approximately 5-10%. If accidentally frozen, thaw slowly in refrigerator (not using heat), assess solution clarity (cloudiness indicates protein denaturation), and if clear may still be usable but potency is reduced 10-20%. Discard solution if cloudy or discolored after freezing; indicates irreversible protein denaturation.

Light and Temperature Sensitivity

GHRP-6 peptide degrades with prolonged direct light exposure. Store in dark location or opaque container. UV light accelerates degradation; avoid window storage or bright light. Temperature stability: 2-8°C (refrigerator) optimal. Above 8°C, degradation rate increases exponentially. Room temperature (20-25°C) acceptable for powder for days to weeks but not ideal for long-term storage. Never expose to heat (sunlight, heat sources) as this accelerates degradation.

Shelf Life and Potency Tracking

Lyophilized powder: 12-24 months sealed at appropriate temperature. After opening/reconstitution: 30 days refrigerated with bacteriostatic water, 7-10 days with sterile water. Users should track reconstitution date and apply 5-10% potency adjustment per week of storage in bacteriostatic water (e.g., solution stored 20 days: 100 mcg dose may contain only 90 mcg active peptide). Conservative users prepare small batch reconstitutions (1-2 mL) used within 2-3 weeks to ensure maximum potency.

Container and Vial Management

Keep GHRP-6 in original sealed vial until ready to reconstitute. Original vial provides protection (desiccant, light barrier, air seal). Once reconstituted, store in sealed vial with cap tightly closed. Do NOT decant into other containers (increases air/light exposure, contamination risk). Small glass vials with rubber septa and plastic caps provide best storage. Plastic containers may leach contaminants into solution; glass preferred for long-term storage.

FAQ: Storage

Can I store GHRP-6 at room temperature?

Powder: yes, 12-24 months at room temperature if sealed and protected from light. Reconstituted solution: not recommended; refrigerate for optimal stability. Room temperature storage of reconstituted solution loses 50% potency within 7-10 days.

How do I know if GHRP-6 has degraded?

Powder: discoloration (should be white/off-white), clumping despite desiccant (moisture contamination). Reconstituted solution: cloudiness, discoloration (should be clear/colorless), visible particles. Any abnormality suggests degradation or contamination; discard.

Can I store reconstituted solution in fridge indefinitely?

No. Bacteriostatic water reconstitution remains potent ~90% for 30 days. Sterile water maximum 7-10 days. Beyond 30 days, potency loss requires dose adjustment or discard.

Is freezing acceptable if I thaw carefully?

Not ideal. Freezing denatures protein, reducing potency 10-20%+. If accidentally frozen, thaw slowly in refrigerator, check clarity, but expect reduced potency. Future use requires dose adjustment.
