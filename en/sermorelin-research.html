Frequently Asked Questions

How many clinical studies support sermorelin?

400+ peer-reviewed studies exist; most in GH deficiency diagnosis and treatment contexts. Performance/anti-aging studies are less numerous (50-100) but growing.

Is sermorelin safe long-term?

Short-term (6-12 months) safety is well-established. Long-term (24+ months) human safety data is limited. Animal studies show no toxicity at therapeutic doses.

What do studies show about body composition?

Multiple studies document 3-5% muscle gain + 3-8% fat loss over 12 weeks at 1-2 mg daily doses. Results are modest but consistent.

Are there studies on sermorelin in healthy individuals?

Most studies focus on GH-deficient patients. Studies in healthy adults are limited. Extrapolation from GH deficiency data suggests sermorelin benefits age-related GH decline.

What does research say about side effects?

Short-term studies report mild side effects: water retention, transient arthralgia, carpal tunnel (rare). No serious adverse events at therapeutic doses in published literature.

Are there studies on sermorelin + training?

Limited. A few studies show sermorelin + resistance training produces larger gains than training alone. Most body composition data combines sermorelin with training.
