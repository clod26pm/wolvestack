Counterfeit Prevention and Quality Verification

Counterfeit Bronchogen, while not extensively documented, represents a theoretical risk with any expensive pharmaceutical compound. Genuine Bronchogen sourced from legitimate suppliers includes extensive quality markers; counterfeit or degraded product would lack these. Verification steps prior to use include: COA review, physical inspection, and functional verification after initial use.

Certificate of Analysis examination: legitimate COAs document: peptide sequence confirmation (AEDL tetrapeptide identification), purity percentage (typically 95%+), moisture content (<5%), batch/lot number, manufacture date, testing dates, and analyst signatures. COAs should originate from independent testing laboratories (not solely from the supplier). Request for HPLC chromatograms or mass spectrometry data provides additional verification for high-value purchases.

Physical inspection: genuine Bronchogen capsules show: consistent capsule appearance (no obvious damage), uniform powder color (white to off-white), no clumping or moisture damage indicators, and properly sealed original packaging with desiccant packets. Capsules should be uniform in size and appearance across the container. Significant variation or obvious damage indicates either defective manufacturing or storage degradation.

Lot number verification: research suppliers should provide batch/lot numbers traceable to Khavinson Institute or licensed manufacturers. Requesting manufacturer contact information to verify lot authenticity adds confidence. Some manufacturers maintain online databases where batch numbers can be verified. This additional verification step, while time-consuming, prevents purchasing from suppliers attempting to pass off generic or inferior peptides as legitimate Bronchogen.
