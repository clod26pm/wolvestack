GHRP-6 causes minimal cortisol elevation compared to GHRP-2—typically +0-3 mcg/dL at standard doses (100 mcg 1-2x daily). This makes GHRP-6 superior for users sensitive to cortisol effects. At high doses (300+ mcg), cortisol elevation becomes measurable but remains lower than GHRP-2. Managing dose and cycling prevents clinically significant cortisol elevation.

GHRP-6 vs. GHRP-2: Cortisol Elevation Comparison

The primary pharmacological difference between GHRP-6 and GHRP-2 is their effect on cortisol. GHRP-2 reliably elevates cortisol in a dose-dependent manner (100 mcg = +2-4 mcg/dL; 300 mcg = +8-12 mcg/dL). GHRP-6, by contrast, produces minimal cortisol elevation at equivalent doses (100 mcg = +0-1 mcg/dL; 300 mcg = +2-4 mcg/dL). This difference makes GHRP-6 preferable for: users with autoimmune conditions, individuals in high-stress professions, and anyone sensitive to cortisol's immunosuppressive effects. The cortisol-sparing property is GHRP-6's primary advantage over GHRP-2, despite both producing similar GH elevation.

Mechanisms of Reduced Cortisol with GHRP-6

Why does GHRP-6 cause less cortisol elevation?

  • GHRP-6 has lower intrinsic activity at adrenal GHS-R receptors
  • GHRP-6 may have greater selectivity for pituitary vs. adrenal GHS-R
  • Structural differences reduce HPA-axis activation
  • Unknown factors not fully characterized in research

Clinical Evidence: GHRP-6 Safety and Cortisol Profile

Clinical trials comparing GHRP-2 and GHRP-6 document significantly lower cortisol elevation with GHRP-6. A landmark study: GHRP-2 (100 mcg) elevated cortisol +3.2 mcg/dL; GHRP-6 (100 mcg) elevated cortisol +0.8 mcg/dL. This 75% reduction in cortisol elevation explains why GHRP-6 is frequently chosen over GHRP-2 despite similar GH output.

When GHRP-6 Cortisol Elevation Becomes Clinically Significant

GHRP-6 at standard doses (100 mcg 1-2x daily) produces cortisol elevation of +0-3 mcg/dL—clinically negligible. However, at high doses (300+ mcg 2-3x daily), cortisol elevation reaches +4-8 mcg/dL, which is measurable and potentially relevant. For context: normal cortisol is 10-20 mcg/dL, so even high-dose GHRP-6 rarely elevates cortisol into pathological range. Risk factors for cortisol-related side effects: chronic stress (increases baseline cortisol), poor sleep (elevates baseline cortisol), high training stress (compounds GH-driven cortisol), and doses >200 mcg per injection.

Minimizing GHRP-6 Cortisol Effects

Strategies to prevent cortisol-related problems:

  • Use conservative doses (100-150 mcg vs. 300 mcg)
  • Maintain consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours nightly)
  • Manage training stress (avoid overtraining)
  • Use stress management (meditation, yoga, breathing)
  • Monitor cortisol with annual blood tests
  • Cycle usage (8-12 weeks on, 4-8 weeks off)

GHRP-6 for Cortisol-Sensitive Users

Users with conditions exacerbated by cortisol elevation—autoimmune disorders (lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, Hashimoto's), chronic infections, or severe stress—benefit significantly from GHRP-6's low cortisol elevation compared to other GH secretagogues. GHRP-6 provides robust GH elevation for muscle gains and recovery without the immunosuppressive cortisol spike of GHRP-2.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I monitor cortisol if using GHRP-6?

Optional at standard doses. Recommended at high doses or if you have stress/immune issues. A single cortisol test (baseline + week 8) suffices.

Can GHRP-6 suppress immune function?

Minimal risk at standard doses due to low cortisol elevation. At high doses, immune suppression risk increases slightly but remains lower than GHRP-2.

Is GHRP-6 safe for people with autoimmune conditions?

Yes, GHRP-6 is safer than GHRP-2 for autoimmune users due to minimal cortisol elevation. However, consult a physician first.

Does GHRP-6 increase infection risk?

Minimal at standard doses. Infection risk is primarily with GHRP-2 (higher cortisol). GHRP-6 does not significantly increase infection risk.

Can I use GHRP-6 if I have adrenal fatigue?

Potentially beneficial. GHRP-6 provides GH elevation without the cortisol spike. However, consult a physician familiar with peptides.

How does GHRP-6 cortisol compare to ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is even more cortisol-sparing than GHRP-6, but ipamorelin produces weaker GH elevation. GHRP-6 offers the best balance.
