What Is the Evidence for ARA-290's Mechanism?

Selectively activates the innate repair receptor (IRR), a CD131/EPOR heterodimer, triggering anti-inflammatory and tissue-protective signaling without any effect on red blood cell production. Suppresses microglia activation and pro-inflammatory cytokine release. Dose-dependently reduces neuropathic pain through central inflammation suppression.

These pathways have been identified through in vitro studies, animal models, and where available, human trials.

What Makes ARA-290 Unique in Research?

What is ARA-290?

Synthetic 11-amino acid peptide engineered from the tissue-protective domain of erythropoietin (EPO). It is researched for neuropathic pain relief, inflammation reduction, nerve fiber regeneration, improved autonomic function, metabolic control in diabetes.
