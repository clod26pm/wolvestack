How Long Should a Retatrutide Cycle Last?

Standard Retatrutide cycles run 48+ weeks (continuous therapy in trials). This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

Phase 3 TRIUMPH-4 reported 71.2 lb average weight loss (December 2025). Diabetes trial (TRIUMPH-1) showed 16.8% weight loss. Seven Phase 3 trials with results expected by year-end 2026. Over 20 ongoing clinical trials.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Gastrointestinal effects predominate: diarrhea, vomiting, constipation, nausea. Generally manageable and dose-dependent. Potential pancreatitis risk similar to GLP-1 agonists.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most Retatrutide-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does Retatrutide Dosing Change When Stacking?

Triple agonism provides inherent synergy — not typically stacked with other GLP-1 or GIP agonists.

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.

What Is the Bottom Line on Retatrutide Dosing?

Start at 4, dose once weekly, and run cycles of 48+ weeks (continuous therapy in trials). Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.
