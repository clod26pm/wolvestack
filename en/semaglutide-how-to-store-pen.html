Unopened semaglutide pens are refrigerated at 36-46°F (2-8°C) until use. Once in-use, pens are stored at room temperature 68-77°F (20-25°C) for up to 30 days. Heat, light, and freezing damage the drug; avoid temperatures exceeding 86°F (30°C) or dropping below freezing. Proper storage maintains full potency; degraded or stored incorrectly pens may lose 10-30% efficacy.

Pre-Use Storage (Unopened Pens)

Temperature: unopened semaglutide pens require refrigeration at 2-8°C (36-46°F) until first use. Most users store in home refrigerator in a dedicated bin. Duration: semaglutide pens remain potent for 24 months from manufacture date if refrigerated properly. After 24 months, pens expire and efficacy may degrade. Location in fridge: store in the main compartment (not door, which experiences temperature fluctuations); avoid placing directly against back wall where temperature may be coldest. Light: store in original carton away from direct light. Accidental freezing: if a pen accidentally freezes, do NOT use—freezing damages the molecular structure of semaglutide. Discard and request replacement. Temperature monitoring: refrigerator thermometers help ensure temperature stability.

In-Use Storage (After First Injection)

Temperature: once first injection is made and the pen has been used, it can be stored at room temperature 20-25°C (68-77°F) for 30 days. Room temperature storage is more convenient for daily life and carries no efficacy loss if temperature remains stable. Duration: after initial use, the pen is stable for 30 days regardless of temperature (within safe range). On day 31, discard the pen even if dose remains—potency may have degraded. Heat exposure: storage above 25°C (77°F) increases degradation rate; exceeding 30°C (86°F) significantly damages the drug and reduces efficacy 10-30% over hours to days. Avoid: leaving pens in hot cars (can exceed 50°C/120°F), direct sunlight, on heating vents, or in warm bathrooms (shower steam damages pens).

Freezer and Heat Damage Prevention

Never freeze: semaglutide is destroyed by freezing at 0°C (32°F) or below. If a refrigerated pen accidentally freezes (left in a freezer or exposed to sub-freezing temperatures), it becomes unusable. Signs: frozen pens may show crystallization or cloudiness if thawed; discard these pens. Heat damage: exposure to 30°C (86°F) for several hours or 40°C (104°F) for minutes causes measurable degradation. Risk scenarios: cars on hot days (interior temperature >50°C), shipping without cooling packs during summer, beach bags left in sun. Prevention: always verify pens did not experience extreme temperatures during shipping or transport. Using temperature-monitored packaging during summer shipping ensures drug integrity.

Shipping and Travel Considerations

Domestic shipping (US): semaglutide shipped via standard mail or courier without cooling packs may experience heat exposure, risking efficacy loss. Safer approach: use pharmacy mail-order services that refrigerate during transit or request expedited shipping minimizing transit time. Ice pack inclusion: if self-arranging shipping, use insulated packaging with ice packs (wrapped in paper to avoid direct contact); include temperature data logger to verify temperature stability. Duration: semaglutide can tolerate 72-96 hours at room temperature (20-25°C) during shipping without efficacy loss. International travel: pens may be carried in checked or carry-on luggage; TSA allows injectable medications (with prescription label). In transit, keep pens in carry-on bag (checked luggage may exceed temperature extremes at high altitude or in unheated cargo holds). Destination: once arrived, resume refrigeration if planning to store >30 days.

Needle Handling and Contamination Prevention

After injection: immediately recap the needle or use the pen's needle safety mechanism. Never leave an uncapped needle exposed. Contamination: keep the pen clean and dry; visible dirt or debris should be wiped with a dry cloth (never wash with water which can damage the label and paper). Needle reuse: single-use needles should NOT be reused; using a needle twice risks contamination, dulling, and infection. Always use a fresh needle at each injection. Accidental needle sticks: if accidentally stuck with a used needle, wash the area immediately and consider post-exposure prophylaxis if source is known to be positive for bloodborne pathogen.

Expiration Dates and Stability

Manufacturing date vs. expiration: semaglutide pens are labeled with an expiration date (not manufacturing date). This date assumes proper storage conditions (refrigerated until opening, then 30 days room temperature). Using expired pens: beyond the expiration date, potency degrades unpredictably; 5-30% loss is possible. Using an expired pen is unlikely to cause harm but may provide suboptimal weight loss. Tracking: write the date of first use on the pen with a marker to track the 30-day window. Disposal: after 30 days, or upon expiration, discard the pen in a sharps container (never trash) to prevent needle stick injuries. Pharmacy disposal: many pharmacies accept sharps containers for safe disposal.

Storage Problems and Solutions

Forgotten in car on hot day: if a pen is left in a hot car for hours, discard it—potency likely degraded. Thawed from frozen state: discard; do not re-freeze or use. Dropped or damaged housing: if the pen casing is cracked but glass appears intact, the pen may still be usable if stored appropriately. If glass appears damaged or contains visible particles, discard. Leaking pen: if liquid leaks from the pen, discard immediately; leakage suggests structural failure and contamination risk. Visibility of drug: semaglutide is colorless or clear with a slight amber tint; visible cloudiness, particles, or discoloration indicate degradation—discard the pen.
