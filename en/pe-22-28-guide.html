What Is PE-22-28 and Why Does Dosing Matter?

PE-22-28 (PE-22-28 (spadin analog, TREK-1 inhibitor)) is a Ion channel inhibitor peptide. Synthetic shortened analog of spadin with enhanced TREK-1 potassium channel specificity and brain stability. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

How Long Should a PE-22-28 Cycle Last?

Standard PE-22-28 cycles run 4-8 weeks. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

Superior TREK-1 inhibition (IC50: 0.12 nM vs 40-60 nM for native spadin) with rapid antidepressant effects in rodent stress models. 4-day onset compared to weeks for conventional antidepressants. No human clinical trials.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most PE-22-28-related side effects are dose-dependent.
