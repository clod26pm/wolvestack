Is GHRP-2 Legal in the United States?

GHRP-2 occupies a complex legal gray area in the US. The peptide is not FDA-approved for human use, making it technically a research chemical. Federal law does not prohibit purchasing, possessing, or using GHRP-2 for personal research purposes. However, selling GHRP-2 explicitly marketed for human consumption violates FDA regulations. Distributing GHRP-2 with medical claims violates both FDA and FTC regulations. State-level restrictions vary. Some states classify uncontrolled research peptides as controlled substances, criminalizing possession. Most states default to federal law. The safest approach: purchase GHRP-2 only from vendors clearly labeling it as "research chemical" and "not for human consumption," and ensure your state does not classify research peptides as controlled substances.

GHRP-2 and WADA Regulations: Athletic Competition

The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) explicitly prohibits GHRP-2 at all times (in-competition and out-of-competition) in all sports. GHRP-2 is classified under the "Peptide Hormones, Growth Factors and Related Substances" category. Using GHRP-2 as an athlete results in sanctions including competition bans, medal stripping, and mandatory out-of-competition testing periods. Athlete testing detects GHRP-2 through specialized peptide analysis (liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry, LC-MS). The detection window for GHRP-2 is approximately 48-72 hours post-injection, though metabolite detection can extend to 7-14 days. Athletes competing in WADA-governed sports must abstain from GHRP-2 at least 2-4 weeks before competition to ensure clearance.
