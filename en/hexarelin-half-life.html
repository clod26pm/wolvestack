Hexarelin has a short half-life of approximately 30 minutes with peak GH response, returning to baseline by ~240 minutes total. This ultra-short duration necessitates precise timing around workouts and meals, with injections spaced 6-8 hours apart to avoid receptor desensitization and maintain consistent GH stimulation across daily cycles.

What Is Hexarelin's Half-Life?

Hexarelin's half-life refers to the time it takes for the peptide's concentration to drop to 50% of peak levels. The GH response peaks within 15-30 minutes of subcutaneous injection, reaches maximum at 30 minutes, and returns to baseline by approximately 240 minutes (4 hours). The peptide itself is metabolized quickly by serum proteases, making the window for GH stimulation remarkably brief compared to longer-acting GHRHs like CJC-1295.

This ultra-short pharmacokinetic profile is actually what makes hexarelin so potent: the acute, intense GH pulse is more stimulating to the somatotroph axis than prolonged, lower-level stimulation. However, the brevity also demands precision in timing and frequency of injections to maintain stable daily GH elevation.

How Does Half-Life Affect Dosing Frequency?

Because hexarelin's effects last only ~4 hours, users aiming for consistent daily GH stimulation typically inject 1-3 times daily, with at least 6-8 hours between doses. Spacing doses too close together (e.g., every 4 hours) risks excessive receptor stimulation and faster downregulation, reducing efficacy over time. Conversely, spacing doses too far apart (>10 hours) creates large gaps in GH coverage.

A practical schedule: 7 AM pre-workout, 1 PM mid-day, and 10 PM before bed. This distributes the GH stimulus across the day while allowing adequate inter-dose recovery. Some users prefer just morning and evening (2x daily) for simplicity, accepting slightly less consistent coverage in exchange for improved compliance.

Timing Hexarelin Injections Around Exercise

The short half-life makes hexarelin ideal for stacking with workouts. Injecting 30-60 minutes before intense resistance training or cardio enhances the anabolic effect of exercise by amplifying GH output during the workout window. The GH peak coincides with elevated post-exercise cortisol and adrenaline, potentially maximizing muscle protein synthesis signaling.

For fat loss and body composition, timing injections before fasted morning cardio can enhance lipolysis (fat mobilization) during the GH-elevated period. For sleep and recovery, evening injections 1-2 hours before bed favor sleep-stage GH secretion and nocturnal recovery processes, though prolactin elevation may disrupt sleep quality in some users.

Comparing Hexarelin Half-Life to Other GHRPs

GHRP-2 has a similar half-life (30-45 minutes) but slightly lower potency and less prolactin elevation. GHRP-6 also peaks within 30 minutes but triggers stronger appetite stimulation. Ipamorelin, another GHRP variant, has a similar short half-life but is less potent and causes minimal prolactin elevation, making it a "gentler" option for conservative users.

CJC-1295 (GHRH) has a dramatically longer half-life (6-8 hours), allowing once-daily dosing but with lower peak GH output. The advantage of hexarelin's short half-life: intense GH pulses that rapidly downregulate somatostatin, allowing repeated stimulation. The disadvantage: constant dosing schedule required to maintain coverage.

Half-Life and Receptor Downregulation

Repeated hexarelin dosing within the same day can cause rapid ghrelin receptor downregulation (desensitization), reducing GH output over time. This is why spacing doses 6-8 hours apart is critical: it allows partial receptor recovery before the next stimulus. Some users report efficacy loss after 4-6 weeks of constant use, suggesting adaptive downregulation occurs despite proper spacing.

This is why cycling is essential: 8-12 weeks of use followed by 4-8 weeks off allows complete receptor resensitization. Some advanced users implement pulse protocols: 3 weeks on (high potency), 1 week off (recovery), repeated 3-4 times before a full break. The science behind this is limited, but user reports suggest it maintains efficacy better than continuous dosing.

Peak-to-Trough Variation and Anabolic Effect

The short half-life means hexarelin users experience large GH swings: high peaks at 30 minutes post-injection, near-zero levels by 240 minutes. This creates a pulsatile GH profile similar to natural circadian rhythms (which is favorable) but with exaggerated amplitude. Some research suggests that pulsatile GH release (high peaks, low troughs) is more anabolic than sustained elevation.

However, the tradeoff: larger peaks also trigger proportionally larger cortisol and prolactin responses, increasing side effect severity. Users seeking maximum results accept this trade; those minimizing side effects might prefer the smoother profile of longer-acting peptides or lower hexarelin doses spaced throughout the day.

Storage and Stability Implications

Although hexarelin the peptide degrades quickly in the body, the lyophilized powder is stable for 2-3 years when stored at 2-8°C. Reconstituted hexarelin must be refrigerated and used within 14 days; after that, bacterial contamination risk rises significantly. The short in-vivo half-life doesn't affect shelf stability—only bacterial growth and chemical degradation matter for storage.

This means users can safely stockpile weeks or months of dried powder, but reconstituted solutions must be used on a rolling basis. Some users prefer to reconstitute multiple 1-2 mL aliquots from a single vial, using one aliquot at a time and discarding the remainder after 14 days.

Detection Window and Legal Considerations

Hexarelin's short half-life means it clears from circulation within 4-6 hours, making it undetectable by standard GH assays if tested more than 6 hours post-injection. This is partly why it's popular in competitive circles and why WADA banned it: the short window makes detection difficult. However, sophisticated GH secretagogue-specific tests can reveal exogenous GH release patterns for hours post-use.

For research users not competing, this is largely academic. But if testing is a concern, understanding the pharmacokinetic window (4-6 hours post-injection to clearance) is critical for avoiding detection or test failure.

FAQ: Hexarelin Half-Life & Timing

Q: Can I inject hexarelin twice in one day?
A: Yes, but space doses at least 6-8 hours apart. Closer spacing risks receptor desensitization and increased side effects without proportional GH gains.

Q: What's the best time to inject hexarelin?
A: 30-60 minutes before intense exercise or resistance training. For sleep benefits, inject 1-2 hours before bed on an empty stomach.

Q: Does hexarelin stay in your system all day?
A: No. The GH response peaks at ~30 minutes and returns to baseline by ~240 minutes (4 hours). You need multiple daily injections for consistent daily elevation.

Q: How does hexarelin's half-life compare to testosterone?
A: Hexarelin is much shorter (~30-240 min peak-to-baseline). Testosterone esters range from 2-3 days (propionate) to 8-12 days (undecanoate). This is why hexarelin dosing is far more frequent.

Q: If hexarelin's half-life is 30 minutes, why wait 6-8 hours between doses?
A: The half-life is how long it takes to drop to 50% concentration. Even at low levels, it can occupy receptors and reduce subsequent GH response. 6-8 hours allows near-complete clearance and receptor recovery.

Q: Can I take hexarelin once daily?
A: Technically yes, but results are suboptimal. Once-daily dosing (usually 200 mcg pre-workout) is used by budget-conscious users accepting lower efficacy in exchange for simpler compliance.
