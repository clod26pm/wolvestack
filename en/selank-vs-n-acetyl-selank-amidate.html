Selank is the original peptide: fast-acting (hours to days), orally bioavailable, lower cost. N-Acetyl Selank Amidate (NSA) is a modified version: more stable, potentially longer-lasting effects, higher cost. For anxiety relief, Selank is ideal; for sustained effects or oral preference, NSA may be superior. Both are safe and effective.

What Is Selank?

Selank is a heptapeptide (7 amino acids) derived from tuftsin, originally developed in Russia. It's been used clinically for 25+ years as an anxiolytic and immune-supporting peptide. The original formulation is immediately bioactive, produces fast symptom relief (3-7 days typical), can be given orally or via injection, and has extensive clinical safety data.

What Is N-Acetyl Selank Amidate (NSA)?

N-Acetyl Selank Amidate is a chemical modification of Selank: an acetyl group added to the amino terminus, and the carboxyl terminus modified to an amide. These modifications increase proteolytic stability (resistance to enzymes that break down peptides), extend serum half-life, and may improve oral bioavailability. NSA is a newer variant developed to optimize Selank's pharmacokinetics.

Bioavailability: Oral vs Injectable

Original Selank: good oral bioavailability due to special delivery mechanisms (possibly via caveolin-1 mediated endocytosis). Can be taken orally, though injection is more common. NSA: potentially superior oral bioavailability due to increased stability. Both can be injected; NSA may maintain activity longer in systemic circulation.

Speed of Action

Selank: onset 3-7 days typically; peak effects 2-4 weeks. Some users feel immediate anxiolytic effects (acute stress buffering) but sustained benefit takes days to weeks. NSA: potentially slower onset (extended pharmacokinetics) but longer duration of effect once established. For acute anxiety relief, Selank may have edge; for sustained benefit, NSA may be superior.

Duration of Effects

Selank: requires daily dosing for sustained benefit; benefits diminish within 1-3 days of discontinuation. NSA: potentially longer duration per dose; some anecdotal reports of benefits persisting 2-3 days after last dose. This may allow less frequent dosing with NSA, but formal data is lacking.

Stability and Shelf Life

Selank: stable at 2-8°C for 2-3 years. Sensitive to proteolytic enzymes (stomach acid, intestinal proteases). NSA: increased chemical stability due to modifications, potentially longer shelf life. Once reconstituted: Selank liquid degrades faster than NSA liquid (estimated). NSA may be better choice for travel or long-term storage.

Dosing Protocols

Selank: standard 250-500 mcg daily. Can be given as single dose or split (AM/PM). NSA: dosing less well-established; likely lower doses needed due to higher potency or longer duration. Possible 100-250 mcg daily range, but individual variation high. Conservative dosing recommended until more research available.

Side Effects: Comparison

Selank: well-studied side effect profile; very rare serious effects; mild transient effects (nausea, headache) in ~5-10%. NSA: minimal human safety data; side effect profile presumed similar but not formally established. Potential for novel effects (e.g., due to structural modifications) not yet documented. Proceeding cautiously warranted.

Cost-Benefit Analysis

Selank: less expensive (more widely available, established market), extensive research backing, proven clinical efficacy. NSA: more expensive (newer, less available), theoretical improvements (stability, duration) but limited human data. For cost-conscious users or those wanting proven efficacy: Selank. For those wanting optimization and willing to pay premium: NSA.

Research Evidence

Selank: 25+ years of Russian clinical research, dozens of peer-reviewed studies on efficacy and safety, documented use in clinical settings. NSA: minimal published research; mostly theoretical/chemical data. Very limited human trials or case reports. Selank has vastly stronger evidence base.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Selank if: you want proven efficacy, prefer lower cost, need fast anxiety relief, want extensive safety documentation. Choose NSA if: you want longest possible duration, prefer oral administration and suspect higher stability matters, are willing to risk unknown safety data for theoretical benefit, have budget flexibility. For first-time users: Selank is safer starting point.

Stacking Selank and NSA Together?

Theoretically possible but not recommended. Combining both = redundant effects without synergy, increased cost, and unknown interactions. If switching from Selank to NSA: stop Selank 2-3 days first, then start NSA. Do not combine in same protocol.
