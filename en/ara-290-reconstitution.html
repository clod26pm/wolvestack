ARA-290 arrives as freeze-dried powder requiring reconstitution with bacteriostatic 0.9% sodium chloride water. Mix by gentle swirling (never shake), achieving concentrations of 1-4 mg/mL depending on total volume. Proper reconstitution is critical: incorrect water choice or technique destroys the peptide. Reconstituted ARA-290 is stable for 4 weeks refrigerated when using bacteriostatic water.

Why ARA-290 Arrives as Powder and Must Be Reconstituted

ARA-290 is manufactured as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder because liquid peptide solutions degrade rapidly. The powder is stable at room temperature for 18-24 months. Lyophilization removes water while preserving peptide structure, dramatically extending shelf life. When you receive the powder, you must rehydrate it—reconstitute it—before injection.

Supplies Required for Reconstitution

  • ARA-290 lyophilized powder vial (typically 2-10 mg per vial)
  • Bacteriostatic 0.9% sodium chloride water (NOT normal saline, NOT distilled water)
  • 3 mL sterile syringe
  • 27-29 gauge sterile needle
  • Alcohol prep pad
  • Sharps container

Why Bacteriostatic Water Is Essential

Bacteriostatic water is 0.9% sodium chloride + 0.9% benzyl alcohol. The benzyl alcohol prevents bacterial/fungal contamination, allowing the reconstituted vial to be stored and used over 4 weeks. Normal saline lacks this preservative; reconstituting with saline creates infection risk if the vial isn't used immediately. Distilled water is hypotonic and damages peptide structure.

Critical: Always use bacteriostatic water specifically labeled for peptide reconstitution.

Step-by-Step Reconstitution

  1. Draw bacteriostatic water: Calculate desired final concentration (see below), then draw that volume into a sterile syringe using a clean needle.
  2. Sterilize vial: Wipe the powder vial rubber septum with an alcohol prep pad. Let it air-dry.
  3. Inject water slowly: Using a sterile needle, slowly inject the bacteriostatic water into the powder vial. Do NOT create pressure—go slowly to avoid foaming.
  4. Swirl gently: After injection, gently swirl the vial for 2-3 minutes. DO NOT SHAKE—shaking denatures peptides. The powder will dissolve, creating a clear solution.
  5. Wait 30 minutes: Allow the vial to sit at room temperature for complete hydration.
  6. Verify clarity: The solution should be completely clear. Cloudiness indicates contamination or incomplete dissolution—discard the vial if cloudy.

Concentration Calculation Formula

Formula: Final Concentration (mg/mL) = Powder Amount (mg) / Water Volume (mL)

Examples:

  • 2 mg powder + 1 mL water = 2 mg/mL
  • 2 mg powder + 2 mL water = 1 mg/mL
  • 4 mg powder + 1 mL water = 4 mg/mL
  • 10 mg powder + 5 mL water = 2 mg/mL

Common concentrations:

  • 1 mg/mL: easier to draw accurately, larger injection volumes (2-4 mL for typical doses)
  • 2 mg/mL: standard, balance of accuracy and volume
  • 4 mg/mL: smaller injection volumes, harder to measure precisely

Storage Guidelines After Reconstitution

  • Room temperature (18-25°C): 24 hours maximum. Risk of bacterial contamination increases. Use only if necessary for immediate injection.
  • Refrigerated (2-8°C): Up to 4 weeks with bacteriostatic water. Standard storage method. Store in the original vial on a shelf (not door—temperature fluctuates there). Keep away from light.
  • Frozen (-20°C): Up to 6 months, though repeated freeze-thaw cycles degrade peptide quality. Only freeze if you won't use within 4 weeks. Divide into single-use portions before freezing to minimize thaw cycles.

Best practice: Reconstitute only what you'll use within 1-2 weeks. Store refrigerated in the back of the fridge, away from temperature fluctuations. Label the vial with reconstitution date and expiration date (4 weeks from reconstitution).

Troubleshooting Common Reconstitution Problems

Problem: Powder won't dissolve after 30 minutes
Solution: The water may be too cold. Warm the vial gently in your palm (not in hot water—heat denatures peptides). Swirl gently for another 30 minutes. If still undissolved, peptide may be damaged or contaminated; discard.

Problem: Solution is cloudy after reconstitution
Solution: This usually indicates contamination or incomplete dissolution. Discard the vial. Ensure your next reconstitution uses sterile bacteriostatic water and proper sterile technique.

Problem: Can't draw from the vial (resistance)
Solution: (a) Pressure equalization: inject air into vial before drawing. (b) Use larger drawing needle (18 gauge). (c) Warm the vial slightly to reduce viscosity. (d) If still stuck, the reconstituted peptide may have solidified—discard.

Problem: Foam appears during injection into vial
Solution: Foam damages peptides. If it appears, stop injecting, let the vial sit for 1 hour undisturbed. Foam may settle. If significant foam remains, discard the batch.

Advanced Reconstitution Tips

  • Vial pre-warming: Slightly warming the powder vial (held in warm hands) before injecting water speeds dissolution
  • Syringe temperature: Drawing from a cold bacteriostatic water bottle increases viscosity. Allow water to reach room temperature before use.
  • Needle choice: Smaller-gauge drawing needles (27-29 gauge) work but are slower. 18-20 gauge is standard for faster, easier drawing.
  • Multiple vials: If reconstituting multiple vials, use separate sterile needles for each to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Labeling: Immediately label reconstituted vials with the date, concentration, and expiration date (4 weeks from reconstitution).

Proper Drawing Technique from Reconstituted Vial

  • Use a fresh 18-20 gauge drawing needle each time (larger needle makes drawing easier)
  • Equalize vial pressure: draw air into syringe equal to the volume you'll withdraw, inject into vial before drawing
  • Draw slowly to avoid creating foam (foam destroys peptide)
  • Replace drawing needle with injection needle (27-31 gauge) after drawing, before injection
  • Never reuse drawing needles

Signs of Contamination—Discard Immediately

  • Cloudiness or particles in solution
  • Unusual color (should be clear)
  • Foul odor
  • Growth visible in vial

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use normal saline instead of bacteriostatic water?
Not recommended. Normal saline lacks benzyl alcohol preservative. The reconstituted vial will be contaminated if not used within 24 hours. Bacteriostatic water is specifically designed for peptide reconstitution and allows 4-week storage.
What if I shake the vial instead of swirling?
Shaking creates foam and denatures the peptide, potentially destroying it. If you accidentally shake, let the vial sit undisturbed for 30-60 minutes. The foam may settle. If cloudiness remains, the peptide is likely damaged—discard.
How do I know the reconstituted solution is good?
After 30 minutes, the solution should be completely clear with no particles. If it's cloudy, precipitation has occurred—discard. Clear = good. Any cloudiness = discard.
Can I reconstitute the entire vial at once or should I do it in portions?
Reconstitute the entire vial at once. Once reconstituted, the vial can be stored refrigerated for 4 weeks. Reconstituting portions at a time wastes powder and increases contamination risk with each opening.
Should I refrigerate the powder before reconstitution?
No. Store lyophilized powder at room temperature in its original vial until ready to reconstitute. Refrigerating powder increases risk of moisture absorption, which damages it.
Can I freeze the reconstituted solution to extend storage?
Yes, freezing at -20°C extends storage to 6 months. However, each freeze-thaw cycle slightly degrades peptide quality. Minimize freeze-thaw cycles; if freezing, divide into portions so you thaw only what you need.
