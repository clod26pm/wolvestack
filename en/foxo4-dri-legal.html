FDA Approval Status: What Does Research-Use-Only Mean?

FOXO4-DRI is classified as a research chemical, not an approved pharmaceutical. The FDA does not approve research chemicals—they're used in laboratory and scientific studies by qualified researchers. Unlike pharmaceuticals (which undergo clinical trials and FDA review), research chemicals exist in a regulatory gray space: legal to manufacture and sell as research reagents, but not legal to market for human use. The label "research use only" is a legal disclaimer indicating the substance is not intended for human consumption and the manufacturer doesn't claim safety or efficacy for humans.

United States Legal Status: Research Chemicals vs. Drugs

In the US, FOXO4-DRI can be legally manufactured and sold as a research chemical under existing FDA frameworks. Vendors must label products "not for human consumption" to comply with FDA guidelines. The legal gray area: research chemicals aren't explicitly prohibited, but marketing them for human use or distributing with knowledge of human use creates legal liability. Manufacturers protect themselves with disclaimers. Individual users who purchase research chemicals for personal use operate in a gray zone—not explicitly illegal under most circumstances, but not explicitly endorsed by regulators.

Controlled Substance Classification

However, this doesn't make it legal—it simply avoids additional criminal penalties associated with controlled substance possession.
