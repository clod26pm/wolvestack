How to Evaluate Peptide Vendors: Quality and Safety Indicators

Legitimate vendors share characteristics: (1) clear "research use only" labeling (indicates understanding of regulatory requirements); (2) third-party testing results available (proof of purity/identity); (3) detailed synthesis information (batch numbers, manufacturing dates); (4) transparent pricing with no misleading marketing claims; (5) responsive customer service and return policies; (6) positive customer reviews documenting real products. Avoid: vendors making health claims ("cures," "treats," "FDA-approved"), vendors with unclear manufacturing information, vendors with numerous negative reviews documenting low-quality or counterfeit products, vendors operating from suspicious jurisdictions without transparent contact information.

Third-Party Testing: Why It Matters

Reputable vendors submit FOXO4-DRI to independent laboratories (ISO 17025 certified) for testing: HPLC/mass spectrometry confirms peptide identity, amino acid analysis verifies correct sequence, endotoxin testing ensures sterility, microbial testing confirms absence of contamination. Vendors should provide Certificate of Analysis (CoA) for each batch showing: peptide identity confirmed, purity ≥95%, endotoxin <0.5 EU/mg, microbial testing results. Request CoA before purchasing—legitimate vendors provide it. If vendors refuse to share testing data, the product quality is questionable.
