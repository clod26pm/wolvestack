Sermorelin reconstitution: add bacteriostatic water (typically 2 ml for 10 mg vials) to lyophilized powder, roll gently until dissolved (don't shake). Reconstituted sermorelin must be refrigerated (2-8°C) and is stable 3-7 days. Unopened, lyophilized powder is stable at room temperature 12+ months. Each reconstituted vial yields 5 mg/ml solution. Proper reconstitution is critical for stability, sterility, and accurate dosing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much bacteriostatic water should I use?

Standard ratio is 2 ml bacteriostatic water per 10 mg sermorelin powder, yielding 5 mg/ml concentration. Adjust ratio based on desired concentration.

Why use bacteriostatic water instead of sterile water?

Bacteriostatic water contains benzyl alcohol (antimicrobial), preventing bacterial growth in multi-dose vials. Sterile water lacks preservatives; use only for single-use syringes.

How long does reconstituted sermorelin last?

Refrigerated at 2-8°C, reconstituted sermorelin is stable 3-7 days. Some users report extended stability (10-14 days) with proper storage; don't rely on this.

Can I freeze sermorelin after reconstitution?

Freezing is not recommended; ice crystal formation may damage peptide structure. Refrigeration (2-8°C) is standard. Some sources claim freeze-thaw is acceptable; avoid if possible.

How do I know if my reconstituted sermorelin is degraded?

Appearance: cloudy, discolored, or particulate sermorelin is degraded. Smell: off-odors indicate contamination. Efficacy: reduced effects suggest degradation.

Should I shake or roll the vial when reconstituting?

Roll gently (don't shake). Vigorous shaking introduces air and denatures the peptide. Gentle rolling for 30-60 seconds is sufficient.
