Frequently Asked Questions

What is the key difference between BPC-157 and PRP?

PRP delivers concentrated growth factors harvested from your blood; BPC-157 stimulates your body's own growth factor production. PRP acts as external factor delivery; BPC-157 acts as internal signaling enhancer.

How quickly does each therapy work?

PRP typically shows results in 2-4 weeks; BPC-157 requires 4-8 weeks but shows continued improvement through 12+ weeks. PRP onset is faster; BPC-157 results compound longer.

Which is more cost-effective?

BPC-157 is substantially less expensive: $50-200 per cycle vs $800-3,000 per PRP injection. Most PRP protocols require 3-6 injections; BPC-157 can be run as a single cycle.

Can you combine BPC-157 with PRP?

Yes, and preliminary reports suggest synergy. PRP provides immediate growth factor boost; BPC-157 enhances endogenous mechanisms. Limited data but theoretically additive.

Which is better for tendons vs cartilage?

Both work for both tissues. PRP may have slight edge in cartilage degradation; BPC-157 may have slight edge in tendon repair. Difference is modest; responsiveness is highly individual.

Do insurance companies cover either?

Some PRP treatments are covered by insurance for specific diagnoses; most are out-of-pocket. BPC-157 is not FDA-approved and never covered. Cost burden entirely on patient.
