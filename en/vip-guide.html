What Is VIP and Why Does Dosing Matter?

VIP (Vasoactive Intestinal Peptide) is a Neuropeptide, vasodilator, anti-inflammatory. Endogenous 28-amino acid neuropeptide produced in gut, pancreas, brain, and neuroendocrine tissues. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for VIP has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended VIP Dosage?

The most widely used VIP dosage is 50-100 mcg per spray or 200 mcg daily inhaled, administered 4 times daily (nasal) or daily (inhaled) via intranasal spray or inhalation.

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of VIP is 2 minutes (extremely short), which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For VIP specifically, the ongoing continuous therapy cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Neuropeptide, vasodilator, anti-inflammatory compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

How Do You Reconstitute VIP?

VIP typically comes as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder. Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water — never saline or sterile water, as BAC water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol that prevents bacterial growth and extends shelf life to 4-6 weeks.

How Long Should a VIP Cycle Last?

Standard VIP cycles run ongoing continuous therapy. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Limited clinical data. Nasal irritation possible. Short half-life limits systemic toxicity. Well-tolerated in preliminary studies.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most VIP-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does VIP Dosing Change When Stacking?

Combines with inhaled corticosteroids or bronchodilators for synergistic airway relaxation in asthma/COPD.

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.

What Is the Bottom Line on VIP Dosing?

Start at 50, dose 4 times daily (nasal) or daily (inhaled), and run cycles of ongoing continuous therapy. Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.

Source matters — always use research-grade VIP from vendors with third-party COA testing. Underdosed or contaminated products are the most common reason for poor results.
