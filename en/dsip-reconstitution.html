DSIP powder is reconstituted by mixing with bacteriostatic water at calculated ratios to create injectable solution. A typical reconstitution uses 1mg DSIP powder with 1mL bacteriostatic water (1mg/mL concentration). The process requires sterile technique, accurate measurement, and proper storage at 2-8°C (refrigerated) or room temperature for 30+ days.

What Is DSIP Reconstitution?

Reconstitution is the process of converting lyophilized (freeze-dried) DSIP powder into an injectable liquid solution. DSIP is synthesized and shipped as stable powder because this form has extended shelf life and survives shipping without degradation. Once reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, DSIP becomes injectable but has reduced shelf life (30-90 days depending on storage conditions). Reconstitution requires precision to ensure accurate dosing and sterility to prevent infection.

What Supplies Do I Need?

Essential supplies: sterile DSIP powder (1-5mg vials are standard), bacteriostatic water (0.9% benzyl alcohol-preserved saline), sterile syringes (1mL or 3mL), sterile needles (27-29 gauge), alcohol pads, sterile vials for storage. Bacteriostatic water is critical—it contains benzyl alcohol as a preservative, which extends reconstituted solution stability to 30+ days. Do not use regular distilled water.

How Do I Calculate Concentration?

Concentration is expressed as mass per volume (mg/mL). The formula is: Concentration (mg/mL) = Total mg of DSIP ÷ Total mL of bacteriostatic water. Example: 1mg DSIP + 1mL bacteriostatic water = 1mg/mL concentration. For 0.5mg/mL, mix 1mg DSIP with 2mL water. Choose a concentration that yields comfortable injection volumes (0.1-1mL). Document the exact amounts used.

What Is the Step-by-Step Process?

First, gather supplies and sanitize your workspace with 70% isopropyl alcohol. Wash your hands thoroughly. Inspect the DSIP powder vial for any visible contamination—it should be white to off-white powder. Draw the calculated volume of bacteriostatic water into a sterile syringe using a sterile needle.

Swab the rubber septum of the DSIP vial with an alcohol pad, allowing it to air dry completely. Inject the bacteriostatic water slowly into the DSIP vial at an angle, allowing the water to run down the inside of the vial rather than striking the powder directly. Gently roll the vial between your hands for 30-60 seconds—do not shake vigorously. Once fully dissolved, the solution should appear clear with no visible particles.

Why Is Bacteriostatic Water Essential?

Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol, which inhibits bacterial growth and extends solution shelf life to 30-90 days. Regular distilled or sterile water lacks preservative; reconstituted DSIP in plain water degrades within 24-48 hours and supports rapid bacterial proliferation. Using non-bacteriostatic water introduces infection risk and reduces peptide stability dramatically.

How Should I Store Reconstituted DSIP?

Reconstituted DSIP is stable for 30+ days at room temperature (68-77°F) due to benzyl alcohol preservation. For extended stability (up to 90 days), refrigerate at 2-8°C (35-46°F). Do not freeze reconstituted solution. Store in the original vial with a clear label indicating reconstitution date, concentration, and expiration date. Keep away from direct sunlight.

What Mistakes Should I Avoid?

Common errors: (1) using non-bacteriostatic water; (2) shaking the vial vigorously, creating bubbles; (3) injecting water too forcefully, causing foaming; (4) failing to allow alcohol to evaporate; (5) using non-sterile supplies; (6) heating the vial. Slow, gentle rolling—not shaking—ensures complete dissolution without damage.

What If the Powder Won't Dissolve?

Complete dissolution typically takes 30-60 seconds of gentle rolling. If powder persists after this time, the vial may contain impurities or the powder may have been damaged. Never inject solution containing visible powder particles. If powder remains visibly undissolved after 2-3 minutes of gentle rolling, the product may be defective.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need special equipment?
A: No. Standard sterile syringes, needles, and vials are sufficient.

Q: Can I use tap water?
A: No. Only bacteriostatic water should be used. Tap water lacks preservative and will degrade DSIP within hours.

Q: What concentration should I choose?
A: Choose a concentration that yields comfortable injection volumes (0.1-1mL). Most users prefer 1mg/mL.

Q: How long can unopened powder be stored?
A: DSIP powder is stable 12-24 months at room temperature or longer if refrigerated.

Q: Can I share reconstituted DSIP?
A: Sharing is generally safe if stored properly, but each person should maintain their own vials.

Q: What should I do with leftover solution after 30 days?
A: Discard it. DSIP degradation increases after 30 days at room temperature.
