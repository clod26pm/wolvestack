LL-37 Frequently Asked Questions

LL-37 is a human antimicrobial peptide with multiple therapeutic applications: wound healing acceleration (30–60% faster), immune enhancement, biofilm disruption, skin rejuvenation, and infection control. It operates through multiple mechanisms including direct antimicrobial action, immune modulation, angiogenesis, and collagen synthesis. Works best as adjunctive therapy in combination with standard medical care.

What is LL-37?

LL-37 (human cathelicidin) is an endogenous antimicrobial peptide—a 37-amino acid protein produced by neutrophils, macrophages, and epithelial cells. It's the only human member of the cathelicidin family and has multiple biological roles: antimicrobial defense, immune modulation, tissue repair, and inflammation resolution. Research compound, not FDA-approved for therapeutic use.

How do I prepare LL-37 for injection?

LL-37 powder is reconstituted with bacteriostatic water or saline: Add water slowly to powder vial (target 1–2 mg/mL). Let dissolve gently at room temperature or 4°C for 5–10 minutes. Gently roll to mix—do not shake (causes aggregation). Store at 2–8°C, use within 7–14 days. Always use sterile technique.

What are the side effects of LL-37?

Mild side effects: transient localized redness/swelling at injection site, mild systemic inflammatory markers for 24–48 hours. Rare side effects: hemolysis at extremely high systemic doses (above 100 mcg/kg), immune overstimulation in autoimmune conditions. Dose-dependent cytotoxicity above 75 mcg/mL in vitro. Most users tolerate well at standard doses (50–100 mcg).

How long does LL-37 last in the body?

LL-37 has a short half-life due to rapid proteolytic degradation: systemic LL-37 persistence is measured in minutes to hours (estimated 15–30 minutes). Local LL-37 (wound, injection site) lasts longer, with therapeutic effects persisting days to weeks. This short half-life is the main limitation for systemic use but supports local/topical applications.

Can I take LL-37 orally?

Oral LL-37 has minimal bioavailability because it's destroyed by gastric and intestinal proteases. Not effective for systemic effects. Rectal/colonic application or encapsulation in protease-resistant formulations may have better absorption—research ongoing. Injectable or topical routes strongly preferred.

Does LL-37 interact with medications?

LL-37 has low risk of drug interactions since it's a peptide (not absorbed systemically at oral doses) and acts through immune mechanisms rather than metabolic pathways. However, use caution with immunosuppressive medications—they may reduce LL-37 efficacy. Consult healthcare provider if combining with prescription drugs.

How long before I see results from LL-37?

Timeline varies by application: Immune markers shift within 24–72 hours. Wound improvement visible within 1–2 weeks. Skin changes appear after 4–8 weeks. Full benefits often manifest over 4–12 weeks. Consistent application and appropriate dosing crucial for timely results.

Can I combine LL-37 with other peptides?

Yes. Common stacks: LL-37 + BPC-157 (wound healing), LL-37 + TB-500 (tissue repair), LL-37 + Ipamorelin (immune + growth hormone effects). Reduce individual doses when stacking. Offset cycles (rotate peptides) can provide continuous coverage. Always research peptide compatibility.
