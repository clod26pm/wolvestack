What Is NAD+ and Why Does Dosing Matter?

NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) is a Coenzyme, metabolite, cellular cofactor. Endogenous molecule synthesized from dietary tryptophan or nicotinamide (vitamin B3); exists in all living cells as essential cellular energy currency. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for NAD+ has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended NAD+ Dosage?

The most widely used NAD+ dosage is 250-1000 mg daily (NR/NMN precursors); 5-10 mg daily (NADH), administered once daily via oral (tablets, capsules, sublingual).

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of NAD+ is ~1 hour for NAD+ itself; precursors (NMN, NR) have variable half-lives, which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For NAD+ specifically, the continuous supplementation; no cycling required cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Coenzyme, metabolite, cellular cofactor compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

When Should You Dose NAD+?

With a half-life of ~1 hour for NAD+ itself; precursors (NMN, NR) have variable half-lives, timing your NAD+ administration affects peak blood levels. Most protocols call for once daily.

For peptides administered before bed (common with GH-related compounds), the goal is to amplify the natural nocturnal growth hormone pulse. For healing peptides, proximity to the injury site via local injection can improve outcomes.

How Long Should a NAD+ Cycle Last?

Standard NAD+ cycles run continuous supplementation; no cycling required. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

100+ animal studies demonstrate longevity benefits, mitochondrial function improvement, and metabolic health gains. Limited human clinical trials — mostly smaller pilot studies on NR and NMN supplementation showing successful NAD+ elevation. Large-scale human trials ongoing.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

NADH well-tolerated. High-dose nicotinamide (>3000 mg) can cause nausea, skin flushing, elevated liver enzymes. NMN and NR precursors show fewer side effects with no flushing or liver toxicity reported at standard doses.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most NAD+-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does NAD+ Dosing Change When Stacking?

Synergizes with resveratrol and quercetin (sirtuin activators). Pairs with MOTS-C and SS-31 for comprehensive mitochondrial/cellular energy optimization.

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.

What Is the Bottom Line on NAD+ Dosing?

Start at 250, dose once daily, and run cycles of continuous supplementation; no cycling required. Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.

Source matters — always use research-grade NAD+ from vendors with third-party COA testing. Underdosed or contaminated products are the most common reason for poor results.
