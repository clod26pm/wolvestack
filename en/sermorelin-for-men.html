Frequently Asked Questions

Does sermorelin increase testosterone?

Indirectly. GH and testosterone have reciprocal stimulation; elevated GH supports testosterone production. Sermorelin doesn't directly increase testosterone but optimizes its secretion.

Can sermorelin improve erectile function?

Yes. GH improves endothelial function (blood vessel health), supporting erectile function. Improved sleep and reduced fatigue also enhance sexual performance and libido.

Is sermorelin safe for prostate health?

Yes. Sermorelin doesn't increase DHT (like testosterone) and doesn't increase prostate cancer risk. Some evidence suggests GH support may improve BPH (enlarged prostate) outcomes.

What's the best male muscle-building protocol?

500-1000 mcg daily sermorelin + 4-5x/week strength training + 2+ g/kg protein + 7-9 hours sleep. Expect 4-6 lbs muscle gain over 12 weeks for trained males.

Should men stack sermorelin with testosterone?

Yes, synergistically. Testosterone and sermorelin amplify each other's effects. Monitor lipid panels and hematocrit when stacking; may require estrogen management.

Can older men benefit from sermorelin?

Especially. Men 50+ have profound GH decline; sermorelin shows largest relative benefits in this demographic. Dosing may need adjustment for age-related sensitivity.
