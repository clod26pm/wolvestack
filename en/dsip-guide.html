What Is DSIP and Why Does Dosing Matter?

DSIP (Delta-Sleep-Inducing Peptide) is a Neuropeptide sleep modulator. Natural neuropeptide isolated in 1974 from rabbit cerebral venous blood during induced sleep. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for DSIP has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended DSIP Dosage?

The most widely used DSIP dosage is 100-300 mcg, administered once daily in the evening via subcutaneous injection or intravenous.

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of DSIP is not established, which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For DSIP specifically, the 4-12 weeks cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Neuropeptide sleep modulator compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

When Should You Dose DSIP?

With a half-life of not established, timing your DSIP administration affects peak blood levels. Most protocols call for once daily in the evening.

How Do You Reconstitute DSIP?

DSIP typically comes as a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder. Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water — never saline or sterile water, as BAC water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol that prevents bacterial growth and extends shelf life to 4-6 weeks.

How Long Should a DSIP Cycle Last?

Standard DSIP cycles run 4-12 weeks. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

Double-blind trials showed 59% increase in total sleep time and higher sleep efficiency versus placebo in insomnia patients. However, short-term DSIP treatment was not identified as providing major therapeutic benefit for chronic insomnia — effects were modest and variable.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Minimal adverse effects in human studies. Rare mild headache or dizziness. Long-term safety profile not established.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most DSIP-related side effects are dose-dependent.

What Is the Bottom Line on DSIP Dosing?

Start at 100, dose once daily in the evening, and run cycles of 4-12 weeks. Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.
