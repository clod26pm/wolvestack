Most common side effects are mild jitteriness and slight appetite suppression, typically appearing in the first 1-2 weeks and resolving as tolerance develops. Rare side effects include anxiety elevation, sleep disruption (only with evening injection), and GI upset. Most side effects are dose-dependent and manageable via dose reduction or adjusted timing.

Jitteriness and Tremor

Most common side effect (first 1-2 weeks, higher doses >2 mg). Manifests as mild hand tremor, nervous energy, restlessness. Mechanism: sympathomimetic stimulation from increased norepinephrine/dopamine. Management: reduce dose 0.5 mg, ensure hydration, avoid caffeine, may resolve spontaneously in days as body adapts. Usually resolves by week 2.

Appetite Suppression

Common (mild, usually 2-4 hours post-injection). Users report slight reduction in hunger, early satiety. Mechanism: orexin itself suppresses appetite during arousal (evolutionary—active individuals don't need to eat). Not dangerous but can lead to insufficient caloric intake if severe. Management: ensure breakfast before injection, eat 3-4 meals regardless of hunger, track calorie intake.

Sleep Disruption and Insomnia

Not a side effect of proper morning/afternoon injection; indicates timing error. Evening injection (after 4 PM) suppresses sleep onset and fragments sleep architecture. Management: adjust timing to morning only. If any evening dose taken, allow 12-16 hours before sleep. Insomnia from orexin is entirely preventable with correct timing.

Anxiety and Nervousness

Rare (dose-dependent, higher doses >2.5 mg, sensitive individuals). Manifests as jittery feeling, worry, restlessness. Mechanism: excessive sympathetic stimulation. Management: reduce dose to 1-1.5 mg, ensure caloric intake, avoid other stimulants, ensure sleep adequate night before, consider morning rather than afternoon injection.

Gastrointestinal Effects

Rare: mild nausea, loose stools, or constipation (variable GI response to peptide or food intake changes). Usually mild and transient. Management: take with food, ensure hydration, take over-the-counter GI support if needed. Seek care if severe or persistent.

Cardiovascular Effects

Mild elevation in HR (5-15 bpm), BP (2-5 mmHg), respiratory rate (transient, peaks 2-4 hours). Not dangerous in healthy individuals. Dizziness rare, usually associated with excessive dose or rapid standing. Management: monitor vitals if cardiovascular risk factors present, reduce dose if excessive, consult provider if concerning symptoms.

Headaches

Rare: mild tension-type headaches (first 1-2 days, usually resolves). Mechanism: sympathomimetic effect on vascular tone or simply initial nervous system stimulation. Management: hydrate well, reduce dose, take on full stomach, may resolve spontaneously. Over-the-counter analgesics acceptable if needed.

Dose-Response Relationship for Side Effects

0.5-1 mg: minimal side effects, mild effects. 1-2 mg: low side effects (jitteriness in 10-20% first week). 2-3 mg: moderate side effects (jitteriness in 30-40%, mild appetite suppression common). 3-5 mg: significant side effects (anxiety, GI upset, sleep disruption if given late day). >5 mg: severe side effects, not recommended.

Timeline of Side Effect Resolution

Day 1-3: peak side effects if present (jitteriness, mild anxiety). Day 4-7: marked improvement as body adapts (50-75% reduction). Week 2-4: minimal remaining side effects (95% resolved). Some users have no side effects; others take longer. Consistency matters: irregular timing delays adaptation.

Individual Variability in Side Effects

Body weight: lighter individuals (especially women <120 lbs) experience stronger effects at equivalent doses; start with 0.5 mg. Caffeine sensitivity: sensitive individuals experience more jitteriness; reduce dose or avoid caffeine. Anxiety baseline: anxious individuals more likely to report anxiety elevation—start low. Age: older individuals may tolerate slightly more variation.

Managing Jitteriness Specifically

Jitteriness is dose-dependent and temporary. Reduce dose 0.5 mg, increase water intake (1-2L post-injection), avoid additional caffeine for 4-6 hours, eat adequate breakfast (fuel for nervous system), do moderate exercise 1-2 hours post-injection (burns excess sympathomimetic energy), ensure sleep adequate night before. Usually resolves by week 2 even without dose reduction.

When to Contact a Healthcare Provider

Persistent elevated BP (>150/100 sustained >30 min), severe chest pain/palpitations, severe anxiety unresponsive to dose reduction, signs of allergic reaction (rash, difficulty breathing), severe GI symptoms (vomiting, severe diarrhea), any concerning neurological symptoms. Most side effects are manageable; seek care if severe or atypical.
