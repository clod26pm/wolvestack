Frequently Asked Questions

Will CJC-1295 cause virilization?

No. Virilization requires androgens. CJC-1295 only elevates GH, which is naturally higher in women post-workout. No voice, hair, or clitoral changes.

Can I use with hormonal birth control?

Yes, no interactions. CJC-1295 doesn't affect estrogen or progesterone. Can use concurrently without concern.

How much muscle can women gain?

2-8 lbs lean mass over 12 weeks, typically. Women gain less muscle per unit time than men due to lower testosterone, but gains are real.

Is lower dosing necessary for women?

Helpful for minimizing side effects like increased appetite or water retention. 50-100 mcg vs 100-200 mcg for men is common but not mandatory.

Will this affect menstrual cycle?

Unlikely at therapeutic doses. GH itself doesn't suppress cycling. Some users report slightly lighter cycles due to improved metabolic state, not hormonal suppression.

Is CJC-1295 safe during pregnancy?

No. CJC-1295 not studied in pregnancy; unknown fetal effects. Discontinue if attempting to conceive. Not compatible with pregnancy/breastfeeding.
