AOD-9604 vs 5-Amino-1MQ

Differences, Benefits & Which Is Better

AOD-9604 and 5-Amino-1MQ are both popular research peptides that work through different mechanisms. AOD-9604 is a Lipolytic peptide, GH fragment analog focused on fat loss, while 5-Amino-1MQ is a Small molecule NNMT inhibitor targeting fat oxidation.

What Are AOD-9604 and 5-Amino-1MQ?

AOD-9604 (Fragment 177-191 of Human Growth Hormone (modified)) is a Lipolytic peptide, GH fragment analog. Synthetic analog of human GH fragment 177-191, engineered to selectively activate lipolysis without systemic GH effects. It is researched for fat loss, increased fat oxidation, improved body composition, joint health support, no IGF-1 elevation.

5-Amino-1MQ (5-Amino-1-Methyl-Quinolinium) is a Small molecule NNMT inhibitor. Synthetic compound designed to inhibit nicotinamide N-methyltransferase (NNMT) and boost NAD+ synthesis through the salvage pathway. It is researched for fat oxidation, metabolic flexibility, improved grip strength, increased endurance, reduced muscle recovery time, anti-aging effects.

While both are popular research peptides, they work through fundamentally different mechanisms and serve different primary purposes.

How Do AOD-9604 and 5-Amino-1MQ Work Differently?

AOD-9604 mechanism: Activates beta-3 adrenergic receptors in adipose tissue, triggering cAMP-mediated lipolysis and release of stored fat for energy. Suppresses lipogenesis (new fat formation) while promoting fat breakdown. Unlike full growth hormone, it does not raise IGF-1 or impact glucose metabolism, blood sugar, or insulin sensitivity.

5-Amino-1MQ mechanism: Selectively inhibits the NNMT enzyme, which normally degrades nicotinamide and prevents NAD+ synthesis. By blocking NNMT, it frees nicotinamide for NAD+ production via the salvage pathway and activates SIRT1 longevity pathways. Also stimulates AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK) in adipocytes and myocytes, promoting mitochondrial biogenesis and fat oxidation.

These distinct mechanisms are why the two peptides are often used for different research goals — or combined to target multiple pathways.

How Do the Dosing Protocols Compare?

AOD-9604: 250-1000 mcg daily administered once daily, ideally morning fasted via subcutaneous injection. Half-life: not established. Cycle: 12-24 weeks.

5-Amino-1MQ: 150-500 mcg subcutaneous, 50-100 mg oral administered once to twice daily via subcutaneous injection or oral. Half-life: not established in humans. Cycle: 4-12 weeks.

How Do the Benefits Compare?

AOD-9604 benefits: fat loss, increased fat oxidation, improved body composition, joint health support, no IGF-1 elevation.

5-Amino-1MQ benefits: fat oxidation, metabolic flexibility, improved grip strength, increased endurance, reduced muscle recovery time, anti-aging effects.

How Do the Side Effects Compare?

AOD-9604: Well-tolerated across 900+ participants in clinical trials. Minimal adverse effects — potential mild headache, dizziness, or injection site reactions. One of the safest peptides in terms of documented human tolerability.

5-Amino-1MQ: Good tolerability in preclinical data. No major adverse events reported. Limited human safety data — long-term effects in humans unknown.
