Selank powder should be stored at 2-8°C (refrigerator) in light-protected containers with desiccant packets. Reconstituted Selank solution requires freezer storage (-18°C or colder) and has a 2-4 week stability window in freezer, 3-5 days in refrigerator. Improper storage leads to peptide degradation, reduced efficacy, and potential contamination.

Selank Powder: Dry Storage Requirements

Unopened Selank powder is most stable under cool, dry, dark conditions. Optimal storage: 2-8°C (refrigerator temperature), in airtight container, with desiccant packets to absorb moisture. Original packaging from manufacturers usually meets these standards. Shelf life: 2-3 years at 2-8°C under proper conditions; 6-12 months at room temperature (not recommended).

Light Protection and Oxidation

Peptides are sensitive to UV light and oxygen exposure. Store Selank in amber or opaque containers, NOT clear plastic. Light exposure causes photodegradation and oxidation, reducing potency over weeks to months. Room light exposure: avoid direct sunlight, keep in dark cabinet or drawer. Even indirect ambient light degrades peptides over extended periods.

Moisture and Desiccant Management

Atmospheric moisture is peptide's enemy. Humidity causes hydration of powder, promoting bacterial growth and peptide degradation. Always store with desiccant packets (silica gel). Replace desiccants annually or if they appear saturated. Keep caps/containers closed immediately after use. Do NOT store in bathrooms (high humidity) or near water sources.

Room Temperature Storage (Not Ideal, But Possible)

If refrigeration unavailable, room temperature (18-25°C) is tolerable for SHORT periods (weeks to months). Stability degrades significantly compared to refrigeration. Do NOT expose to heat sources, direct sunlight, or humidity. Shelf life at room temp: ~6 months maximum. For longer storage, refrigeration is essential.

Freezer Storage for Extended Shelf Life

Deep freezer storage (-18°C or colder) extends Selank powder shelf life to 3-5 years. This is useful for bulk purchases or long-term stock. Allow powder to warm to room temperature BEFORE opening (to prevent condensation). Once opened, return to freezer immediately; do not leave exposed.

Reconstituted Selank: Liquid Storage

Once reconstituted (dissolved in water or saline), Selank becomes fragile. Liquid Selank is viable for: 2-4 weeks in freezer (-18°C or colder), 3-5 days in refrigerator (2-8°C), <24 hours at room temperature. Freezer is preferred for long-term stability. For shorter-term use (daily), refrigerator is acceptable; toss after 5 days.

Freeze-Thaw Cycles and Degradation

Repeated freeze-thaw cycles degrade peptides. Each freeze-thaw: ice crystal formation damages peptide structure. Minimize cycles by: portioning liquid into individual-use vials before freezing (use one vial, don't repeatedly freeze-thaw same bottle). Thaw at refrigerator temperature (slow), not room temperature (fast degradation during thaw). Once thawed, do NOT refreeze.

Reconstitution Best Practices

Use bacteriostatic water or normal saline (not plain water—contamination risk). Reconstitute at cool temperature (not warm), as heat accelerates degradation. Inject solvent slowly to avoid foaming and peptide denaturation. Allow dissolved peptide to settle for 10-15 minutes before use. Store immediately at appropriate temperature.

Visual Inspection: Signs of Degradation

Discard Selank if powder shows: discoloration (should be white to off-white), clumping (indicates moisture intrusion), or any visible contamination. Discard liquid if it shows: cloudiness (bacterial growth), discoloration, or particle precipitation (structural breakdown). Smell: fresh Selank has minimal odor; sour or off-smell indicates bacterial growth.

Transportation and Travel Storage

Short-term transport (hours to 1-2 days): ice pack in insulated container maintains 2-8°C during travel. Longer travel: portable cooler with reusable ice packs. Avoid exposure to vehicle heat or direct sunlight. For flights: pack in carry-on (not checked luggage, which may reach extreme temperatures). Once at destination, transfer to proper storage immediately.

Common Storage Mistakes to Avoid

1) Storing in clear bottles (light exposure). 2) Storing in warm locations like medicine cabinets or windowsills. 3) Repeated opening of container (oxygen/moisture exposure). 4) No desiccant packets (humidity accumulates). 5) Storing reconstituted peptide at room temperature for days. 6) Freeze-thaw cycling (freezing, thawing, refreezing same vial).
