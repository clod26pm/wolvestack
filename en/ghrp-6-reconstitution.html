Reconstitution Basics

GHRP-6 arrives as lyophilized powder requiring reconstitution before use. Reconstitution dissolves the powder into a liquid solution suitable for injection. Standard approach: draw sterile water/bacteriostatic water into syringe, inject into GHRP-6 vial, gently swirl (don't shake) until completely dissolved. Shaking introduces air bubbles and denatures protein; gentle swirling achieves dissolution without damage.

Diluent Selection

Sterile water: simplest option, acceptable for short-term storage (7-10 days). Bacteriostatic water: contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative, extends storage stability to 30 days refrigerated. Normal saline (0.9% NaCl): acceptable alternative, similar storage duration as bacteriostatic water. Most users prefer bacteriostatic water for extended storage and convenience.

Concentration Optimization

Standard concentrations: 5 mcg/mL (easier for micro-dosing), 10 mcg/mL (standard commercial), or 20 mcg/mL (higher concentration for users with large daily volumes). Example: 100 mcg dose using 10 mcg/mL solution requires 10 mL injection. Users preferring smaller injection volumes select 20 mcg/mL. Typical reconstitution dissolves entire vial (50-100 mcg of GHRP-6) into 5-10 mL diluent.

Reconstitution Technique

Draw desired volume of diluent into sterile syringe. Inject into GHRP-6 vial slowly along the inner wall to minimize pressure changes. Let stand 1-2 minutes before gently swirling to dissolve. Do NOT shake vigorously (introduces air, denatures protein). Gentle swirling achieves dissolution within 2-3 minutes. Solution should be clear and colorless. Cloudiness indicates contamination or improper reconstitution.

Storage After Reconstitution

Refrigerate reconstituted solution at 2-8°C immediately after preparation. Do NOT freeze. Potency degrades approximately 5-10% daily at room temperature, losing 50% activity within 7-10 days. At 2-8°C, potency remains 90%+ for 30 days. Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles (each cycle reduces potency 5-10%). Solution stored beyond 30 days requires dose adjustment based on potency loss.

Stability Factors

Sterile water: 7-10 days at 2-8°C. Bacteriostatic water: 30 days at 2-8°C. Normal saline: 30 days at 2-8°C. Light exposure: minimal impact on short timeframes, but store in dark/opaque container for extended storage. Temperature fluctuations: minimize by storing in consistent location (refrigerator shelf, not door). Air exposure: minimize by using appropriate syringe techniques and avoiding unnecessary opening of vial.

FAQ: Reconstitution

Can I use tap water?

Never. Tap water contains microorganisms and minerals that contaminate solution and may cause infections. Always use sterile water (0.9% benzyl alcohol bacteriostatic water or normal saline).

How do I know if reconstitution failed?

Cloudy or discolored solution indicates contamination or improper reconstitution. Clear, colorless solution is normal. Anything other than clear indicates failed reconstitution—discard and try again.

Can I freeze reconstituted GHRP-6?

Not recommended. Freezing denatures protein and reduces potency. Refrigerate at 2-8°C instead. If frozen accidentally, thaw slowly in refrigerator (don't use heat) and reassess solution clarity before using.

How long can I store reconstituted solution?

Bacteriostatic water: 30 days at 2-8°C. Sterile water: 7-10 days. Solution beyond 30 days requires dose adjustment (increase by 10-20% per additional week) due to potency loss.
