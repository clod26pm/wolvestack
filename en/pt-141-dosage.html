What Is PT-141 and Why Does Dosing Matter?

PT-141 (Bremelanotide) is a Melanocortin receptor agonist. Synthetic α-MSH analog targeting MC1R and MC4R for sexual desire modulation. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for PT-141 has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Nausea (40% incidence, especially first injection), flushing (20%), injection site reactions (13%), headache (11%). Transient blood pressure increases (2-3 mmHg average). Contraindicated in uncontrolled hypertension.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most PT-141-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does PT-141 Dosing Change When Stacking?
