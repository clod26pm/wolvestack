KPV lyophilized powder requires freezer storage (-20°C or colder). Reconstituted solution: refrigerate 2-8°C. Unopened vials stable for 24+ months if stored correctly. Avoid freeze-thaw cycles, temperature fluctuations, light exposure.

Freezer Storage (Lyophilized)

Optimal: -20°C freezer or colder. Unopened vials stored correctly remain stable 24+ months. Temperature stability critical—avoid temperature fluctuations or repeated freeze-thaw cycles.

Storage location: freezer (not ice maker compartment—too variable). Ensure freezer maintains consistent -20°C or colder.

Opening frequency: minimize. Each opening introduces potential moisture and temperature fluctuation. Use single-dose or fresh multi-dose vials rather than repeatedly opening same vial.

Refrigerator Storage (Reconstituted)

Reconstituted peptide (non-preserved, sterile water): use within 24 hours, refrigerate 2-8°C.

Reconstituted peptide (bacteriostatic water preserved): usable 1-4 weeks depending on preservative formulation, refrigerate 2-8°C.

Don't freeze reconstituted solutions—freeze-thaw damages peptide.

Verify expiration on reconstitution—discard expired solutions.

Preventing Degradation

Freeze-thaw cycles degrade peptides. Avoid repeated freezing/thawing of same vial.

Temperature stability essential—excursions above -20°C (or above 2-8°C for refrigerated) accelerate degradation. Avoid room temperature storage.

Light exposure: minimal. Store in opaque container if extended storage.

Moisture: minimize. Keep vials sealed until use.

Transport and Travel

For short trips (24-48 hours): insulated cooler with gel ice packs maintains cold chain. Verify cooler maintains 2-8°C.

For longer trips: portable medication cooler (travel-approved, maintains temperature). Alternatively, have supply shipped to destination.

Avoid leaving in warm cars, direct sunlight, or exposed to temperature extremes.

Signs of Degradation

Cloudiness or precipitation in lyophilized vial: powder might have absorbed moisture. Discard.

Discoloration: white powder should remain white. Yellow or brown discoloration suggests degradation. Discard.

Cloudiness in reconstituted solution: contamination or degradation. Discard.

Odor: any unusual odor suggests degradation. Discard.

When in doubt, discard and obtain fresh supply. Degraded peptides might lose potency or become unsafe.

Documentation and Tracking

Label vials with: contents, concentration, date received, expiration date, storage location. This prevents confusion and accidental use of expired product.

Maintain inventory: track quantity, usage, reorder timing. Prevents running out unexpectedly.

Storage log: optional but useful for tracking temperature/access, ensuring proper rotation.

Disposal of Expired or Degraded Product

Lyophilized peptides: can typically be disposed with regular trash (inert powder). Check with pharmacy for specific guidance.

Reconstituted solutions: should be disposed down sink with running water (dilutes, breaks down). Some jurisdictions require pharmacy disposal—verify local regulations.

Needles/syringes: sharps containers for safe disposal. Don't dispose in regular trash.

FAQ

How long is KPV stable?

Unopened powder: 24+ months at -20°C. Reconstituted: 24 hours (non-preserved) to weeks (preserved).

Can I store in regular freezer?

Yes, if maintains -20°C. Avoid ice maker compartments (temperature fluctuates). Use dedicated freezer section.

What if powder looks discolored?

Discard. Yellow/brown discoloration indicates degradation. White powder is normal.

Can I refreeze after thawing?

Not recommended. Freeze-thaw damages peptides. Thaw only what you'll use.

How do I transport KPV?

Insulated cooler with gel packs maintains cold chain. Verify 2-8°C maintained during transport.

How do I dispose of expired KPV?

Powder: verify local regulations (often regular trash). Solution: down drain with running water or pharmacy disposal.
