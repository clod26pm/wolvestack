GHRP-6 requires subcutaneous injection with a 28-31 gauge insulin syringe. Inject 100-300 mcg on empty stomach 2-3 times daily, 4-6 hours apart. Rotate injection sites (abdomen, thigh, arm) to prevent lipohypertrophy. Use 45-90 degree needle insertion at 1/4-1/2 inch depth with aspiration before injecting.

Injection Sites

Optimal sites: abdomen (1-2 inches lateral to navel), outer thigh, upper arm lateral surface, or hip/buttock. Abdominal injections absorb fastest. Rotate sites to prevent lipohypertrophy (fatty nodule development) and lipoatrophy. Maintain a rotation calendar.

Aseptic Technique

Wash hands 20+ seconds. Gather: reconstituted GHRP-6 vial, sterile insulin syringe (1 mL, 28-31 gauge), sterile needle, alcohol prep pads, gauze, sharps container. Inspect peptide for particulates or discoloration. Cleanse injection site with alcohol for 15-20 seconds using firm circular motions. Allow to air-dry 30 seconds.

Needle Insertion

Pinch skin to create 1/2 inch fold. Insert needle at 45-90 degree angle into subcutaneous fat layer. Insert 1/4 to 1/2 inch depending on body composition. Aspirate before injection to check for blood vessel penetration; if blood appears, withdraw and select different site.

Injection Procedure

Steadily depress plunger over 3-5 seconds. Rapid injection increases discomfort; slow injection reduces trauma. Withdraw needle and apply gentle pressure with gauze for 10-15 seconds to prevent leakage. Massage area gently 20-30 seconds to distribute peptide. Dispose of needle/syringe in sharps container; never recap needles.

Syringe Selection

Insulin syringes (1 mL, 100 IU) are gold standard: small gauge (28-31) and sharp beveled needles for accurate micro-dosing. Tuberculin syringes acceptable but larger gauge causes more discomfort. Use sterile single-use only—never reuse. Dullness after single injection increases next-use discomfort and infection risk.

Pain Management

GHRP-6 injections typically well-tolerated with minimal discomfort. Small-gauge needles (28-31) reduce trauma versus larger gauges. Topical anesthetic (EMLA cream applied 30 minutes prior) reduces discomfort for pain-sensitive users. Allow solution to warm to room temperature (10-15 minutes) to reduce discomfort versus cold injection.

Injection Timing and Frequency

GHRP-6 requires 2-3 daily injections due to short 2.5-hour elimination half-life. Standard protocol: 150 mcg at 6-7 AM, 200 mcg at 12-1 PM, 150 mcg at 6-7 PM on empty stomach spaced 4-6 hours apart. Empty stomach timing (30-60 minutes before/after) optimizes GH response by avoiding meal-induced glucose spikes that suppress GH. Evening injection should occur 3-4 hours before bedtime to avoid interfering with natural sleep-associated GH secretion.

FAQ: Injection Technique

Blood appears when aspirating?

Withdraw immediately and select different site. This indicates blood vessel penetration. Always aspirate before injecting.

Can I reuse needles?

Not recommended. Needles dull after single injection, increasing discomfort and infection risk. Single-use sterile needles cost minimal and are essential.

Is intramuscular injection more effective?

No. Intramuscular produces faster absorption but similar or reduced GH response. Causes more tissue trauma and discomfort. Subcutaneous is the standard.

How deep should needle go?

Target 1/4 to 1/2 inch depth depending on body composition, reaching subcutaneous fat layer without penetrating muscle beneath.
