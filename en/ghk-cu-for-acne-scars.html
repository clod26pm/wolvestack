How GHK-Cu Helps Acne Scars

Acne scars are depressed (lost collagen volume). GHK-Cu directly stimulates collagen synthesis in scarred areas, filling depressions and improving scar appearance. Combined with microneedling (which triggers additional collagen response), results are dramatic: scars become less visible, skin texture improves, skin appears more uniform.

Scar Types and GHK-Cu Effectiveness

Rolling Scars: Gentle, broad depressions. GHK-Cu + microneedling very effective. 4-6 treatments produce obvious improvement in 70-80% of users.

Boxcar Scars: Angular, uniform-depth scars. Moderate improvement with GHK-Cu + microneedling. May require subcision or laser for complete resolution.

Icepick Scars: Deep, narrow punctate scars. Limited improvement with GHK-Cu alone. Better combined with deeper microneedling (1.5-2.0mm) or other procedures.

Atrophic Scars: General term for depressed scars. GHK-Cu is effective for mild-moderate atrophic scars.

GHK-Cu + Microneedling Protocol for Scars

Microneedling depth: 1.0-1.5mm (sufficient to trigger collagen response without excessive trauma). Frequency: every 4-6 weeks. GHK-Cu application: apply 24-48 hours post-microneedling, daily for 4 weeks until next microneedling. Repeat 4-6 cycles (16-24 weeks total) for obvious scar improvement. Results: rolling scars dramatically improve; boxcar/icepick scars show moderate improvement.

Combined Approach for Stubborn Scars

Severe scars may need combined therapy: GHK-Cu + microneedling + subcision (for deep/severe scars) or laser resurfacing (for extensive scarring). Consulting a dermatologist for severe acne scarring is recommended before committing to GHK-Cu alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long until acne scars improve?

Weeks 4-8: initial improvement. Weeks 8-16: obvious scar elevation visible. Peak results by 4-6 microneedling cycles (16-24 weeks total).

Can GHK-Cu treat active acne while improving scars?

Yes—GHK-Cu's anti-inflammatory effects help active acne while collagen stimulation improves scars. This makes it ideal for acne-prone skin with existing scarring.

Will my acne scars completely disappear?

Unlikely to disappear completely, but visible improvement of 30-60% is common. Complete resolution requires more aggressive treatments (subcision, laser, chemical peels).

Is GHK-Cu better than laser for scars?

Different mechanisms. Laser: faster results, more expensive, more downtime, more aggressive. GHK-Cu: slower, gentler, more affordable, longer timeline. Choice depends on scar severity and timeline.
