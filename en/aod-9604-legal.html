Global Legal Status: What You Need to Know

AOD-9604's legal status varies significantly by country. It is not FDA-approved in the United States, never completed clinical development for therapeutic approval, and exists in a regulatory gray area in most jurisdictions.

United States: AOD-9604 is not FDA-approved and is classified as a research chemical. It is legal to purchase from research supply vendors labeled "for research use only." However, using it for personal fat loss is off-label and not medically endorsed. The FDA does not actively enforce against research peptide possession for personal use (unlike illicit drugs), but clinical use without FDA approval is illegal. Therapeutic administration by healthcare providers would violate FDA regulations.

European Union: Status varies by member state. Some EU countries (UK post-Brexit, Germany, Netherlands) allow research chemical sales with "not for human consumption" disclaimers. Others have stricter regulations. Verify with your specific country's health authority before purchasing.

Canada: AOD-9604 is not Health Canada-approved. Research chemical sales exist in a legal gray area. Clinical use is not permitted without approval. Personal possession for research purposes is generally not prosecuted, but therapeutic use is not medically endorsed.

Australia: Unique status—AOD-9604 was approved by the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) under the brand name Metabol for investigational use. However, development was discontinued, and it's not currently available through standard pharmaceutical channels. It exists as a research chemical in Australia similar to other countries.

Key caveat: Laws change rapidly. Always verify current legal status in your jurisdiction before purchasing or using AOD-9604. Consult a lawyer if uncertain about local regulations.

WADA Banned Status: Athletic and Competitive Implications

The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) lists AOD-9604 as a prohibited substance in sport, classified under "Hormone and Metabolic Modulators." This has critical implications for athletes.

WADA-tested sports include: Olympic athletics, professional cycling, swimming, rowing, weight lifting, most professional team sports (NFL, NBA, soccer leagues in many countries), MMA/combat sports, and many others. Comprehensive list at wada-ama.org.

Consequences of testing positive: Testing positive for AOD-9604 in WADA-tested sports results in disqualification from competition, bans ranging from 1-4 years depending on sport and circumstances, loss of medals/titles, and potential criminal charges in some jurisdictions. The consequences are severe and permanent to athletic careers.

Detection window: AOD-9604 clears from circulation in 90 minutes due to its short half-life. However, metabolites may be detectable in urine for 24-48 hours post-injection via sophisticated testing. WADA testing has evolved; assume any use in the weeks before competition carries detectable risk.

Non-WADA sports: Body building competitions, CrossFit competitions (which don't use WADA testing), and many recreational sports don't test for AOD-9604. Athletes in these sports can use AOD-9604 legally (assuming it's legal in their jurisdiction), though sport-specific organizations may have separate rules. Always verify with your sport's governing body.

Practical clarity: If you're a competitive athlete in any WADA-tested sport, AOD-9604 is off-limits. The legal, professional, and reputational consequences are not worth the modest fat-loss benefit. Non-tested sports are more permissive.

FDA Approval History and Clinical Development Timeline

AOD-9604 underwent clinical trials but never achieved FDA approval. Understanding this history clarifies its regulatory status.

1990s-early 2000s: Monash University developed AOD-9604 and conducted Phase 1 and Phase 2 clinical trials in approximately 900 overweight human participants. Results demonstrated safety and efficacy. The compound showed promise for weight management and improved body composition.

Early 2000s: Development was licensed to Australian pharmaceutical company Metabol, which pursued commercialization. The TGA in Australia approved it under the brand name Metabol, marking the only governmental approval of AOD-9604 for investigational therapeutic use.

2000s-2010s: Metabol applied for FDA approval in the United States. However, the FDA requested additional clinical data, particularly regarding long-term safety and efficacy in larger populations. Development was discontinued, likely due to commercial viability concerns (development costs, competitive landscape with emerging GLP-1 agonists, regulatory burden).

Post-2010: AOD-9604 has not been pursued for FDA approval. It remains available as a research chemical from research supply vendors, used primarily by the peptide research community. No major pharmaceutical company has revived the development program.

Current status: AOD-9604 will likely never be FDA-approved. The development window has closed—GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) have captured the pharmaceutical fat-loss market with superior efficacy, and AOD-9604 lacks the commercial incentive for modern approval pathways.
