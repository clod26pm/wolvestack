What's the difference between legal and FDA-approved?

Legal and FDA-approved are different. Pinealon is legal (not prohibited) but not approved for human use. FDA approval involves rigorous clinical trials establishing safety and efficacy. Lack of FDA approval doesn't mean a compound is dangerous—it means its safety and efficacy in humans are not formally established. Many research chemicals are legal but unapproved. Understanding this distinction is important for informed decision-making.

Can I face legal consequences for researching Pinealon?

In most jurisdictions, purchasing and using research chemicals for personal research carries no legal consequences provided they're not controlled substances.
