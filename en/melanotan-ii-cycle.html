Standard Melanotan II cycles last 8-12 weeks with loading phase (0.5-1 mg daily for 2 weeks) followed by maintenance (0.2-0.5 mg daily) for 4-8 weeks. Off-cycle breaks last 4-8 weeks, allowing melanocyte signaling to normalize. Year-round continuous protocols exist but offer no additional benefit and increase cumulative side effects.

Loading Phase: High Dose, Fast Saturation (Days 1-14)

Loading protocol: 0.5-1 mg daily for 7-14 days until visible tanning begins. Some users inject 2x daily (0.5 mg each) for faster saturation, though this increases side effects proportionally. Loading creates rapid plasma peptide accumulation, saturating melanocyte MC1R receptors. By day 7-10, visible tanning appears. Loading phase goals: establish baseline melanin production and achieve visible results quickly. Most users aim to see obvious darkening by day 10-14, then transition to maintenance.

Maintenance Phase: Steady-State Dosing (Weeks 3-8)

Reduce dose to 0.2-0.5 mg daily (or every other day) to maintain tanning while minimizing cumulative side effects. Maintenance dosing establishes equilibrium where melanin production rate matches melanin clearance rate. Lower maintenance doses reduce nausea, hunger, and sexual effects compared to continued loading. The goal is to sustain tanning intensity without proportional side effect accumulation. Most users prefer 0.3-0.5 mg daily as optimal balance.

Peak Phase: Maximum Tanning Intensity (Weeks 8-12)

Weeks 8-12 represent maximum tanning achieved and maintained. Further dosing produces minimal additional darkening. Side effect tolerance typically develops despite continued peptide exposure. Most users continue maintenance dosing through week 8-12 then discontinue for off-cycle break. Some aggressive users extend cycles to week 12-16, but benefit diminishes after week 8. Conservative cycling (8-10 weeks) often yields better outcomes than extended protocols.

Off-Cycle Protocol: Duration and Recovery Timeline

Off-cycle breaks last 4-8 weeks (typically 4-6 weeks). Melanocyte signaling normalizes, sexual/appetite side effects subside, pituitary sensitivity (if any) recovers. Off-cycle length roughly equals on-cycle length for balanced approach. Some users run aggressive cycling (12 weeks on, 2 weeks off) but benefit/side effect ratio is less favorable than conservative cycling. Most experienced users advocate 8-12 week cycles with 4-6 week breaks for optimal results and safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the most common loading dose?

0.5-1 mg daily for 7-14 days until visible tanning. Some users inject 2x daily but this increases side effects proportionally without major benefit.

How long is the typical maintenance phase?

4-8 weeks at 0.2-0.5 mg daily. Reduced from loading dose to minimize side effects while maintaining results.

Do I need to take breaks between cycles?

Recommended. 4-8 week breaks every 12 weeks allow melanocyte signaling normalization and side effect resolution.

Can I run Melanotan II year-round?

Yes at low doses (0.1-0.2 mg daily), but cycled use shows better side effect profile with similar tanning results.

What happens when I stop injecting?

Tanning plateaus immediately. Fade begins 2-4 weeks post-cessation. Complete return to baseline: 8-12 weeks.

How many cycles per year is safe?

2-3 cycles (16-24 weeks use + 8-12 weeks off) considered safe based on user reports and experience.

How Does Individual Variation Affect Melanotan Outcomes?

Individual variation in response to Melanotan is one of the most important but least discussed aspects of peptide research. Genetic polymorphisms, baseline health status, age, sex, body composition, and concurrent medications all influence how an individual responds to any given protocol. This variability explains why two people following identical protocols can experience markedly different outcomes, and underscores the importance of personalized approach rather than one-size-fits-all recommendations.

Metabolic factors play a particularly significant role in determining individual response. Liver function, kidney clearance rates, plasma protein binding capacity, and enzyme activity levels all affect how quickly Melanotan is processed and eliminated from the body. Individuals with faster metabolism may require higher doses or more frequent administration to achieve the same tissue concentrations as those with slower metabolic rates.

Age-related physiological changes can also significantly impact Melanotan outcomes. Older individuals may experience enhanced benefits in some domains due to age-related decline in endogenous peptide production, while simultaneously facing increased sensitivity to certain side effects. Starting with conservative doses and titrating gradually is particularly important for older researchers who may have reduced clearance capacity and altered receptor sensitivity.
