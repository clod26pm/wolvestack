Melanotan II for Beginners

Complete Starter Guide

📖 10 min read 🔬 Research & Education ⚠️ Not medical advice ✅ Last reviewed: April 2026

Beginners should start with a 0.1-0.25 mg test dose to assess tolerance, followed by 0.25-0.35 mg every other day during a 2-3 week loading phase. Inject subcutaneously into abdomen or thigh, combine with sun exposure for best results, expect visible tanning within 7-14 days, and manage nausea with food, hydration, and anti-nausea supplements.

What Should Beginners Know Before Starting Melanotan II?

Melanotan II is a powerful compound requiring careful dosing and realistic expectations. Beginners must understand: the compound is unapproved for human use, side effects can be unpredictable, melanoma risk (however theoretical) exists for sensitive individuals, and sustained use requires commitment to skin monitoring. Never begin without researching legal status in your jurisdiction and establishing a dermatology baseline.

Why Start with a Test Dose?

A test dose (0.1-0.25 mg) reveals individual tolerance before committing to a loading phase. Some people are extremely sensitive and experience severe nausea even at low doses; others tolerate standard doses without issue. Inject the test dose, wait 24-48 hours, document side effects and any tanning response, then decide whether to proceed to loading phase dosing.

What Is the Beginner Loading Phase?

Load 0.25-0.35 mg every other day (e.g., Mon-Wed-Fri) for 2-3 weeks. Most people see visible darkening by day 7-10 of loading. Expect nausea, flushing, and possibly involuntary erections during the first 5-7 days; most people develop tolerance within 2 weeks. Stay hydrated, take injections before sleep if possible, and avoid alcohol during loading.

How Often Should Beginners Inject?

Every-other-day injection (e.g., Monday-Wednesday-Friday) is ideal. This allows partial clearance between doses, reducing nausea accumulation while maintaining steady-state concentration. Daily injections increase nausea risk. More-spaced intervals (every 3 days) work if nausea is severe, but extend the loading phase by 1-2 weeks.

What Are Beginner-Friendly Injection Sites?

Subcutaneous injection into lower abdominal fat (pinch skin and inject at 45-degree angle into fatty tissue) is easiest for beginners. Alternative sites: lateral thigh, upper arm (triceps area). Avoid intramuscular injection — use insulin syringes (0.5-1 mL) calibrated for the dose volume. Rotate sites every 3-5 injections to prevent lipohypertrophy.

Should Beginners Combine Melanotan II with Sun Exposure?

Yes, but with caution. Sun exposure dramatically enhances tanning (2-3 shade increases vs 1-2 without sun). However, limit sun exposure to 20-30 minutes per session, 2-3 times weekly, and use SPF 15-30 sunscreen afterward to prevent excessive photoaging while preserving tanning stimulus. Indoor tanning beds are an alternative without UV burn risk.

How Long Should a Beginner's First Cycle Be?

2-3 weeks of loading phase is standard for beginners. This achieves visible, stable tanning without excessive compound exposure. After reaching desired tan, switch to maintenance doses (0.25-0.35 mg 1-2 times weekly) to sustain the tan. Total cycle duration of 4-6 weeks (loading + early maintenance) is reasonable before taking a 4-8 week break.

What Mistakes Do Beginners Make?

Starting at too-high doses (>0.5 mg in loading phase) maximizes nausea without additional tanning benefit. Combining with alcohol increases side effects. Not staying hydrated worsens nausea. Injecting daily instead of every-other-day increases problems. Injecting intramuscularly instead of subcutaneously. Failing to baseline with dermatology photos before starting. Expecting permanent results without maintenance.

FAQ: Beginner Questions

How long does the test dose take to show effects?

Nausea, flushing, or involuntary erections (in men) typically appear within 30-60 minutes. Tanning response is not visible from a single test dose. Effects resolve within 4-12 hours.

Can I skip the test dose?

Technically yes, but not recommended. Test dose reveals individual sensitivity in a low-risk context. Some people are severe non-responders (develop nausea intolerable at any dose), and the test dose identifies this before committing to a full loading phase.

What if I have no nausea?

Congratulations — you're likely in the 40% of people who tolerate MT-II well. Still increase doses gradually over 3-4 days to loading phase dose to monitor for delayed sensitivity.

Can I inject at different times of day?

Yes. Some prefer injecting before sleep (nausea resolves during sleep). Others inject in the morning and manage nausea during waking hours. Consistency matters more than timing.
