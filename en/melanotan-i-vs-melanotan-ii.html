Melanotan I and II both activate melanocortin receptors but differ significantly. Melanotan I is selective for MC1R, producing gradual tanning (7-14 days) with mild side effects. Melanotan II activates MC1R + MC3R/MC4R, causing faster tanning (3-5 days) but intense hunger, nausea, and sexual effects. Only Melanotan I (Scenesse) is FDA-approved for EPP.

Mechanism Comparison: Receptor Selectivity

Melanotan I: selective MC1R activation with minimal activity at other melanocortin receptors. Results in melanin production without systemic side effects. Melanotan II: MC1R + MC3R/MC4R activity (appetite center). Broader receptor activity explains greater side effects. MC3R/MC4R activation in hypothalamus causes intense hunger; limbic region activation causes sexual overstimulation. This fundamental difference explains dramatically different side effect profiles despite similar tanning efficacy.

Tanning Speed and Timeline Comparison

Melanotan II: visible tanning 3-5 days, peak intensity 2-3 weeks. Melanotan I: visible tanning 7-14 days, peak intensity 4-6 weeks. MT-II tans 2-3x faster than MT-I due to broader receptor stimulation and higher circulating half-life. For users prioritizing rapid tanning, MT-II is superior. For users tolerating gradual tanning, MT-I avoids intense side effects.

Side Effect Profiles and Tolerability

Melanotan II: intense hunger (70-80% of users), severe nausea in 20-30%, sexual effects (often overwhelming) in 80%+. Severity: eating 3-5 additional meals daily common; sexual effects sometimes unmanageable. Melanotan I: mild nausea (20-30%), sexual effects (30-50%), hunger absent. Severity: most users report side effects as tolerable and manageable. This is the primary reason many users prefer MT-I despite slower tanning.

Regulatory Status and Safety Data

Melanotan I (Scenesse): FDA-approved 2014 for EPP. 10+ year clinical trial data, no malignancy signal, extensive safety documentation. Melanotan II: not FDA-approved, not approved worldwide. Lacks systematic long-term safety studies. Limited safety data comes from observational user reports. This regulatory distinction reflects Melanotan I's superior safety profile and more thorough evaluation.

How Does Individual Variation Affect Melanotan Outcomes?

Individual variation in response to Melanotan is one of the most important but least discussed aspects of peptide research. Genetic polymorphisms, baseline health status, age, sex, body composition, and concurrent medications all influence how an individual responds to any given protocol. This variability explains why two people following identical protocols can experience markedly different outcomes, and underscores the importance of personalized approach rather than one-size-fits-all recommendations.

Metabolic factors play a particularly significant role in determining individual response. Liver function, kidney clearance rates, plasma protein binding capacity, and enzyme activity levels all affect how quickly Melanotan is processed and eliminated from the body. Individuals with faster metabolism may require higher doses or more frequent administration to achieve the same tissue concentrations as those with slower metabolic rates.

Age-related physiological changes can also significantly impact Melanotan outcomes. Older individuals may experience enhanced benefits in some domains due to age-related decline in endogenous peptide production, while simultaneously facing increased sensitivity to certain side effects. Starting with conservative doses and titrating gradually is particularly important for older researchers who may have reduced clearance capacity and altered receptor sensitivity.
