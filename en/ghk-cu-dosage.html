Topical Dosage: Concentration Matters

Cosmetic GHK-Cu serums: typically 0.5-2%, most commonly 1% (optimal for balance of efficacy and cost). Higher concentrations (5-10%) don't produce proportionally better results and increase irritation/irritant contact dermatitis risk. Studies show 1% is the sweet spot for visible improvement without excessive irritation.

Application Quantity and Frequency

Typical application: 1-2 pumps (or 1-2mL) of GHK-Cu serum per application (face, neck, décolletage). Apply to clean, slightly damp skin for better absorption. Frequency: daily (morning or evening, not both) produces optimal results; 3-4x weekly is minimum effective frequency. No benefit to higher frequency (e.g., twice daily).

Layering with Other Products

Apply GHK-Cu serum to damp skin, then layer with: lightweight moisturizer, hydrating serums, or sunscreen (morning application). GHK-Cu can be layered under or over other serums/moisturizers—order doesn't critically matter. Avoid heavy occlusive creams immediately after GHK-Cu (they may trap moisture and reduce penetration), though light hydrating creams are fine.

Intradermal/Injection Dosage

Medical/clinical intradermal GHK-Cu: 1-5mg per injection site, 2-3x weekly for 8-12 weeks. Higher penetration than topical; produces faster/more dramatic results but requires medical supervision. Intradermal is not cosmetic use and should only be done under healthcare provider guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 1% GHK-Cu better than 0.5%?

Both are effective. 1% is research-proven; 0.5% requires longer use for visible results but is less irritating. Choice depends on skin sensitivity.

Can I use more GHK-Cu than recommended?

Using more doesn't improve results and increases irritation risk. Stick to standard recommendations. More isn't better with topical peptides.

How do I know what concentration my serum is?

Check product label—legitimate products list GHK-Cu concentration (usually 1%). If concentration isn't listed, it may be proprietary blend with unknown GHK-Cu content.

Can I mix GHK-Cu with other serums?

Yes, but not necessary. Layering serums is simpler than mixing. If mixing, use neutral pH (7) solutions to avoid peptide degradation.

What if I accidentally use too much GHK-Cu?

Excess would simply sit on skin and be wasted or cause temporary irritation. Rinse off with water if excessive irritation develops. No systemic toxicity from topical GHK-Cu.

Does more frequent application produce faster results?

No. Once daily is optimal; twice daily provides no additional benefit. Consistency over months matters more than frequency.
