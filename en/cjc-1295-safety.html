CJC-1295 Safety

Comprehensive Safety Analysis and Risk Mitigation

Medical Disclaimer

For informational and educational purposes only. Not FDA-approved for human use. Consult a licensed healthcare professional. See full disclaimer.

CJC-1295 safety: generally well-tolerated at therapeutic doses (100-200 mcg 2-3x weekly). Common side effects: transient headache (10%), mild water retention (15%), increased appetite (20%), injection site irritation (5%). Serious adverse effects rare. Contraindications: active cancer, uncontrolled diabetes, untreated sleep apnea. Long-term safety data absent; cycling recommended. Monitoring: baseline and annual blood work if using chronically.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the most common side effect?

Increased appetite (20-30% of users). Usually mild and manageable. Followed by transient headache (10%) and mild water retention (15%).

Is CJC-1295 safe for long-term use?

Long-term data absent (no human studies beyond 16-24 weeks). Cycling recommended: 12-16 weeks on, 4-8 weeks off, to preserve receptor sensitivity.

Will CJC-1295 cause diabetes?

Very unlikely. May slightly elevate blood glucose in diabetics (managed easily). Non-diabetics show minimal changes. Annual glucose screening prudent if using chronically.

Can CJC-1295 cause carpal tunnel?

Possible but rare. Water retention can aggravate pre-existing carpal tunnel. Users with pre-existing carpal tunnel should monitor closely.

Is CJC-1295 safe for those with cancer history?

Contraindicated. Any growth hormone-elevating agent theoretically risks promoting dormant cancer cells. Avoid if personal/family cancer history.

What monitoring should I do?

Baseline blood work (lipids, glucose, IGF-1) before first cycle. Annual monitoring if using chronically. More frequent if pre-existing metabolic disease.
