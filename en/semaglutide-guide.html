What Is the Recommended Semaglutide Dosage?

The most widely used Semaglutide dosage is 0.25-2.4 mg weekly (injection) or 3-14 mg daily (oral), administered once weekly (injection) or daily (oral) via subcutaneous injection or oral.

For Semaglutide specifically, the ongoing, titrated over 4-5 weeks cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — GLP-1 receptor agonist incretin mimetic compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

How Long Should a Semaglutide Cycle Last?

Standard Semaglutide cycles run ongoing, titrated over 4-5 weeks. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

STEP trials: 16-22.5% weight loss at 2.4 mg vs 3.1% placebo at 68 weeks. Multiple cardiovascular outcome trials. Approved for diabetes (2017), weight management (2021), and kidney disease (2025). 15,000+ clinical trial subjects.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (20-40% incidence, decreasing over 4-8 weeks). Thyroid C-cell tumor risk in rodents (human relevance unclear). Rare pancreatitis and diabetic retinopathy worsening.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most Semaglutide-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does Semaglutide Dosing Change When Stacking?

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.

What Is the Bottom Line on Semaglutide Dosing?

Start at 0.25, dose once weekly (injection) or daily (oral), and run cycles of ongoing, titrated over 4-5 weeks. Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.
