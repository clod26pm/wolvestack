FAQ

Q: What is the best time to inject?
A: Evening injection, 30-60 minutes before sleep, aligns with natural GH secretion patterns. Post-workout injection supports recovery-related signaling. Consistent timing optimizes results more than perfect timing.

Q: Can I mix compounds in one syringe?
A: Yes, combining reconstituted peptides in a single syringe is common. Ensure both solutions use the same reconstitution medium (bacteriostatic water). This practice simplifies dosing when using multiple peptides simultaneously.

Q: How do I know if my peptide is authentic?
A: Request COAs (Certificates of Analysis) from suppliers demonstrating third-party purity testing. Reputable vendors provide detailed documentation. Vague suppliers lacking transparency may indicate lower quality.

Q: What if I miss a dose?
A: For once-daily protocols, take the dose as soon as remembered unless close to next dose (then skip that dose). Maintain consistent timing going forward. Occasional missed doses minimally impact overall results.

Q: Can these cause cancer or serious health problems?
A: Available research suggests favorable safety profiles at therapeutic doses. Long-term human safety data remains limited given research chemical status. Theoretical concerns include potential increased cancer risk with excessive GH elevation, though evidence remains unclear.

Q: Do results persist after stopping?
A: Muscle and strength gains generally persist post-cycle. However, without continued training and nutrition, gradual deconditioning occurs. GH-related improvements in recovery and body composition regress more quickly after cessation than strength/muscle gains.
