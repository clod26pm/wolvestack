Sermorelin is administered via subcutaneous injection using 30-31 gauge needles at 45-degree angle into abdomen, thighs, or flanks. Reconstitute powder with bacteriostatic water, store refrigerated, draw appropriate dose, clean injection site with alcohol, pinch skin, inject slowly, withdraw and apply pressure. Rotate injection sites daily to prevent lipodystrophy. Total injection time: 2-3 minutes. Pain is minimal; most users report no discomfort after 2-3 injections.

Frequently Asked Questions

What needle size should I use?

30-31 gauge needles are standard. 25-27 gauge is too large (increased pain); 32+ gauge is too thin (slow injection). 30G/31G is the goldilocks zone.

Where on my body should I inject?

Abdomen (preferred), thighs (outer), or flanks are ideal. Rotate sites daily: abdomen day 1, left thigh day 2, right thigh day 3, flanks day 4-7, repeat.

Does the injection hurt?

Minimal to no pain. 30-31G needles are very fine. Most users report no discomfort after 2-3 injections as injection technique improves. Warm skin reduces discomfort.

How do I reconstitute sermorelin powder?

Add bacteriostatic water (typically 2 ml for 10 mg vials) to lyophilized powder. Roll (don't shake) vial until powder dissolves. Store at 2-8°C; use within 3-7 days.

What angle should I inject at?

45-degree angle is standard for subcutaneous injection. Pinch skin, insert needle slowly at 45 degrees, inject solution, wait 3-5 seconds, withdraw and apply light pressure.

How do I prevent lipodystrophy (lumps)?

Rotate injection sites daily. Never inject in the same spot twice per week. This prevents fat tissue breakdown, inflammation, and nodule formation.
