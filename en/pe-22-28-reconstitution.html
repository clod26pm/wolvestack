PE-22-28 reconstitution requires sterile bacteriostatic water, careful mixing to avoid foam, and refrigeration at 2-8°C. Reconstituted solution is stable 2-3 weeks refrigerated. Never freeze reconstituted peptide. Proper technique prevents degradation and maintains efficacy.

What Is PE-22-28 and Why Proper Reconstitution Matters

PE-22-28 is a thymic peptide delivered as lyophilized powder requiring reconstitution before injection. Proper reconstitution preserves peptide integrity, maintains efficacy, and prevents contamination. Poor technique (vigorous shaking, wrong water, improper storage) degrades the peptide significantly.

Materials Required for Safe Reconstitution

Sterile bacteriostatic water (benzyl alcohol 0.9% preservative), sterile syringes (3mL for reconstitution, insulin syringe for injections), sterile needles (22-25 gauge for reconstitution, 27-31 for injections), alcohol prep pads, vial of lyophilized PE-22-28. Never use regular distilled water or saline—only bacteriostatic water prevents bacterial growth.

Step-by-Step Reconstitution Procedure

Clean vial cap with alcohol pad. Draw appropriate volume of bacteriostatic water (typically 1-2mL depending on desired concentration). Slowly inject water into lyophilized powder at 45-degree angle—do NOT force. Allow powder to sit undisturbed for 2-3 minutes. Gently roll (do NOT shake) vial until powder fully dissolves. Solution should be clear. If foam appears, let sit 30 minutes before use.

Critical: Never Shake Reconstituted PE-22-28

Vigorous shaking creates foam bubbles, which denature peptide through mechanical disruption and oxidative stress. Instead, gently roll vial between palms or use slow circular motions. If you accidentally shake vigorously, let foam completely settle (overnight if necessary) before drawing solution. Minor shaking causes minimal damage; aggressive shaking significantly reduces efficacy.

Achieving Proper Concentration

Standard concentrations: 10 mg/mL (add 1mL water to 10mg vial), 20 mg/mL (add 0.5mL water to 10mg vial). Lower concentration (10 mg/mL) easier for beginners—larger injection volumes mean easier dosing accuracy. Higher concentration (20 mg/mL) requires smaller injection volumes, useful if frequent small injections preferred.

Storage After Reconstitution

Refrigerate reconstituted PE-22-28 at 2-8°C immediately after preparation. Stable 2-3 weeks in refrigerator. Never freeze reconstituted solution—freeze-thaw cycles form ice crystals that denature peptides. Keep vial upright, sealed, and protected from light. Check for cloudiness or discoloration before use—signs of contamination or degradation.

Pre-loading Syringes for Convenience

Once reconstituted, peptide can be pre-loaded into sterile insulin syringes for convenience. Draw accurately, cap needle securely, store upright in refrigerator. Pre-loaded syringes are stable 7 days if properly sealed. Label syringes with date and peptide amount. This approach is convenient but sacrifices stability compared to fresh reconstitution.

Signs of Degraded or Contaminated PE-22-28

Do NOT use if: solution is cloudy or discolored (bacterial contamination or degradation), visible particles present (contamination), vial shows signs of leakage (contamination risk), reconstituted >3 weeks ago (stability exceeded), solution has unusual odor or appearance. When in doubt, discard and start fresh—cost of new vial is minimal compared to risk of contaminated injection.

Troubleshooting Reconstitution Problems

Foam won't dissolve: let sit overnight undisturbed. Powder won't dissolve: ensure water is truly bacteriostatic (check label), try slightly warmer (room temperature) water, ensure needle isn't clogged. Solution is cloudy: likely bacterial contamination, discard immediately. Particles visible: contamination, discard vial.
