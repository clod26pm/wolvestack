GHRH Receptor Binding and Pituitary Signaling

CJC-1295 binds the GHRH receptor (a G-protein coupled receptor, or GPCR) on the surface of somatotrope cells. Receptor binding triggers a intracellular signal cascade: activation of GAs proteins, stimulation of adenyl cyclase, increase in cAMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate), and activation of protein kinase A (PKA). PKA phosphorylates calcium channels, increasing intracellular calcium, which triggers exocytosis - the release of pre-synthesized GH granules from the somatotrope into the bloodstream. Additionally, PKA phosphorylates transcription factors that upregulate GH gene expression, increasing GH synthesis for future release.
