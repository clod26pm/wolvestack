Frequently Asked Questions

Is sermorelin safe for women?

Yes. Sermorelin carries no virilization risk (it's not androgenic). Safety profile is similar to men's. No evidence of increased cancer or hormonal disruption risk.

Will sermorelin cause virilization?

No. Sermorelin stimulates GH, not androgens. Virilization risks are specific to androgens (testosterone, DHT). Sermorelin has zero virilization risk.

Where will I lose fat on sermorelin?

Fat loss is preferential from hips, thighs, and lower body in women (vs shoulders/chest in men). Visceral fat also decreases. Face becomes slightly leaner.

How much muscle will I gain?

2-4 lbs lean mass over 12 weeks is typical for women. Results are smaller than men's due to lower baseline testosterone amplifying GH effects less.

Can I use sermorelin while menstruating?

Yes. Sermorelin doesn't disrupt menstrual cycles or hormonal balance. Inject normally during all phases of the menstrual cycle.

Do I need to adjust dose for female physiology?

Dose is typically the same as men's (500-1000 mcg daily). Weight-based dosing (10-15 mcg/kg) is more precise and accounts for smaller body size.
