What Is GHRP-2 and Why Is Injection the Preferred Route?

GHRP-2 (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-2) is a synthetic hexapeptide GH secretagogue that stimulates growth hormone release by mimicking ghrelin at the GHS-R receptor. While GHRP-2 is available as an intranasal spray, subcutaneous injection provides superior bioavailability, more consistent blood levels, and greater GH response compared to nasal administration. The peptide has a half-life of approximately 30 minutes, with peak plasma GH elevation occurring 15-30 minutes post-injection. Subcutaneous delivery bypasses hepatic first-pass metabolism, ensuring 100% bioavailability of the administered dose. Intranasal administration, by contrast, shows variable mucosal absorption (40-70%), inconsistent GH peaks, and frequent systemic loss through postnasal drainage. For research purposes, subcutaneous injection is the gold standard delivery method supported by clinical literature.

What Is the Correct GHRP-2 Injection Dosage?

Standard GHRP-2 dosing for research use ranges from 100-300 mcg per injection, administered 2-3 times daily. Most users employ a consistent dose of 100-150 mcg for gentler effects or 200-300 mcg for maximum GH stimulation. Dosing depends on body weight (approximately 1-2 mcg/kg for modest response, 2-4 mcg/kg for aggressive stimulation), tolerance, and desired cortisol elevation acceptance. Injections are typically spaced 4-6 hours apart to allow GH response clearance between doses. Injections should be administered on an empty stomach, ideally 30-45 minutes before meals, as food ingestion blunts GH response by ~30%. A common protocol involves morning injection upon waking (fasted state), midday injection (fasted if possible), and evening injection pre-dinner. Total daily GHRP-2 intake ranges from 200-900 mcg depending on protocol intensity.

  • 100-150 mcg per injection for conservative users
  • 200-300 mcg per injection for standard protocols
  • Administer on empty stomach 30-45 minutes before meals
  • Space injections 4-6 hours apart

How to Prepare GHRP-2 for Injection

GHRP-2 arrives as lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder in vials and requires reconstitution before injection. Standard reconstitution uses bacteriostatic water at a ratio of 1 mcg/0.01 mL (100 mcg/mL final concentration). Aseptic technique is essential: sanitize the rubber stopper with alcohol swabs, allow to air dry completely, use sterile 29 gauge insulin syringes, and maintain a sterile work surface. Draw the calculated volume of bacteriostatic water into a sterile syringe (typically 1-2 mL), inject slowly into the vial at a slight angle to minimize bubble formation, and gently swirl (never shake vigorously, as this denatures the peptide). Reconstituted GHRP-2 should be clear and colorless. Never inject cloudy or discolored solutions. Properly reconstituted GHRP-2 is stable refrigerated at 2-8°C for 30+ days.

Injection Site Selection and Rotation

Optimal GHRP-2 injection sites include the abdominal wall (2-3 inches lateral of the navel), lateral thighs, and upper outer arms—areas with established subcutaneous fat and minimal neurovascular involvement. The abdomen provides the largest surface area for site rotation and is often preferred for consistency. Never inject into muscular areas, near large blood vessels, or over bony prominences. Rotate injection sites with each dose to prevent lipohypertrophy (permanent fat pad enlargement and fibrosis), lipodystrophy (fat loss), and injection site granulomas. A systematic rotation pattern (e.g., upper abdomen → lower abdomen → left thigh → right thigh → left arm → right arm) ensures adequate spacing between injections in the same area. Leave at least 1 inch between consecutive injection sites to prevent local tissue damage.

  • Abdominal wall: safe, consistent site
  • Lateral thighs: large surface area
  • Upper outer arms: accessible for self-injection
  • Rotate sites systematically

Proper Injection Technique: Depth and Angle

GHRP-2 is administered as a subcutaneous (SubQ) injection, deposited into the subcutaneous adipose tissue layer below the dermis but above muscle. Use a 29-31 gauge insulin syringe (0.3-0.5 mL capacity) with a needle length of 6-8 mm, which is optimal for subcutaneous penetration without reaching muscle. Pinch the skin gently to create a raised fold, insert the needle at a 45-90 degree angle, and depress the plunger slowly over 3-5 seconds. Arm position matters: injecting into the abdomen is easiest at 45-90 degrees; thigh injections are best at 45 degrees; arm injections may require assistance. After injection, withdraw the needle and apply gentle pressure with a sterile gauze pad for 10 seconds. Do not massage the injection site, as this can increase local inflammation and promote absorption variability.

  • Use 29-31 gauge insulin syringes
  • Inject at 45-90 degree angle
  • Inject slowly over 3-5 seconds
  • Apply light pressure; do not massage

Frequently Asked Questions

Can GHRP-2 be injected intravenously?

No. GHRP-2 is a peptide and would be destroyed by bloodstream proteases. Only subcutaneous or intranasal routes are viable.

What happens if I inject GHRP-2 intramuscularly by accident?

IM injection causes more pain but does not cause serious harm. GHRP-2 is still absorbed systemically.

Can I inject GHRP-2 through clothing without alcohol swabs?

No. Injecting through clothing or without sanitation increases infection risk significantly.

How do I know if my GHRP-2 solution is contaminated?

Contaminated solutions appear cloudy, discolored, or have visible particles. Do not inject.

Is it safe to inject GHRP-2 while traveling?

Yes, with precautions. Keep GHRP-2 refrigerated in an insulated cooler and check local regulations.

Can I reduce injection pain by using smaller needles?

Smaller gauges reduce trauma but may be too dull. The 29-31G standard balances sharpness with minimal tissue damage.
