Proper Administration for Safety and Efficacy

CJC-1295 injection: 29-31 gauge needle, subcutaneous (under skin, above muscle), abdomen or thigh preferred. Standard dose: 100-200 mcg per injection. Technique: pinch skin, insert needle at 45-degree angle 0.5 inches, inject slowly, withdraw needle. Pain minimal. Inject 2-3x weekly at 24-48 hour intervals. Rotate sites to minimize irritation. Cost per injection: $3-8.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the needle painful?

Minimal to none. 29-31 gauge needles are very thin. Thinner than diabetic insulin needles. Most users feel 0-2/10 discomfort.

Where exactly should I inject?

Lower abdomen (pinch fat pad) or outer thigh. Avoid areas with visible blood vessels. Rotate between 4-8 sites to prevent lipodystrophy.

Can I do intramuscular instead of subcutaneous?

Not recommended. CJC-1295 designed for subcutaneous. IM injection offers no advantage and carries higher irritation risk.

How deep should the needle go?

0.5-0.75 inches. Just under the skin layer. If you hit muscle (deep injection), you've gone too far. Pinch skin to create safe distance.

What if I accidentally inject intramuscularly?

No danger; the peptide still works. May cause more local irritation or bruising. Likelihood of hitting muscle is low with proper technique.

Can I share needles between people?

Absolutely not. High infection/disease transmission risk. Single needle = one injection site = personal use only. Never share.
