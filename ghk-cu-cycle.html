What Is GHK-Cu and Why Does Dosing Matter?

GHK-Cu (GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)) is a Tripeptide-copper complex. Naturally occurring peptide found in human plasma, saliva, and urine; levels decline significantly with age from 200 ng/mL at age 20 to 80 ng/mL by age 60. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

The standard research dosage for GHK-Cu has been established through preclinical studies and community protocols. This guide covers the evidence-backed dosing range, timing, and cycle structure.

What Is the Recommended GHK-Cu Dosage?

The most widely used GHK-Cu dosage is 1-3 mg (injectable), topical formulations vary, administered once daily via subcutaneous injection or topical application.

Beginners should start at the lower end of this range and titrate upward only if needed. The half-life of GHK-Cu is approximately 2-4 hours, which directly determines how often you need to dose to maintain stable blood levels.

For GHK-Cu specifically, the 4-12 weeks cycle length is standard. Longer isn't always better — Tripeptide-copper complex compounds require cycling to maintain receptor sensitivity.

When Should You Dose GHK-Cu?

With a half-life of approximately 2-4 hours, timing your GHK-Cu administration affects peak blood levels. Most protocols call for once daily.

For peptides administered before bed (common with GH-related compounds), the goal is to amplify the natural nocturnal growth hormone pulse. For healing peptides, proximity to the injury site via local injection can improve outcomes.

How Long Should a GHK-Cu Cycle Last?

Standard GHK-Cu cycles run 4-12 weeks. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

Over 1,000 published studies document wound healing, skin remodeling, and anti-aging properties. The peptide modulates 4,000+ human genes and has strong evidence for collagen/elastin synthesis, anti-inflammatory activity, and hair growth promotion.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Well-tolerated in research and cosmetic use. Mild injection site reactions possible. Topical use may cause temporary skin redness in sensitive individuals. No systemic toxicity reported.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most GHK-Cu-related side effects are dose-dependent.

How Does GHK-Cu Dosing Change When Stacking?

Pairs naturally with TB-500 for wound healing (GHK-Cu for skin/collagen, TB-500 for systemic inflammation) and with retinoids and vitamin C in topical skincare protocols.

When stacking, some researchers reduce individual peptide doses by 20-30% since synergistic effects mean full doses of each aren't always necessary.

What Is the Bottom Line on GHK-Cu Dosing?

Start at 1, dose once daily, and run cycles of 4-12 weeks. Monitor your response and adjust within the established range.
