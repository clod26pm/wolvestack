Best Peptides for Anti-Aging & Longevity

Top anti-aging peptides ranked: Epithalon for telomerase activation and cellular renewal, GHK-Cu for collagen induction and skin health, Humanin for mitochondrial protection, MOTS-c for metabolic flexibility, SS-31 for cellular energy optimization, and NAD+ precursors for sirtuin activation. Multi-peptide anti-aging stacks addressing distinct aging mechanisms typically outperform monotherapy.

Epithalon: Telomerase Activation & Cellular Renewal

Most comprehensive anti-aging peptide. Activates telomerase enzyme, restores telomere length (preventing cellular aging), regulates circadian rhythm, and promotes cellular regeneration through multiple pathways.

  • Dosing: 10 mg daily SC (cycled: 10 days on / 10 days off)
  • Research: Multiple human trials demonstrating telomere lengthening and improved cellular health markers
  • Timeline: 3-6 months for measurable telomere restoration

GHK-Cu: Collagen Remodeling & Skin Health

Copper-peptide complex that upregulates collagen I and III synthesis, enhances wound healing, improves skin elasticity, and supports connective tissue integrity. Visible skin improvement within 4-8 weeks of consistent use.

  • Dosing: 250 mcg daily intranasal or subcutaneous
  • Best for: Skin aging, wrinkles, connective tissue health, post-procedure recovery
  • Synergy: Pairs exceptionally well with Epithalon and topical retinoids

Humanin: Mitochondrial Protection & Lifespan

Mitochondrial-derived peptide that increases ATP production, activates survival pathways (AMPK, Sirt1, AKT), and provides protection against age-related diseases and cellular senescence.

  • Dosing: 250 mcg daily intranasal
  • Research: Extends lifespan in animal models; slows Alzheimer's progression in human trials; neuroprotective
  • Best for: Cognitive aging prevention, disease resistance, metabolic health

MOTS-c: Metabolic Flexibility & Glucose Control

Mitochondrial open reading frame peptide that improves mitochondrial function, enhances glucose utilization, reduces age-related metabolic decline, and increases mitochondrial biogenesis.

  • Dosing: 250 mcg daily intranasal
  • Best for: Metabolic health, glucose control, insulin sensitivity, preventing metabolic dysfunction with aging

SS-31 (Elamipretide): Mitochondrial Energetics

Penetrates inner mitochondrial membrane, stabilizes cardiolipin, restores electron transport chain efficiency, and reduces oxidative stress at the cellular level—addressing fundamental aging mechanisms.

  • Dosing: 5-10 mg daily intranasal
  • Research: Slows cardiac and neurologic aging in preclinical models; clinical trials in Barth syndrome and mitochondrial disease

NAD+ Precursor Peptides: NMN & NR

Restore cellular NAD+ levels (dramatically depleted in aging), activate sirtuins and PARP enzymes, enhance cellular repair mechanisms, and improve metabolic health through NAD-dependent pathways.

  • Dosing: 500-1000 mg daily oral
  • Research: Improved muscle function, longevity markers, cardiovascular health, and cognitive function in human trials

Combining and Stacking Anti-Aging Peptides for Synergistic Results

While individual peptides show anti-aging benefits, combinations targeting multiple aging pathways often outperform single peptide approaches. A comprehensive anti-aging stack might combine telomerase activation (Epithalon), collagen support (GHK-Cu), mitochondrial protection (Humanin/MOTS-c), and NAD+ restoration, simultaneously addressing cellular senescence, structural aging, metabolic decline, and energy production.

Cycling protocols are important: 12-16 weeks of active use followed by 4-8 weeks off optimizes receptor sensitivity and prevents tolerance development. Some advanced longevity protocols use rotating multi-peptide cycles (Epithalon weeks 1-12, then switch to different mitochondrial peptides weeks 13-24) to continuously stimulate different anti-aging pathways without needing breaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which anti-aging peptide should I start with?
Epithalon for telomerase activation and broad-spectrum cellular anti-aging; GHK-Cu if skin is primary concern. Both have strongest research backing.
How long before I see anti-aging results?
Skin improvements (GHK-Cu): 4-8 weeks. Cellular aging markers (Epithalon): 3-6 months. Long-term lifespan benefits: 1-2+ years with consistent use.
Can I take all anti-aging peptides together?
Yes. They address different aging pathways (telomeres, collagen, mitochondria, metabolism). Combination longevity stacks can be synergistic. Medical guidance recommended.
Do these peptides actually extend human lifespan?
Strong animal model evidence exists. Limited human lifespan data currently available. Expected benefits include cellular aging slowdown, disease prevention, and improved healthspan.
Are anti-aging peptides safe for long-term use?
Yes, generally well-tolerated with proper cycling and medical monitoring. Some peptides benefit from periodic breaks to maintain receptor sensitivity.
What age should I start anti-aging peptides?
Preventive use can begin at 30-40 years old. Therapeutic use typically starts at 45+. Earlier intervention may optimize long-term longevity outcomes.
