Long-Term Powder Storage Stability?

Lyophilized (freeze-dried) Epithalon powder remains stable for several years in proper conditions. Studies suggest 3-5 year stability at 2-8°C, though some degradation occurs over time. The exact shelf life depends on manufacturing quality, purity level, and storage precision. High-quality peptides from reputable vendors maintain potency across years if sealed properly.

Vacuum-sealed, inert-gas-flushed vials provide better long-term storage than simple sealed containers. Premium vendors use nitrogen-flushed vials to displace oxygen, preventing oxidative degradation. If a vial has been opened previously (even briefly), potency may decline faster—treat opened vials as having shorter shelf life (6-12 months vs. 3-5 years).

When purchasing bulk supplies for multi-year use, verify that vials are sealed with inert gas, have recent manufacturing dates, and come from vendors with temperature-controlled storage. Ask for batch testing data showing stability. Some vendors store poorly, allowing vials to experience temperature fluctuations that degrade peptides months before expiration.

Temperature Fluctuations and Degradation Acceleration?

Temperature cycling (repeated warming and cooling) accelerates peptide degradation. A vial moved between freezer and room temperature daily degrades faster than one maintained at constant 2-8°C. Each thermal cycle introduces expansion stress and promotes chemical breakdown. Keep vials in the main refrigerator body at steady temperature—never the door (fluctuating).

Freezing (below 0°C) actually risks peptide degradation from ice crystal formation. The consensus recommendation is refrigeration (2-8°C), not freezing. If you freeze vials to extend shelf life, thawing may reduce potency. Freezing is a last resort, not a preferred storage method. Steady refrigeration outperforms freeze-thaw cycling every time.

Summer heat exposure during shipping or storage is a major degradation risk. Some vendors ship without temperature control, exposing peptides to 80-90°F during transport. Request refrigerated shipping if available (slight premium cost). Upon receipt, immediately place vials in refrigeration. Any extended room-temperature storage (beyond a few hours) accelerates degradation.
