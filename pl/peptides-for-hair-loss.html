Frequently Asked Questions

GHK-Cu (copper peptide) has the strongest research support for hair regrowth among peptides. Studies show it can enlarge hair follicles at rates comparable to 5% minoxidil. It works through multiple mechanisms including Wnt pathway activation, anti-inflammatory effects, angiogenesis, and stem cell stimulation. For best results, combine topical GHK-Cu application with microneedling. TB-500 is the second most popular option, working through stem cell migration and follicle cycling activation.

Generally, no. Peptides work best on areas where miniaturized follicles still exist — meaning areas that are thinning but still have some hair or visible vellus (fine) hairs. Areas that have been completely bald for years likely have follicles that are replaced by scar tissue and cannot be revived by peptides alone. The earlier you intervene in the hair loss process, the better your chances of meaningful improvement with any treatment, including peptides.

Expect a minimum of 3-6 months before seeing noticeable results. Hair follicle biology operates on slow timescales — the growth cycle alone takes months. Early signs may include reduced shedding (month 1-2) and the appearance of new vellus hairs (month 3-4). Meaningful density improvements typically emerge around months 5-6, with maximum results by 9-12 months. Patience and consistency are essential. Some users experience an initial shedding phase in weeks 2-4, which is a normal sign that dormant follicles are being pushed into new growth cycles.

Yes, many users combine GHK-Cu with conventional hair loss treatments. Since GHK-Cu works through different mechanisms than minoxidil (which primarily acts as a vasodilator) and finasteride (which blocks DHT), the combination could theoretically provide additive benefits. Apply minoxidil and GHK-Cu at different times (e.g., minoxidil in the morning, GHK-Cu at night) to avoid diluting either product. There are no known harmful interactions between these compounds, though formal interaction studies haven't been conducted.

Not strictly necessary, but strongly recommended. Microneedling alone has been shown to promote hair growth by activating the wound healing cascade and releasing growth factors. When combined with GHK-Cu, microneedling dramatically increases peptide absorption into the scalp and delivers it closer to the dermal papilla. A 2013 study showed that microneedling plus minoxidil significantly outperformed minoxidil alone — the same principle likely applies to GHK-Cu. Use a 0.5-1.5mm needle depth, once weekly, with GHK-Cu applied immediately after.

Yes, peptides like GHK-Cu and TB-500 work through gender-neutral mechanisms (follicle stimulation, stem cell activation, anti-inflammation) and may be particularly advantageous for women. Unlike finasteride, which is contraindicated in women of childbearing age due to birth defect risks, topical GHK-Cu has no known hormonal effects. Women experiencing diffuse thinning or female pattern hair loss may respond well to peptide protocols. However, it's important to rule out underlying causes like thyroid dysfunction, iron deficiency, or hormonal imbalances before starting any hair loss treatment.
