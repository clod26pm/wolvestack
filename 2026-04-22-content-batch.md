## 🔍 Trend Report

**Top 3 timely findings from today's research sweep (FDA, PubMed, Reddit, news):**

1. **TODAY IS THE DAY — FDA Category 2 removal becomes effective April 22, 2026.** On April 15, the FDA published its 503A Categories Update officially removing 12 bulk drug substances from Category 2, effective seven days later — which is today. The list: **BPC-157, TB-500, LL-37, DiHexa, DSIP, Epitalon, GHK-Cu (injectable), KPV, PEG-MGF, Melanotan II, MOTs-C, and Semax.** Translation: the FDA no longer classifies these as posing "significant safety concerns." BUT — and this matters — removal from Category 2 is NOT the same as being added to the 503A bulks list. Each peptide still needs a Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) review. BPC-157 and TB-500 are both on the July 23, 2026 PCAC agenda. Until a peptide is formally added to the 503A list, compounding pharmacies still can't legally produce it for patient prescriptions. This is a huge search-traffic day — competitors are racing to update headlines. Our edge is nuance: explain the Cat-2-removal vs. 503A-addition distinction that most affiliate sites are glossing over.

2. **New preclinical systematic reviews published in 2026 give us fresh PubMed citations for the Wolverine Stack content pillar.** PMC12313605 ("Emerging Use of BPC-157 in Orthopaedic Sports Medicine") analyzed 544 articles (1993–2024) and distilled to 36 studies — 35 preclinical, 1 clinical. PMC12446177 ("Regeneration or Risk? A Narrative Review") pairs the benefit narrative with a safety counterweight. Key extract worth reusing in multiple articles: in gastrocnemius crush injury models, *combined* BPC-157 + TB-500 produced earlier restoration of contractile function than either peptide alone — the single cleanest preclinical justification for the stack, and most competitor articles aren't citing it.

3. **GLP-1 compounding is quietly being choked off while peptide attention is on the reclassification headline.** FDA's April 1 clarification re-emphasized the "essentially a copy" standard — vitamin B12 + semaglutide formulations are now explicitly in the agency's crosshairs. 503A pharmacies still have a limited enforcement safe harbor (max 4 prescriptions per calendar month per preparation). This reshapes the GLP-1 affiliate / content conversation. Patients who got used to $200/month compounded semaglutide are about to lose access; expect an email-capture spike from "what do I do now" queries.

**Secondary signal:** The STAT News piece (April 6) "Why RFK Jr. backs peptides but questions vaccines" is driving political-medicine Twitter and is the most-shared peptide news item of the month. It's making peptides a culture-war object, which is great for reach but messy for trust-building content. Stay apolitical in our materials; cite STAT as a neutral source when context is needed.
