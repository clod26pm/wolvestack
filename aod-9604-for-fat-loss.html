What Is AOD-9604 and Why Does Dosing Matter?

AOD-9604 (Fragment 177-191 of Human Growth Hormone (modified)) is a Lipolytic peptide, GH fragment analog. Synthetic analog of human GH fragment 177-191, engineered to selectively activate lipolysis without systemic GH effects. Getting the dosage right is critical — too little may produce no measurable effect, while excessive amounts increase risk without proportional benefit.

How Long Should a AOD-9604 Cycle Last?

Standard AOD-9604 cycles run 12-24 weeks. This timeframe is based on the compound's mechanism and the time needed to observe measurable effects.

12-week RCT showed 2.6 kg weight loss vs 0.8 kg placebo. However, a larger 24-week Phase III trial (536 subjects) failed to demonstrate significant weight loss, leading to development discontinuation in 2007. Six controlled trials established tolerability across 900+ subjects.

What Side Effects Can Affect Dosing?

Well-tolerated across 900+ participants in clinical trials. Minimal adverse effects — potential mild headache, dizziness, or injection site reactions. One of the safest peptides in terms of documented human tolerability.

If side effects emerge, the first step is typically reducing the dose rather than discontinuing entirely. Most AOD-9604-related side effects are dose-dependent.
