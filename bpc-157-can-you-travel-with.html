What Is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a Pentadecapeptide (15 amino acids). Derived from a protective protein found in human gastric juice. Also known as BPC-157, Bepecin, PL 14736, PL-10, it has been studied for its potential effects on tissue repair, gut healing, tendon and ligament recovery, wound healing, neuroprotection.

One of the few peptides with over 100 published preclinical studies, and the only peptide derived from human gastric juice with demonstrated tissue-protective effects across virtually every organ system studied. In the research community, BPC-157 has gained attention for its distinctive profile.

How Does BPC-157 Work?

BPC-157 upregulates growth hormone receptors and promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation) through the FAK-paxillin pathway, which is critical for cell migration and tissue repair. It modulates the nitric oxide system and influences the dopaminergic, serotonergic, and GABAergic neurotransmitter systems. Research also shows it accelerates the formation of reticulin and collagen fibers during wound healing.

This multi-pathway activity helps explain why BPC-157 shows potential across several different applications.

What Does the Research Say About BPC-157?

Extensive preclinical research across 100+ published studies demonstrates tissue-protective effects across the GI tract, musculoskeletal system, nervous system, and cardiovascular system. No human clinical trials completed to date, though several are planned.

Researchers should consult the latest peer-reviewed literature for the most current findings.

What Is the Recommended BPC-157 Dosage?

Standard BPC-157 dosing: 200-500 mcg, administered once or twice daily via subcutaneous or intramuscular injection, oral.

Half-life: approximately 4 hours (stable form). Cycle length: 4-12 weeks.
