# Peptide Content Batch — March 23, 2026

## Trend Report

### Trend 1: FDA Reclassification Still Pending — Community Growing Impatient

On February 27, HHS Secretary RFK Jr. announced on the Joe Rogan Experience that ~14 of the 19 restricted peptides would move from Category 2 back to Category 1, restoring legal compounding access. Nearly a month later, the FDA has still not published the formal updated list. Reddit communities and wellness forums are buzzing with frustration over the delay, speculation about which 5 peptides will remain restricted, and questions about what "legal compounding" will actually look like for pricing and access. This is a massive content angle — the information gap is enormous and people are hungry for clear explainers.

### Trend 2: Peptide Sciences Shutdown Sends Shockwaves Through Grey Market

Peptide Sciences, the largest grey-market research peptide vendor in the U.S., voluntarily shut down on March 6, 2026. The biohacker community is scrambling for alternatives. This is fueling two conversations simultaneously: (1) where to source peptides now, and (2) whether the shift to compounding pharmacies is actually better for consumers. Reddit threads are full of vendor comparisons, purity concerns, and debates about whether compounded peptides will be affordable. Meanwhile, payment processors (Mastercard BRAM enforcement) are making it harder for remaining grey-market vendors to operate.

### Trend 3: TikTok Kitchen-Counter Peptide Mixing Goes Viral — Pharmacists Sound Alarm

A disturbing trend is gaining traction on TikTok: influencers casually demonstrating how to reconstitute powdered peptides into injectable solutions on kitchen counters — no gloves, no sterile technique, just alcohol swabs and bacteriostatic water. Pharmacists and medical professionals are pushing back hard, warning about contamination risks and incorrect dosing. NPR and CNN both ran stories in recent weeks about the disconnect between influencer promotion and actual science. This creates a perfect "myth vs. reality" content angle.
