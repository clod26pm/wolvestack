Hexarelin Cycle Guide

Length, Dosing & PCT Protocol

Standard hexarelin cycle: 8-12 weeks on, 4-8 weeks off. Typical protocol: weeks 1-4 at 50-100 mcg once daily, weeks 5-12 escalate to 100-200 mcg twice daily. Some advanced users pulse-cycle (2 weeks on, 1 week off) to slow desensitization. Cycling essential to maintain receptor sensitivity and prevent tolerance. Without breaks, effectiveness plateaus by week 4-5.

Standard 12-Week Cycle Structure

Weeks 1-4: introduction phase. 50-100 mcg hexarelin once daily (preferably morning) to assess tolerance. Most users tolerate well at low dose; appetite stimulation manageable. GH elevation adequate for early adaptation without overwhelming side effects. By week 4, user can accurately assess personal responsiveness and tolerance for dose escalation.

Weeks 5-8: escalation phase. Increase to 100-150 mcg twice daily (morning/afternoon) based on week 1-4 tolerance. This phase produces maximal anabolic response; muscle gain and fat loss accelerate. GH elevation peaks; IGF-1 reaches 15-20% above baseline. Appetite and cortisol elevation peak during this window; users must manage carefully.

Weeks 9-12: maintenance phase. Continue 100-150 mcg twice daily (some users add third injection 50-100 mcg evening). Anabolic rate slows slightly as receptor desensitization begins despite cycling. Final weeks consolidate gains. Training recovery remains excellent; strength continues improving though at slower rate than week 5-8 peak.

Off-Cycle Period (4-8 Weeks)

Complete cessation of hexarelin. Some users experience brief GH suppression (rebound effect) weeks 1-2 post-cycle, lasting 1-2 weeks before baseline normalizes. Off-cycle allows complete GHS-R1a receptor resensitization and hormonal normalization. Gained muscle is retained with maintained training/nutrition. Off-cycle duration influences next cycle responsiveness: 4 weeks partial resensitization, 8 weeks complete resensitization. Standard recommendation: minimum 4 weeks off, 6-8 weeks preferred.

Pulse-Cycling Alternative

Advanced users sometimes implement pulse-cycling: 2 weeks hexarelin use, 1 week complete break, repeating 3-4 cycles within 8-12 week total duration. Theory: weekly breaks slow receptor desensitization, extending cycle effectiveness. Experience suggests modest slowing of plateau but doesn't prevent desensitization entirely. Pulse-cycling adds complexity; standard continuous cycling remains most researched.

Dose Progression During Cycle

Week 1-2: 50 mcg once daily. Week 3-4: 100 mcg once daily. Week 5-8: 100-150 mcg twice daily. Week 9-12: 150-200 mcg twice daily OR 100-150 mcg twice daily + 50-100 mcg evening. Total daily dose range: week 1-4 (50-100 mcg), weeks 5-12 (200-400 mcg). Dose escalation allows tolerance assessment while minimizing early side effects.

Cycling Rationale

Continuous hexarelin use beyond 12 weeks produces GHS-R1a receptor downregulation and reduced GH responsiveness. Peak GH response may decline 20-40% by week 10-12 despite maintained dosing. Off-cycle periods allow complete receptor resensitization and restoration of baseline GH responsiveness. Cycling prevents the escalating tolerance that makes year-round continuous use progressively ineffective, requiring increasingly higher doses to maintain response.

Multi-Cycle Strategy

Users typically implement 2-4 consecutive cycles yearly (12 weeks on, 6-8 weeks off rotation). This allows cumulative gains across multiple cycles while maintaining GH responsiveness through proper cycling. A user completing 3 cycles yearly achieves 30-40 weeks hexarelin exposure (maintaining good responsiveness throughout) vs continuous use that loses effectiveness by month 3-4. Multi-cycle strategy optimizes long-term results.

FAQ: Hexarelin Cycling

What happens if I cycle too frequently (less than 4 weeks off)?

Incomplete receptor resensitization. Subsequent cycle responsiveness is reduced vs properly rested cycles. First dose of next cycle produces less dramatic GH elevation. Standard 4-8 week off periods prevent this.

Can I do multiple back-to-back cycles without breaks?

Not recommended. Continuous use beyond 12 weeks produces tolerance plateau. Off-cycle breaks essential for receptor resensitization and sustained effectiveness.

Is 8 weeks off-cycle necessary or will 4 weeks work?

4 weeks provides partial resensitization. 8 weeks provides complete resensitization. 6-8 weeks is optimal balance between off-time and back-to-cycle readiness.

Can I use hexarelin year-round if I pulse-cycle?

Pulse-cycling (1-2 days off per week) slows desensitization but doesn't prevent it. Year-round continuous even with pulse-cycling is not recommended; multi-cycle approach (12 weeks on, 6-8 weeks off) is safer long-term strategy.
