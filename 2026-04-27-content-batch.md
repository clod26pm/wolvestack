**Top 3 timely findings from today's research sweep (FDA, PubMed, Reddit, news):**

1. **The PCAC public docket (FDA-2025-N-6895) is open RIGHT NOW — and almost no affiliate site is telling readers how to participate.** Five days ago (April 22), the FDA's Category 2 removal of 12 peptides became effective. The next milestone is the July 23–24, 2026 Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting where BPC-157, TB-500, KPV, MOTs-C, DSIP/Emideltide, Semax, and Epitalon will be reviewed for inclusion on the 503A bulks list. Two deadlines matter: **July 9, 2026** (comments by this date are formally provided to PCAC members in their pre-read packet) and **July 22, 2026 11:59 pm ET** (final docket close). This is unusually high-leverage content: a dated, actionable, civically-framed CTA that 99% of peptide content is ignoring. Massive trust-building opportunity, plus sets up returning traffic in early July when the deadline approaches.

2. **The "intended use" framing buried in the PCAC notice is news that nobody's covering.** The Federal Register posting (2026-04-16, Vol. 91 Issue 73) reveals that BPC-157 is being nominated specifically for **ulcerative colitis** and TB-500 specifically for **wound healing** — not for the indications most of our audience uses them for (tendon repair, performance recovery). This matters because the PCAC will evaluate the safety/efficacy package against *those* indications, not against gym-bro use cases. Even if both peptides are added to the 503A list, the prescription pathway will be tied to the nominated indications, which means physicians prescribing for orthopaedic recovery will still be doing so off-label. This is a nuance opportunity — an "actually, here's what's going to happen in your doctor's office" angle that builds authority.

3. **The GLP-1-and-muscle-loss conversation is finally peer-reviewed enough to weaponize for content.** The SEMALEAN study (PMID 41068996, *Diabetes, Obesity and Metabolism*, 2026) and a 24-month retrospective cohort (PMC12235021) both landed this quarter. Headline finding from SEMALEAN: lean mass dropped –3 kg in the first 7 months on semaglutide before stabilizing, while sarcopenic obesity prevalence fell from 49% → 33% over 12 months. Translation: there's a real but bounded muscle-loss signal, and it's worst in older / pre-frail patients. This is a perfect bridge topic between our GLP-1 audience and our core peptide audience — protein, resistance training, and adjunct peptides become the conversation starter.

**Secondary signal:** A peer-reviewed *qualitative study of Reddit user perspectives on peptide therapy after orthopaedic surgery* dropped in *Sports Health* in 2026 (Rosenberg et al., DOI 10.1177/19417381251411343). It analyzes the exact community we're trying to reach. This is reusable across nearly every long-form article we'll write this year — Reddit chatter is now an academically citable signal.
