Reconstitute FOXO4-DRI by mixing lyophilized powder with bacteriostatic water (1mg peptide + 1mL water for 1mg/mL concentration). Inject water slowly into the vial against the wall, never directly onto powder. Gently swirl (never shake) until fully dissolved in 5-10 minutes. Store reconstituted solution at 2-8°C and use within 2-4 weeks. Proper reconstitution protects peptide integrity and efficacy.

Understanding Lyophilization and Why FOXO4-DRI Arrives as Powder

FOXO4-DRI is synthesized as a liquid peptide, then freeze-dried (lyophilized) to create a stable, shelf-stable powder. Lyophilization removes water while maintaining the peptide's three-dimensional structure. The powder form is far more stable than liquid—aqueous solutions degrade over weeks, while lyophilized peptides remain viable for months or years at 2-8°C. This is why research peptides are shipped as powders. Upon arrival, you must reconstitute by rehydrating the powder with sterile water, which dissolves the peptide and allows subcutaneous injection.

Choosing the Right Reconstitution Vehicle

Bacteriostatic water (sterile water with 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative) is the standard reconstitution vehicle for FOXO4-DRI. The benzyl alcohol serves as a preservative, extending the stability of reconstituted solutions to 2-4 weeks. Alternative: bacteriostatic saline (0.9% NaCl with benzyl alcohol) is equally acceptable and some users prefer it for subjective comfort reasons. Never use: regular tap water (contains bacteria and minerals), distilled water (no preservatives), or non-sterile water of any kind. Sterility is essential—contaminated reconstitution water introduces bacteria into your reconstituted peptide, creating infection risk.

Step-by-Step Reconstitution Process

1. Gather Materials Sterile 1-3mL syringe, sterile 25-27 gauge needle, sterile bacteriostatic water, FOXO4-DRI vial, alcohol prep pad, sterile pad or cotton ball. Work on a clean surface.

2. Calculate Volume Determine desired final concentration. Standard: 1mg/mL (easy math: 1mg peptide + 1mL water). If the vial contains 10mg, add 10mL water for 1mg/mL. Write the intended concentration on a piece of paper for reference.

3. Sterilize the Vial Rubber Stopper Use alcohol prep pad to thoroughly clean the rubber stopper on top of the FOXO4-DRI vial. Allow to air-dry completely (at least 30 seconds). This removes surface bacteria and ensures sterile reconstitution.

4. Draw Water into Syringe Using sterile syringe and needle, draw the calculated volume of bacteriostatic water from the water vial. The water should be at room temperature. If water is cold, hold the syringe briefly in your hand to warm it—cold solution is uncomfortable for injection later.

5. Inject Water into FOXO4-DRI Vial This is the critical step. Insert the needle through the rubber stopper of the FOXO4-DRI vial at a shallow angle. Inject the water slowly against the vial wall (not directly onto the powder pile). The water should run down the vial wall, dissolving powder gradually. Injecting directly onto powder can cause clumping and denatures some peptide molecules. Inject slowly and steadily—rapid injection creates pressure and can damage the peptide.

6. Gently Swirl the Vial After injecting all water, withdraw the needle. Gently swirl the vial between your palms using a circular motion (do not shake up-and-down, which creates foam and denatures peptide). Swirl for 30-60 seconds to fully mix. Allow the vial to rest for 5-10 minutes to permit complete dissolution. The vial should feel warm from the swirling. After 5-10 minutes, swirl again briefly to ensure complete dissolution. The final solution appears clear and colorless—any cloudiness suggests incomplete dissolution or contamination.

Calculating Concentration and Dosing

Determine your desired concentration before reconstituting. Most common: 1mg/mL (1mg peptide + 1mL water). Advantages: simple math, standard insulin syringes (1mL) permit accurate dosing, each 0.1mL = 0.1mg. Example: for 5mg dose, draw 0.5mL. For 10mg dose, draw 1.0mL. Alternative concentrations are possible (10mg/mL if you want smaller injection volumes, or 0.5mg/mL if you want larger volumes for precision), but 1mg/mL is the practical standard.

Temperature Considerations During Reconstitution

Room temperature (20-25°C) is ideal for reconstitution. Cold bacteriostatic water (especially if refrigerated) takes longer to dissolve the peptide and is uncomfortable for injection later—warm the water syringe in your hand before injecting. Hot water (>40°C) can denature the peptide, so never use warm water from a heated source. After reconstitution, the vial may feel slightly warm from swirling friction—this is normal. Cool it naturally at room temperature before storing in the refrigerator.

Storage After Reconstitution

Immediately after reconstitution, store the vial in a refrigerator at 2-8°C (standard refrigerator temperature). Mark the vial with the reconstitution date and concentration using a permanent marker. Reconstituted FOXO4-DRI is stable 2-4 weeks when stored properly. The benzyl alcohol preservative in bacteriostatic water extends shelf life. After 4 weeks, discard any remaining solution—don't use expired peptide. If you only need small amounts, consider reconstituting smaller volumes (0.5mL or 1mL total) and using them within 1-2 weeks for maximum confidence in stability.

Troubleshooting Reconstitution Problems

Problem: Powder won't fully dissolve Wait longer (up to 15-20 minutes). Gently swirl every few minutes. If still cloudy after 20 minutes, the water may be contaminated or you may have used the wrong water—discard and reconstitute fresh. Problem: Cloudiness or crystals in solution—indicates degradation or contamination. Discard and reconstitute with fresh materials. Problem: Vial feels abnormally cold after reconstitution—the water was likely too cold. This is okay, but warm the vial slightly in your hand before injection later. Problem: Difficulty drawing water from water vial—verify the needle isn't clogged. Use a fresh needle if you can't draw.

Common Reconstitution Mistakes

Mistake: Shaking the vial vigorously after adding water (denatures peptide)—use only gentle swirling. Mistake: Injecting water directly onto the powder pile (causes clumping)—always inject against the vial wall. Mistake: Using non-sterile or contaminated water (introduces bacteria)—always use verified sterile, bacteriostatic water. Mistake: Using hot water (denatures peptide)—use room-temperature water only. Mistake: Not marking the vial with reconstitution date (can't track freshness)—always date the vial immediately. Mistake: Storing reconstituted peptide at room temperature (degrades in days)—always refrigerate at 2-8°C.

Can You Reconstitute Multiple Times from the Same Vial?

Yes, you can draw from a reconstituted vial multiple times if you use aseptic technique. Each time you insert a needle through the rubber stopper, use a fresh sterile needle (the first puncture dulls it). Minimize air exposure—draw what you need, withdraw quickly, recap if needed. The vial remains stable at 2-8°C between uses. However, every needle puncture creates a potential contamination point. If you're concerned about contamination or using the same vial repeatedly, consider reconstituting in smaller batches (0.5-1mL) for single or double use, then discarding.

Mixing FOXO4-DRI with Other Peptides During Reconstitution

If you plan to stack FOXO4-DRI with other peptides (e.g., BPC-157, TB-500), don't mix them in the same vial during reconstitution. Each peptide has different stability characteristics and mixing can reduce efficacy of both. Instead, reconstitute each peptide separately in individual vials, then draw from each vial before injecting. This maintains the integrity of both compounds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why can't I shake the vial?

Vigorous shaking creates foam and physical stress on the peptide molecule, causing denaturation. Gentle swirling mixes without damaging the peptide structure.

What if I accidentally used non-sterile water?

Discard the vial immediately. Non-sterile water introduces bacteria into the peptide, creating serious infection risk if injected. Always use verified sterile bacteriostatic water.

Can I heat the vial to speed dissolution?

No. Heat denatures peptides. Room temperature swirling for 5-10 minutes is the correct approach. Never use heat.

How do I know if reconstitution was successful?

The solution should be clear and colorless. Any cloudiness, crystals, or discoloration indicates a problem—discard and reconstitute fresh.

Can I refrigerate the water before reconstituting?

Technically yes, but cold water takes longer to dissolve the peptide and is uncomfortable for injection later. Warm the water syringe in your hand to room temperature before injecting into the vial.

How long can I store reconstituted FOXO4-DRI?

2-4 weeks at 2-8°C when reconstituted with bacteriostatic water. Mark the reconstitution date and discard after 4 weeks. Some users prefer smaller volumes used within 1-2 weeks for maximum confidence.
