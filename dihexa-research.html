What Does the Research Say About Dihexa?

Rat studies show reversal of Alzheimer's-type cognitive dysfunction, outperforming the prescription drug donepezil (Aricept). Increased synaptic density and spine numbers in cognitively impaired models. No human clinical trials — preclinical only.

Dihexa (N-hexanoic-Tyr-Ile-(6) aminohexanoic amide) is a Synaptogenic neuropeptide, HGF/c-Met pathway agonist. Research interest has focused on its potential effects on enhanced synaptogenesis, improved cognitive function, memory enhancement, neuroprotection, increased dendritic spine density.

What Is the Evidence for Dihexa's Mechanism?

Potentiates hepatocyte growth factor (HGF) and its receptor c-Met, driving synaptogenesis (new synapse formation). Preclinical data shows it is millions of times more potent than BDNF at stimulating synapse growth. Also reduces neuroinflammation via NF-κB pathway suppression and protects neurons against oxidative damage.

These pathways have been identified through in vitro studies, animal models, and where available, human trials.

Are There Human Clinical Trials for Dihexa?

What Does the Safety Research Show?

Significant concern about potential cancer risk through HGF/c-Met pathway activation, which is involved in tumor progression. Could theoretically promote maladaptive neural wiring or tumorigenesis. No human clinical safety data available.

What Makes Dihexa Unique in Research?

Millions of times more potent than BDNF at growing new synapses — the most powerful synaptogenic compound identified. The tradeoff is a legitimate concern about cancer risk through the HGF/c-Met pathway.

This differentiator is important because it means Dihexa fills a role that other compounds in its class may not fully replicate.
