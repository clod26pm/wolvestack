What Is TB-500?

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4 Fragment (TB-500)) is a 43-amino acid peptide. Naturally occurring peptide present in virtually all human and animal cells. Also known as TB-500, Thymosin Beta 4, Tβ4, it has been studied extensively for its potential effects on wound healing, tissue repair, inflammation reduction, hair regrowth, cardiac repair, flexibility improvement.

In the research community, TB-500 has gained significant attention due to its favorable safety profile and broad range of potential applications. It is one of the most widely studied peptides in its class.

How Does TB-500 Work?

TB-500 promotes cell migration by upregulating actin, a cell-building protein. It also reduces inflammation, promotes angiogenesis, and supports stem cell differentiation. Its primary mechanism involves sequestration of actin monomers and regulation of cytoskeletal dynamics.

Understanding these mechanisms helps researchers design more effective protocols and predict potential outcomes. The multi-pathway activity of TB-500 is one reason it has attracted so much research interest.

What Does the Research Say About TB-500?

Research shows TB-500 accelerates wound healing, promotes cardiac repair after injury, reduces inflammatory cytokines, and supports dermal healing. Used extensively in equine medicine.

It is important to note that while preclinical results are encouraging, the peptide research field is still evolving. Researchers should consult the latest peer-reviewed literature for the most current findings on TB-500.

What Is the Recommended TB-500 Dosage?

Based on research protocols, common TB-500 dosages range from 2-5 mg (loading), 2 mg (maintenance), administered 2x weekly (loading phase), weekly (maintenance) via subcutaneous or intramuscular injection.

The half-life of TB-500 is approximately 2-3 hours, which influences dosing frequency. Typical research cycles last 4-6 weeks loading, then ongoing maintenance.
