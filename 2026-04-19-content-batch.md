# Peptide Content Batch — April 19, 2026

## Trend Report

### Trend 1: FDA Publishes Day-by-Day Schedule for July PCAC Meeting — Seven Peptides, Two Days

On April 17, just two days after the initial announcement, the FDA posted the detailed agenda for its Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee meeting on July 23–24, 2026. Day 1 (July 23) will review BPC-157, KPV, TB-500, and MOTS-C. Day 2 (July 24) will review Emideltile (DSIP), Semax, and Epitalon. Industry observers immediately noted that the peptides were clustered by mechanism — Day 1 is the "injury recovery and metabolic" group, Day 2 is the "neuro/longevity" group. STAT News reported on April 15 that advisers are "expected to support RFK Jr.'s legalization push," citing pre-meeting position papers. This is the most concrete regulatory development since the February 27 RFK Jr. announcement — the schedule being locked in means vendors, clinicians, and consumers now have a hard deadline to plan around.

### Trend 2: GHK-Cu Is the Breakout Peptide of 2026 — 1,000%+ YoY Search Growth

Multiple industry trackers are now flagging GHK-Cu (copper peptide) as the single fastest-growing peptide by search volume in 2026, with year-over-year growth estimates above 1,000%. The surge is driven by three converging forces: (1) TikTok skin-cycling content that frames copper peptides as a collagen/elastin booster, (2) the broader "skin longevity" narrative popularized by Peter Attia and David Sinclair, and (3) the fact that GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring tripeptide with decades of human topical-use research — giving it a stronger evidence base than most peptides on the market. Dermatology Times and Skin Inc. have both run cover stories on next-generation delivery systems (liposomal, microneedle). GHK-Cu is also on the "probably safe" side of the FDA reclassification debate and is not on the July PCAC list, which may explain why clinics have been quietly building GHK-Cu protocols ahead of the BPC-157 outcome.

### Trend 3: NAD+ Research Paradigm Shifts — From "Topping Off" to "Stopping the Leak"

A new wave of 2026 longevity coverage (including in Hone Health's 2026 trends report and nad.com editorial coverage) documents a meaningful pivot in NAD+ research. For the last five years, the field was dominated by precursor supplementation — NR, NMN, and IV NAD+ infusions — all aimed at raising levels. The new focus is on the enzymes that deplete NAD+ in the first place: CD38, PARPs, and NAMPT. Rather than continuously refilling the tank, researchers are asking why the leak is there. Early studies suggest CD38 inhibition may be more efficient than precursor loading. This is a narrative gift for content — it reframes the entire NAD+ conversation and lets creators revisit the topic with a fresh angle, even among audiences that already know the basics. Subreddit discussion on r/longevity has picked this up in the last 10 days.
