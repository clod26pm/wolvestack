### 1. Peptide Sciences Shutdown Sends Shockwaves Through the Community

On March 6, 2026, Peptide Sciences — the largest grey-market research peptide vendor in the U.S. — voluntarily shut down with no warning. The company was generating an estimated $7.4 million/month in sales as of December 2025. The closure is widely attributed to escalating FDA enforcement (50+ warning letters in 2025, DOJ involvement, the Amino Asylum raid in June 2025) and the emerging shift toward regulated compounding pharmacy access. Reddit and forum communities are scrambling for alternatives while Finnrick Analytics has warned that any site operating under the Peptide Sciences name post-shutdown is fraudulent. **Content angle:** This is a massive trust moment — our audience needs guidance on sourcing quality peptides through legitimate channels.

### 2. First Human IV BPC-157 Safety Data Published

A 2025 IRB-approved pilot study (Lee & Burgess) tested intravenous BPC-157 at doses of 10mg and 20mg in healthy adults. No adverse effects were reported across biomarkers for heart, liver, kidneys, thyroid, or blood glucose. While only 2 participants were enrolled (no control group), this represents a milestone: the first published human IV safety data for BPC-157. Combined with the 2025 HSS Journal systematic review cataloging 544 preclinical studies on BPC-157 in orthopaedic medicine, research momentum is clearly accelerating. **Content angle:** "The first human safety data on BPC-157 is here — here's what researchers found."

### 3. Semaglutide May Slow Biological Aging — First RCT Evidence

A medRxiv preprint reported that once-weekly semaglutide slowed epigenetic aging across multiple DNA methylation clocks in a randomized clinical trial of adults with HIV-associated lipohypertrophy. PCGrimAge showed 3.08 fewer "aging years per year" in the semaglutide group (p=0.007), and DunedinPACE slowed by approximately 9%. This is the first RCT evidence that a GLP-1 receptor agonist can modulate epigenetic biomarkers of aging — a finding with massive implications for the longevity community. **Content angle:** "The weight loss drug that might actually slow aging — what the new research says."
